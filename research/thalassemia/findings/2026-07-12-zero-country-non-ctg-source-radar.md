# Finding: Zero-Country Non-ClinicalTrials.gov Source Radar

Date checked: 2026-07-12
Status: source-route radar, not treatment advice

## Direct Answer

The July 11 zero-country ClinicalTrials.gov labels should not be treated as
evidence of absent access. The July 12 source radar adds non-ClinicalTrials.gov
routes for all five zero-result labels: Indonesia, Singapore, Hong Kong, Iran,
and Russia.

Current label:

`zero_country_non_ctg_source_radar_ready_benchmark_only`

Case-001 state remains:

`branch_review_handoff_packet_incomplete`

## Most Important New Signal

A 2026 case report in *Thalassemia Reports* describes the first Indonesian
pediatric thalassemia patient to undergo gene therapy and reports six-month
transfusion independence after autologous CD34+ hematopoietic stem and
progenitor cells modified with a lentiviral beta-globin vector.

This is a valuable owner-verification source, not a public action route. It
does not prove local availability, affordability, repeatability, Case-001
eligibility, referral, travel, import, purchase, or treatment selection.

The next useful question for a qualified owner is:

> What access path, treatment center, manufacturing route, payer route,
> regulatory route, and long-term monitoring infrastructure made that reported
> case possible, and what parts are reproducible or not reproducible for other
> Indonesian patients?

## Practical Meaning

Public outreach can now ask for:

- official national registry IDs;
- regulator or hospital program pages;
- PubMed IDs or DOIs;
- qualified owner names and roles;
- source country and current status.

Public outreach should still reject replies that jump directly to diagnosis,
eligibility, trial screening, referral, travel, import, purchase, dosing,
treatment selection, private-record exchange, lab contact, or sample routing.

## Islamic Motivation Boundary

Quran 49:6 supports verification before acting on consequential reports. Quran
4:58 supports routing entrusted questions to their proper owner. Quran 16:43
supports asking qualified people when knowledge is missing. These are process
ethics only and are not biomedical evidence for registry status, access,
eligibility, referral, treatment, import, purchase, or clinical action.
