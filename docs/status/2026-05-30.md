## Current Findings

The source refresh sharpens the discipline:

- exa-cel has favorable sponsor-affiliated cost-effectiveness modeling in the
  US literature;
- public HTA review still highlights uncertainty, cost sensitivity, and
  price-reduction dependency;
- China editing and non-editing registry benchmarks remain close enough that
  weak novelty claims should be blocked.

The promotion gate now requires every candidate to carry four fields before it
can move upward: benchmark gap, closest benchmark, measurable endpoint, and
falsification test.

Case-001 relevance remains blocked until qualified specialist review. The
current public state is still research triage, not therapy selection.

## Current Blockers

- Candidate lanes without a named gap, endpoint set, safety stop, and
  falsification test stay below the promotion line.
- Approved or late gene-cell therapies remain benchmarks only; they do not
  prove Indonesia access, center availability, affordability, or case
  suitability.
- Sirolimus, `PRKAB1`/autophagy, `T-BDMC`, and resveratrol remain assay-only
  until identity, HbF, chain-balance, hemolysis, viability, and safety
  endpoints are present.
- HLA, donor, owner-reply, product-access, travel, import, dosing, and exact
  private-date details must stay out of public files.

## Strongest Research Lanes

1. Affordable-cure candidate promotion: apply the May 30 gate before any lane
   moves from interesting signal to gap-review candidate.
2. Curative affordability gaps: keep cost, infrastructure, conditioning,
   monitoring, access, safety, fertility, and assay feasibility as separate
   axes.
3. Advanced-therapy route triage: keep HSCT, autologous gene-cell therapy,
   non-curative drug, stabilization, and registry branches separate.
4. Owner packet readiness: keep diagnosis, transfusion, immune, iron, organ,
   fertility, donor, access, and payer domains upstream of specialist review.
5. Clinical comparator discipline: use hydroxyurea, luspatercept, mitapivat,
   AND017, and gene-cell therapies as benchmark context, not case instructions.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Diagnosis and genotype labels, including clinician-confirmed disease
  category and molecular-record status.
- Transfusion-burden labels, including interval, pre-transfusion hemoglobin,
  units or volume, body weight, Hb response, and annualized burden.
- Immune and blood-bank labels, including matching policy, antibody screen,
  alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs context, reaction history, phenotype or
  genotype context, and crossmatch issues.
- Iron, organ-risk, and chelation labels, including ferritin trend, liver iron,
  cardiac `T2*`, chelator identity, toxicity monitoring, and organ-risk notes.
- Infection, fertility, transplant-readiness, follow-up, HLA/donor, center,
  payer, geography, and affordability labels only as public-safe summaries
  after private owner review.

## Next Evidence Tasks
