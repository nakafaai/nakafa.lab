## Current Findings

The May 18 source refresh did not change the evidence boundary. Adult
ENERGIZE-T remains benchmark evidence with posted results, while pediatric TDT
and pediatric NTDT mitapivat records remain no-result watchlist records.

The practical blocker is now answer handling: if a hematologist answers, the
public repo should store only the outcome label, missing-domain labels, owner
category, public source URL, and next state. Raw replies and patient-specific
instructions stay private.

FDA AQVESME label context remains adult-label and safety-context evidence. It
does not answer case-001 relevance, pediatric relevance, local access,
affordability, liver safety, interaction safety, or trial eligibility.

TIF 2025 remains the baseline clinical-context source for keeping diagnosis,
transfusion burden, iron/organ status, and monitoring records together before
any specialist review.

## Current Blockers

- The minimum hematologist packet is still incomplete unless diagnosis/genotype,
  age and weight band, transfusion burden, iron/organ context, liver tests,
  medication list, and local access constraints are available as de-identified
  labels.
- The pediatric mitapivat lane remains blocked from efficacy claims because
  `NCT07506863` and `NCT07517133` are not yet recruiting and have no posted
  results.
- Adult mitapivat evidence remains a benchmark only. It does not prove
  individual fit, pediatric fit, local availability, affordability, or safety.
- A clinician answer cannot be copied into the public repo. It must be reduced
  to one allowed label, one public action, and one private action.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Diagnosis and genotype context: current subtype wording, `HBB`,
  `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH, delta-beta-thalassemia, `HBG`, and modifier context when
  available.
- Age and weight band, because adult-label, adult-trial, pediatric-trial, and
  patient-specific care contexts remain separate.
- Transfusion burden: dates, pre/post hemoglobin, interval pattern, unit
  volume, red-cell fraction when available, body weight, reaction history, and
  annual `ml/kg/year` if the clinic can calculate it.
- Immune and blood-bank context: ABO/Rh/Kell, extended phenotype or genotype,
  antibody screen, alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs, crossmatch difficulty,
  matching policy, and reaction context.
- Iron, organ, and safety context: ferritin trend, liver iron concentration,
  cardiac `T2*`, chelation identity and monitoring, endocrine and organ
  screening, liver tests, medication list, and local access constraints.
