# Daily Research Journal: 2026-07-23

Audience: founder / researcher
Status: matched-sibling HSCT remains the leading affordable-curative route
benchmark only

## Question And Decision

**Question:** Does new EBMT conditioning guidance close the implementation and
delivery gaps for the leading matched-sibling HSCT benchmark?

**Decision:** `hold_msd_hsct_at_leading_affordable_curative_route_benchmark_only`.

The guidance identifies the correct independent protocol authority, but it does
not add outcome evidence or prove Indonesia delivery or affordability.

## Evidence And Scope

- **Fact:** The EBMT Practice Harmonisation and Guidelines Committee published
  TDT conditioning recommendations after a structured literature review and an
  international expert workshop. Recommendations used GRADE and received panel
  and external review.
- **Fact:** The guideline covers pre-transplant optimization, conditioning by
  age and donor type, iron overload, ineffective erythropoiesis, alternative
  donor transplantation, and gene therapy for children and adults with TDT. It
  explicitly aims to include resource-limited settings.
- **Evidence tier:** This is expert practice guidance, not a new efficacy,
  safety, affordability, or implementation cohort.
- **Scope boundary:** The outcome claim remains limited to the existing
  176-child cohort: under 14, fully matched sibling, low/intermediate transplant
  risk, TDT, and short median follow-up. The broader guideline population does
  not broaden that cohort's outcome evidence.

## Contradiction And Interpretation

- **Resolved contradiction:** The LMIC cohort conflicts on whether its busulfan
  quantity is daily or total. PubMed and Crossref still showed no correction on
  July 23. The cohort can support an outcome and cost-range benchmark, but its
  regimen must not be operationalized.
- **Interpretation:** EBMT guidance is now the named independent source for
  protocol extraction. The public publisher preview did not expose the exact
  recommendations, so no regimen was reconstructed from references or inferred.
- **Interpretation:** A stated resource-limited aim is not proof of Indonesia
  transplant capacity, authorization, payer coverage, delivered cost, or local
  outcomes.

## Mechanism, Safety, Affordability, And Access

- **Mechanism:** Allogeneic matched-sibling HCT replaces ineffective recipient
  hematopoiesis with donor hematopoiesis after conditioning.
- **Safety:** Conditioning toxicity, graft failure, transplant mortality,
  veno-occlusive disease or sinusoidal obstruction syndrome, infection, GVHD,
  fertility or endocrine harm, and ICU burden remain material.
- **Affordability and access:** The LMIC cohort's USD 8,000-10,000 mean cost
  range remains non-itemized. It does not price a guideline-concordant Indonesia
  route or establish local access.
- **Conflict:** The publisher reports multiple author relationships with gene-
  therapy and pharmaceutical companies. This does not invalidate the guidance,
  but it remains part of the source record.

## Falsification And Next Decisive Action

- **Falsification criterion:** Remove the leading-route label if independent
  three-to-five-year outcomes show unacceptable mortality, graft failure, late
  toxicity, infection, or loss of thalassemia-free survival, or if a
  guideline-concordant Indonesia delivery model exceeds the program threshold.
- **Next decisive action:** A transplant-program owner should obtain the full
  EBMT recommendation text and map its age, donor, conditioning, monitoring,
  supportive-care, and iron-management requirements to Indonesia capacity. A
  health-economics owner should then price that mapped route and its
  uncertainties before any promotion beyond `benchmark_only`.

  EBMT guideline was the only new cure-program implementation source; the other

- **Fact:** Exact ClinicalTrials.gov beta-thalassemia queries found no study,
