## Current Findings

- The Indonesian case report is a cross-border proof-of-execution signal and a
  lead for qualified owner verification, not a local access route.
- The mechanism and scope are narrow: autologous lentiviral beta-globin gene
  addition in one 15-year-old transfusion-dependent patient with Hb Malay /
  IVS1-nt5 compound-heterozygous `HBB` variants.
- Six-month transfusion independence is not a durable-cure endpoint. The report
  also provides no authorization ID, registry ID, delivered cost, or payer path.
- The report's no-significant-complications wording conflicts with its own
  month-six leukocyte and platelet values, so hematologic safety is unresolved.
- FDA, TIF, HSA, BPOM-context, and PubMed access-hurdle sources all reinforce
  that gene-cell therapy is infrastructure-heavy and owner-dependent.
- Two current registry signals expand the curative watchlist without changing
  the decision: FT007 is not yet recruiting, and the NIH matched-donor HCT
  record is a conditioning comparator rather than a Case-001 route.
- The canonical roadmap now prioritizes clinician/genotype readiness, verified
  curative access, and one real multi-endpoint affordable-candidate experiment.
