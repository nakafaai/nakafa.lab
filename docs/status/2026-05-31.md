## TL;DR

Nakafa Lab still has no new cure claim, no patient-specific treatment
recommendation, and no proof of Indonesia access to HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR
therapy, mitapivat, luspatercept, or any trial.

The useful May 31 change is a minimum assay readiness gate. It converts the
May 30 promotion rule into the smallest lab-readable package required before a
candidate can move toward quote or partner discussion.

`case001_minimum_assay_readiness_gate_ready`

## What Changed Since 2026-05-30

| Area | What changed | Founder takeaway |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Assay readiness | Added required fields for identity, category, gap, model, controls, endpoints, stop rules, raw data, and privacy. | Interesting is not enough; the test package must be executable. |
| Current evidence | Checked HbF reporter, HbF screening, hemolysis, and 2026 model papers. | The literature supports better assays, not a direct cure claim. |
| Privacy | Kept patient samples and identifiable records blocked without ethics and consent. | Public research can prepare assays without exposing the case. |

## Current Finding

A candidate can pass the May 30 promotion gate and still fail assay readiness.
The lab-facing minimum is now explicit: identity, model, controls, endpoint set,
stop rules, raw-data plan, and privacy boundary.

## Next Evidence Tasks

1. Apply the May 31 gate before lab quote or partner outreach.
2. Keep each candidate in `assay_ready_for_quote`, `hold`, `reject`,
   `benchmark_only`, or `ethics_or_privacy_blocked`.
3. Do not use case-001 samples, records, or clinical facts in public assay
   artifacts without ethics, consent, biosafety, and clinician oversight.
