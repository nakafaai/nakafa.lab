## Current Findings

The strongest current conclusion is a negative gate: any candidate that only
claims HbF induction, gamma-globin reactivation, HPFH mimicry, HBG targeting, or
possible transfusion reduction is novelty-blocked by existing editing
benchmarks unless it answers a different practical gap.

A candidate remains useful only if it answers a different gap:

- affordability or lower infrastructure;
- non-editing or materially simpler validation;
- HbF or `HBG` readout;
- alpha-globin burden or autophagy readout;
- erythroid maturation and viability readouts;
- mature red-cell hemolysis or membrane-safety stop;
- clinician-reviewed relevance before patient-specific claims.

The June 9 source trail supports benchmark and novelty framing only. It does
not support a cure claim, clinical access claim, patient-specific action, trial
screening path, import path, purchase path, or lab contact permission.

## Decision Rule

Reject or downgrade a candidate claim if it only says it raises HbF,
reactivates gamma-globin, copies HPFH, targets HBG, or could reduce
transfusions without a concrete validation path.

Keep a candidate researchable only if it adds affordability or lower
infrastructure, has a non-editing or simpler validation path, and can pass the
multi-endpoint assay gates before any clinical claim is made.
