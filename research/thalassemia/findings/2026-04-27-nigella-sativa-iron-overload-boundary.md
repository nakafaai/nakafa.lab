# Finding: Nigella Sativa Iron-Overload Boundary

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: prophetic-medicine adjunct signal, not treatment advice

## Working Conclusion

`Nigella sativa` is no longer only a generic prophetic-medicine idea in this
repo. A first-pass search found a 2024 randomized clinical trial in pediatric
beta-thalassemia major where `Nigella sativa` plus manuka honey was studied as
an adjunct to conventional therapy for iron-overload management.

This is useful, but it is not a cure claim. The retrieved signal is mainly
iron-overload and oxidative-stress adjunct biology, not direct correction of
the beta-globin defect. First-pass PubMed searches did not find a clean
`Nigella sativa` or thymoquinone fetal-hemoglobin/gamma-globin evidence base.

## Evidence Snapshot

| Source | Signal | Interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| PubMed PMID `39044986` | randomized pediatric beta-thalassemia major trial; abstract reports lower serum ferritin and iron, and lower liver enzymes, in the `Nigella sativa` plus manuka honey arm versus control | adjunct iron-overload signal; not HbF cure evidence |
| ClinicalTrials.gov `NCT04292314` | completed phase 2/3 study with outcomes including ferritin, serum iron, transferrin saturation, inflammatory markers, hemoglobin, reticulocytes, and HbF | broad biomarker trial; results not posted in registry snapshot |
| ClinicalTrials.gov `NCT02816957` | early-phase study of `Nigella sativa` in children with beta-thalassemia major, status unknown | registry signal, incomplete public follow-through |
| PubMed `Nigella sativa HbF` query | zero direct records | no clean HbF lane retrieved |
| PubMed `thymoquinone HbF` query | one noisy conference-abstract collection | not thymoquinone HbF evidence |
| PubChem | thymoquinone `CID 10281`, formula `C10H12O2` | chemistry-resolved constituent, not equivalent to whole seed oil |

## Boundary

Do not describe black seed, black seed oil, thymoquinone, or manuka honey as a
thalassemia cure.

The evidence can support a research question:

- Can a standardized `Nigella sativa` material improve iron-overload or
  oxidative-stress biomarkers when added to standard care?
- Does any effect persist after controlling for transfusion burden, chelation
  adherence, baseline ferritin, liver iron, inflammation, and nutrition?
- Is there any reproducible HbF or transfusion-burden effect, or is the lane
  only supportive?

## Research Decision

Track `Nigella sativa` as a medium-priority adjunct biology lane, below active
HbF and disease-modifying candidates.

Before promotion, the lane needs:

- full-text extraction of the 2024 trial methods and statistics;
- separation of `Nigella sativa`, manuka honey, hydroxyurea, chelation, and
  transfusion effects;
- adverse-event review and product-standardization details;
- validation against iron MRI, ferritin trend, liver enzymes, hemolysis,
  transfusion interval, and HbF where available;
- clinician review before any patient-facing interpretation.

## Islamic Interpretation Guardrail

The black seed hadith is treated as motivation for disciplined research. It is
not treated as a replacement for disease-specific evidence.
