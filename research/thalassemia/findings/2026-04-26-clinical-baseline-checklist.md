# Finding: Clinical Baseline Checklist

Date checked: 2026-04-26
Evidence label: Guideline synthesis

## Why This Exists

Before Nakafa Lab proposes any therapy hypothesis, we need a clean baseline that
a hematologist can audit. For a de-identified patient reported to receive weekly
transfusions, this checklist helps separate missing data from true treatment
gaps.

## Minimum Case Data

- Confirmed thalassemia diagnosis and subtype.
- Genotype if available: beta-thalassemia, HbE/beta-thalassemia,
  alpha-thalassemia, or mixed disease.
- Pre-transfusion hemoglobin trend.
- Transfusion interval, unit count, and approximate packed RBC volume.
- Blood type plus Rh and Kell matching status.
- Antibody screen, alloantibody history, and transfusion reaction history.
- Ferritin trend, liver iron, and cardiac T2* MRI if available.
- Current iron chelation drug, dose, adherence, and side effects.
- Liver, heart, endocrine, bone, and spleen monitoring.
- Exact autoimmune diagnosis and current treatment.

## Baseline Questions For Clinician Review

- Is this clearly transfusion-dependent thalassemia, and which subtype?
- Is weekly transfusion expected for this phenotype, or is there a modifiable
  reason for high transfusion need?
- Are pre-transfusion hemoglobin targets being met?
- Is iron overload controlled by ferritin plus liver/cardiac MRI, not ferritin
  alone?
- Are immune complications affecting transfusion survival or compatibility?
- Would luspatercept, mitapivat, HSCT, Zynteglo, Casgevy, or a trial be
  medically relevant for this patient?

## Safety Boundary

This checklist is not a request to change therapy. It is a structured way to
prepare evidence and questions for licensed clinicians.
