## Current Findings

The practical failure mode after owner routing is over-sharing. A first message
should not attach raw records or ask for a treatment decision. It should ask
one owner-matched question, cite public sources, and define what response would
justify escalation.

The first-contact packet keeps six owner lanes separate:

- hematology: clinical relevance review;
- pharmacy: dispensing, formulary, certification, and special-access
  operations;
- regulatory affairs: product registration, legal import, named-patient,
  compassionate-use, or special-access pathways;
- manufacturer medical information: official regional route and product-status
  routing;
- trial owner: public registry ID contact and screening process;
- payer or financial-access owner: coverage, reimbursement, or support process
  after clinician and legal-pathway review.

## Current Blockers

- Case-001 relevance remains blocked until a qualified clinician reviews the
  diagnosis, age and weight band, transfusion burden, iron and organ status,
  liver tests, medication list, and access constraints.
- First-contact outreach remains blocked if the message contains private
  records, multiple owners, treatment advice, eligibility claims, import
  instructions, or legal conclusions.
- Public notes must not include raw records, screenshots, identifiers, exact
  dates, hospital names, clinician names, doctor messages, or patient-specific
  treatment instructions.
