## Current Findings

The May 16 registry refresh found six mitapivat query records across completed,
active-not-recruiting, and not-yet-recruiting states. The key case-relevant
boundary is not whether mitapivat is interesting. It is whether the evidence
state permits action.

Adult label and adult TDT results can be cited as benchmarks. Pediatric TDT and
pediatric NTDT records are still no-result watchlist records. None of these
states establishes local access or case eligibility.

## Current Blockers

- Case-001 relevance remains blocked until a qualified clinician reviews the
  diagnosis, age and weight band, transfusion burden, iron and organ status,
  liver tests, medication list, and access constraints.
- Pediatric mitapivat remains blocked from efficacy claims because the current
  pediatric TDT and NTDT records are not yet recruiting and have no posted
  results.
- Public notes must not include raw replies, raw records, screenshots,
  identifiers, exact dates, hospital names, clinician names, owner messages,
  adverse-event narratives, or patient-specific treatment instructions.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Molecular diagnosis: `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH or delta-beta context, and
  hemoglobin-analysis timing relative to transfusion.
- Age and weight band, because adult-label, adult-trial, pediatric-trial, and
  patient-specific care contexts remain separate.
- Transfusion burden: dates, pre/post hemoglobin, interval pattern, unit
  volume, red-cell fraction when available, body weight, and reaction history.
- Immune and blood-bank records: ABO/Rh, extended phenotype or genotype,
  Rh/Kell matching, antibody screen, alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs, and
  prior reaction context.
- Iron, organ, and safety context: ferritin trend, liver iron concentration,
  cardiac `T2*`, endocrine monitoring, renal/liver labs, liver tests,
  medication list, and local access or referral constraints.
