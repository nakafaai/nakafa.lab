# Daily Research Journal: 2026-07-18

Audience: founder / researcher
Status: conditioning route promoted to preclinical benchmark only, not medical advice

## Question And Decision

**Question:** Does `CD117` epitope shielding resolve the full-myeloablation
barrier exposed by pediatric exa-cel well enough to promote gene-cell therapy
as an affordable-cure candidate?

**Decision:** `promote_cd117_shielding_to_preclinical_conditioning_benchmark_only`.
Keep exa-cel and the broader gene-cell lane `benchmark_only` for affordable-cure
promotion.

## Evidence Integration

- **Fact:** Pediatric exa-cel remains a strong efficacy benchmark, not an
  affordable-route proof. The primary report's 8 of 8 children with at least
  16 months of follow-up and FDA's later 8 of 9 efficacy-evaluable children are
  different analysis sets or cutoffs and remain separate. FDA still states
  that full myeloablative conditioning precedes treatment.
- **Fact:** A new non-peer-reviewed primary preprint tested the conditioning
  bottleneck. `CIM058` blocks wild-type `CD117`; healthy-donor `CD34+` HSPCs
  prime-edited to carry `CD117 E73K` resist that antibody and can be enriched.
  In NBSGW mice humanized with beta-thalassemia HSPCs of the homozygous
  `IVS1-110/IVS1-110` genotype, antibody exposure before and after transplant
  improved shielded-cell enrichment, globin-chain balance, and erythroid
  maturation, reduced reticulocytosis and splenic iron, and reduced spleen
  size. Methods report two same-genotype donor sources.
- **Interpretation:** The preprint turns removal of full myeloablation into a
  testable route. It does not establish patient benefit, transfusion
  independence, total hemoglobin improvement, clinical safety, access, or
  affordability. The immunodeficient model permits human HSPC engraftment
  without conditioning and partially rescued phenotype without `CIM058`.
- **Fact:** A new EHA/EBMT expert consensus identifies actual price, limited
  availability, and resource allocation as gene-therapy selection constraints.
  This reinforces the access boundary but is not new outcome evidence.
- **Current-source check:** On 2026-07-18, `NCT05356195` remained active, not
  recruiting, with 16 actual enrollees and no posted results. Exact
  ClinicalTrials.gov beta-thalassemia queries found no result or study update
  posted from July 17 onward. A PubMed July 17-18 entry-date query returned the
  conditioning preprint, the EHA/EBMT consensus, and one observational
  endocrinopathy study. These are query-bounded results.

## Mechanism, Scope, And Evidence Tier

- **Mechanism:** antibody blockade suppresses or depletes wild-type `CD117`
  HSPCs while an epitope-shielding `CD117 E73K` edit preserves donor-cell
  resistance and enables post-transplant selection.
- **Genotype and phenotype scope:** the disease-model cells came from the same
  homozygous `IVS1-110/IVS1-110` beta-thalassemia genotype. The experiment does
  not establish response across beta-thalassemia genotypes or TDT clinical
  phenotypes.
- **Evidence tier:** primary preclinical proof-of-concept in humanized
  immunodeficient mice, posted as a preprint. It is below peer-reviewed animal,
  nonhuman-primate, and human clinical evidence.

## Safety, Affordability, And Falsification

- **Safety risk:** the route adds a second genome edit and repeated antibody
  exposure. The reported prime-editing checks were favorable but not a full
  genomic-safety assessment. Long-term multilineage fitness, immune effects,
  antibody-withdrawal durability, graft rejection or graft-versus-host disease
  for an allogeneic route, and clinical conditioning toxicity remain open.
- **Affordability and access:** removing busulfan could reduce a major toxicity
  and supportive-care burden. Prime editing, cell manufacture, antibody
  production and dosing, transplant infrastructure, and follow-up remain, and
  no delivered-cost or Indonesia-access evidence was reported.
- **Falsification criterion:** deprioritize this conditioning route if an
  immunocompetent large-animal study cannot maintain clinically relevant,
  multilineage chimerism after antibody withdrawal without genotoxic
  conditioning or if full genomic, hematologic, and immune safety or total
  delivered cost fails the program threshold.

## Next Decisive Action

A transplant-conditioning owner should obtain the complete nonhuman-primate
dataset cited only as preliminary support and require a controlled
antibody-withdrawal study. The gate must measure durable multilineage
engraftment, marrow and immune safety, genomic integrity, supportive-care use,
and a costed autologous-versus-allogeneic process before any clinical or
affordable-cure promotion.
