## Current Findings

The next practical failure mode is not lack of information. It is asking the
wrong owner. A hematologist can review clinical relevance, but cannot alone
verify regulatory registration or financial coverage. Manufacturer medical
information can route official product questions, adverse-event reporting, and
medical-science-liaison contact, but cannot replace the treating clinician.
Pharmacy can verify dispensing and special-access operations, but not case
eligibility.

## Current Blockers

- Case-001 relevance remains blocked until a qualified clinician reviews the

- Local access remains blocked until the right owner confirms registration,

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Molecular diagnosis: `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH or delta-beta context, and
  hemoglobin-analysis timing relative to transfusion.
- Age and weight band, because adult-label, adult-trial, and pediatric-trial
  contexts remain separate evidence lanes.
- Transfusion burden: dates, pre/post hemoglobin, interval pattern, unit
  volume, red-cell fraction when available, body weight, and reaction history.
- Immune and blood-bank records: ABO/Rh, extended phenotype or genotype,
  Rh/Kell matching, antibody screen, alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs, and
  prior reaction context.
- Iron, organ, and safety context: ferritin trend, liver iron concentration,
  cardiac `T2*`, endocrine monitoring, renal/liver labs, chelator identity,
  liver tests, medication list, and local access or referral constraints.
