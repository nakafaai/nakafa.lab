# Quran Note: At-Tawbah 9:14

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran translation + tafsir intake

## Arabic Anchor

`وَيَشْفِ صُدُورَ قَوْمٍ مُّؤْمِنِينَ`

## First Reading

Quran 9:14 uses the root `shifa` for the healing or soothing of believers'
hearts in a conflict context. This is not a biomedical treatment verse.

## Tafsir Intake

Tafsir Ibn Kathir frames the verse within the wisdom of commanded struggle and
mentions the healing of believers' hearts. Reports cited there include a
specific application to Khuza'ah, while the wording also fits the broader
believing community.

## Research Implication

This verse belongs in the ethical and spiritual healing lane:

- grief, fear, anger, and injustice can be part of the human wound;
- Quranic `shifa` is broader than physical medicine;
- it would be a category error to use this verse as evidence for a molecule or
  a thalassemia treatment.

For Nakafa Lab, the practical effect is humility: the Quranic healing map
includes hearts and communities, so the biomedical lane must not pretend every
`shifa` verse points to a drug.
