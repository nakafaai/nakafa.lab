# Quran Anchor: Al-Hadid 57:25

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran anchor and ethical guardrail, not pharmacology proof

## Scope

This note documents Quran 57:25 for the thalassemia iron-homeostasis research
lane. It does not claim that iron, iron supplements, or any iron-containing
material cures thalassemia.

## Working Reading

Quran.com renders the verse with anchors around messengers, Scripture, balance,
justice, and iron with great might and benefits for humanity.

For Nakafa Lab, the relevant research posture is balance. Iron is necessary for
life and hemoglobin, but in transfusion-dependent thalassemia excess iron can
damage the liver, heart, endocrine system, and other organs.

## Research Implication

- Al-Hadid motivates disciplined investigation of iron biology.
- The thalassemia question is not "more iron"; it is iron regulation,
  overload, chelation, hepcidin, ferroportin, `TMPRSS6`, and erythroferrone.
- The verse belongs beside the hepcidin-ferroportin finding as an ethical
  guardrail: measure iron, respect its power, and avoid careless claims.
- Biomedical ranking still comes from clinical evidence, assays, and physician
  review.

## Guardrail

Do not use Quran 57:25 to justify iron supplementation for thalassemia patients.
Many severe thalassemia patients already face iron overload from transfusion and
ineffective erythropoiesis.
