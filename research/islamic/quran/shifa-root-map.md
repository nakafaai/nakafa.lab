# Quran Root Map: Shifa

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran-wide concept map

## Working Conclusion

The Quranic `shifa` lane should not be reduced to honey. The root appears across
spiritual, ethical, and embodied healing contexts. The research implication is:
seek causes and remedies, but do not force every healing verse into a biomedical
claim.

## Anchor Verses

| Verse | First reading | Research implication |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 9:14 | healing of hearts in a conflict context | spiritual/social healing, not a drug claim |
| 10:57 | Quran as healing for what is in the breasts | guidance, inner disease, ethics |
| 16:69 | bee-derived drink with healing for people | natural-world biomedical hypothesis anchor |
| 17:82 | Quran as healing and mercy for believers | spiritual and ethical healing |
| 26:80 | Ibrahim says Allah heals when he is ill | theology of cure and seeking means |
| 41:44 | Quran as guidance and healing for believers | guidance, clarity, and mercy |

## Correlation To Thalassemia Research

The Quran-wide pattern gives three separate lanes:

1. Faith and ethics: hope, patience, humility, truthfulness, and avoiding false
   cure claims.
2. Biomedical hypothesis: 16:69 justifies careful study of bee-derived
   substances, but does not prove a thalassemia cure.
3. Theology of means: 26:80 supports seeking treatment while knowing cure comes
   from Allah.

The 10:57 and 17:82 notes strengthen the first lane: the Quran heals and guides
the heart, which in this project means resisting exaggeration, despair, and
reckless claims.

The 9:14 and 41:44 notes complete the first-pass `shifa` root map. They confirm
that several healing verses are about hearts, guidance, and receptivity, not
physical pharmacology.

## Research Guardrail

If a verse is about the Quran healing hearts, do not convert it into a molecule
claim. If a verse points to natural creation, ask a testable question and still
require mechanism, toxicity, dose, and reproducibility.
