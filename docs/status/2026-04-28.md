# Daily Research Journal: 2026-04-28

Audience: founder / engineer
Status: active research summary, not a final conclusion and not medical advice

## TL;DR

Nakafa Lab has not found a new medicine.

Today's important update: the novelty claim is narrower. After `VGB-Ex01`
became the closest editing-registry benchmark, `CS-101` / `NCT06024876` is now a
stronger Asia clinical base-editing benchmark because a 2026 Nature report
described five phase 1 patients who stopped transfusion after autologous CD34+
base editing. `NCT07338344` remains the closest non-editing pharmacologic
benchmark because it tests luspatercept plus low-dose thalidomide with
transfusion-burden, HbF/gamma-globin, hemolysis, iron, and safety endpoints.

That means the Nakafa question is no longer: "Has anyone combined HbF and
clinical endpoints?" The stronger question is:

> Can we find a candidate that is more affordable, safer, and lab-validatable
> for HbF/F-cell rescue plus alpha-globin/autophagy, maturation, hemolysis,
> iron-risk, and access gates?

## Research Direction Status

The strongest lane remains:

`HPFH-like HbF/F-cell rescue + alpha-globin/autophagy cleanup + preserved erythroid maturation + no mature red-cell hemolysis`.

But its novelty must be compared against two proximity gates:

- `CS-101` and `VGB-Ex01`: editing benchmarks.
- `NCT07338344`: non-editing pharmacologic benchmark.

## Next Practical Action

1. Use the proximity novelty gate for every new candidate: is the candidate
   clearly different from the `CS-101`, `VGB-Ex01`, and luspatercept +
   thalidomide benchmarks?
2. Do not promote natural or supplement candidates before they pass identity,
   HbF/F-cell, alpha-globin/autophagy, maturation, hemolysis, and safety gates.
3. Keep the doctor-facing case packet and lab assay quote as the priority,
   because patient-relevance claims are invalid without them.
4. If a lab can add one expansion probe, PF-06409577 / `PRKAB1`-AMPK beta1
   remains the most coherent assay probe, not a therapy.
