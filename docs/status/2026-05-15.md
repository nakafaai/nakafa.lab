## Current Findings

The practical failure mode after first contact is copying too much. An owner may
send private contact details, ask for records, name a pathway, redirect to
another owner, or include patient-specific language. The public repo should
capture only labels and public sources, not the raw thread.

The response-capture gate supports labels such as `public_source_confirmed`,
`missing_records_named`, `clinician_mediated_documents_requested`,
`local_access_pathway_named`, `safety_or_adverse_event_route_named`, and
`patient_specific_treatment_advice_received`.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Molecular diagnosis: `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH or delta-beta context, and
  hemoglobin-analysis timing relative to transfusion.
- Age and weight band, because adult-label, adult-trial, pediatric-trial, and
  patient-specific care contexts remain separate.
- Transfusion burden: dates, pre/post hemoglobin, interval pattern, unit
  volume, red-cell fraction when available, body weight, and reaction history.
- Immune and blood-bank records: ABO/Rh, extended phenotype or genotype,
  Rh/Kell matching, antibody screen, alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs, and
  prior reaction context.
- Iron, organ, and safety context: ferritin trend, liver iron concentration,
  cardiac `T2*`, endocrine monitoring, renal/liver labs, liver tests,
  medication list, and local access or referral constraints.
