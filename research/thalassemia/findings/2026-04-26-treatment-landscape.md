# Finding: Current Treatment Landscape

Date checked: 2026-04-26
Evidence label: Guideline + regulator source synthesis

## Working Conclusion

Thalassemia is not an unsolved blank space. The hard gap is access to durable,
safe, and affordable disease-modifying or curative treatment.

For a patient reported to need weekly transfusion, the immediate research
question is not only "what new cure exists?" It is also whether diagnosis,
transfusion planning, iron control, immune complications, and access to newer
therapies have been fully reviewed.

## Current Care Baseline

- TIF 2025 describes regular transfusion as the mainstay for TDT.
- The usual target pre-transfusion hemoglobin for most patients is 9.5-10.5
  g/dL, often achieved with red cells every 2-5 weeks.
- TIF recommends ABO, Rh, and Kell-compatible blood, with antibody screening
  before transfusion.
- Iron overload monitoring is central: serum ferritin at least every 3 months,
  and liver/cardiac MRI starting around age 8-10 years where feasible.

## Approved Disease-Modifying Options

- Luspatercept/Reblozyl: FDA-approved for adult beta-thalassemia patients who
  require regular RBC transfusions. It reduces transfusion burden in some
  patients but is not a cure.
- Mitapivat/Aqvesme: FDA-approved for anemia in adults with alpha- or
  beta-thalassemia. It is oral and includes both transfusion-dependent and
  non-transfusion-dependent trial evidence. Key limitation: liver injury warning
  and REMS monitoring.

## Potentially Curative Options

- Allogeneic hematopoietic stem-cell transplant can be curative for selected
  patients, especially with a suitable donor, but has major risks and access
  constraints.
- Zynteglo is FDA-approved for adult and pediatric beta-thalassemia patients who
  require regular RBC transfusions.
- Casgevy is FDA-approved for transfusion-dependent beta-thalassemia in patients
  12 years and older. It requires autologous stem-cell collection, genome
  editing, myeloablative conditioning, and specialist infrastructure.

## Gene Therapy Access Frontier

The 2026-04-27 deep dive reframes gene therapy as proof of cure biology, not as
the affordable endpoint. Casgevy and Zynteglo validate hematopoietic stem-cell
modification as a route to durable transfusion independence, but the practical
barriers remain large: specialist centers, conditioning chemotherapy,
individualized manufacturing, payer support, and long-term monitoring.

For Nakafa Lab, this means affordable discovery should benchmark against the
same biology rather than drift into vague antioxidant claims. A lower-cost
candidate must show credible movement on HbF, globin balance, erythroid
maturation, red-cell survival, or transfusion burden.

## Practical Research Gaps

- Indonesia access: which therapies are approved, reimbursed, imported, or
  available through trials?
- Genotype: beta-thalassemia, HbE/beta-thalassemia, alpha-thalassemia, or mixed
  disease changes treatment reasoning.
- Immune status: autoimmune disease, alloantibodies, or transfusion reactions
  can change risk and eligibility.
- Affordability: a useful Nakafa Lab hypothesis must be cheaper than current
  curative options or make existing care safer and more effective.

## Next Data Needed For Case Context

- Exact diagnosis and genotype.
- Pre-transfusion hemoglobin and transfusion volume over time.
- Ferritin trend, liver iron, and cardiac T2* if available.
- Current chelation drug and dose.
- Antibody screen, transfusion reaction history, and autoimmune diagnosis.
- Liver test history, especially if mitapivat is ever discussed by a clinician.
