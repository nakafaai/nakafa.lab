\section*{Why We Are Asking}

The family is not asking for treatment changes from this document. The request
is to confirm labels, name missing records, and decide whether the case is
ready for specialist screening or still data-missing.

\section*{What To Confirm}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item What is the current diagnosis category: TDT, NTDT with regular
  transfusion, HbE/beta-thalassemia, alpha-thalassemia, mixed disease, or
  another label?
  \item What molecular records are available or missing: \texttt{HBB},
  \texttt{HBA1/HBA2}, HPFH/delta-beta-thalassemia, and HbF modifier context?
  \item Why is transfusion frequent: high annual requirement, small per-visit
  volume, low Hb increment, shortened red-cell survival, spleen or
  hypersplenism, alloantibody or autoantibody issue, matching issue,
  scheduling, or another cause?
  \item Are iron, chelation, cardiac, liver, endocrine, kidney, bone, spleen,
  immune, and blood-bank records complete enough to interpret risk?
  \item Is the case ready for specialist screening for HSCT, gene-cell therapy,
  disease-modifying drugs, or trials; or is it still data-missing, medically
  unsuitable, access-blocked, or already under review?
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Records To Attach Or Request}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Private historical thalassemia screening and early hematology PDFs.
  \item Current diagnosis note, current HPLC/electrophoresis, and molecular
  testing if available.
  \item Transfusion dates, body weight, blood volume, product type,
  pre-transfusion Hb, post-transfusion Hb if available, interval, and
  reactions.
  \item ABO/Rh/Kell, extended matching policy, antibody screen, named
  antibodies, DAT/direct Coombs with IgG/C3 specificity if available,
  crossmatch problems, and hemolysis workup.
  \item Ferritin trend, liver iron concentration MRI, cardiac \texttt{T2*},
  chelator name, dose, schedule, adherence, side effects, and monitoring.
  \item Autoimmune diagnosis, spleen status, infection/hepatitis context,
  organ-risk monitoring, and any prior referral or advanced-therapy discussion.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Answer Format Requested}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Corrected diagnosis/category label.
  \item Which public-safe labels above are correct, wrong, or not confirmable.
  \item First missing records the family should request.
  \item Whether specialist screening is ready, data-missing, medically
  unsuitable, access-blocked, or already under review.
  \item Which clinical owner should review the next packet: hematology,
  transfusion medicine, genetics, iron/organ-risk specialist, transplant
  center, or another service.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Boundary}

Do not change transfusion, chelation, immune medicines, supplements, diet,
referral plan, or trial enrollment based on this handoff. Clinical decisions
belong to licensed clinicians. The longer Nakafa Lab main paper is a research
appendix, not the first doctor-facing document. After a doctor replies, map the
response into labels and missing records before writing any public case update.
