# Finding: Case-001 Lab Recipient Qualification Gate

Date checked: 2026-06-08
Evidence label: lab-recipient qualification gate, not contact permission and not
treatment advice

## Direct Answer

Do not choose or contact a lab yet. The next useful blocker after the June 7
approval gate is recipient qualification: a future recipient must fit the
preclinical assay job, not a therapy, trial, purchase, import, or patient-care
job.

## Result

| Question | Current answer | Decision |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Can a recipient be selected now? | No founder approval is recorded. | Hold. |
| What recipient type is most useful first? | A lab or CRO that can answer model, readout, controls, raw data, cost, and timeline. | Qualify before contact. |
| Are therapy or trial centers the same as assay labs? | No. ClinicalTrials.gov CD34/HbF records are benchmark context. | Do not route as quote labs. |
| Can private case records be used in qualification? | No. | Public-safe preclinical context only. |

## Practical Rule

Before any approved send, classify the recipient as quote admin/CRO,
erythroid HbF screen, primary erythroid disease model, mature red-cell safety,
or clinical therapy/trial center. Only the first four can support the
preclinical quote lane, and only if privacy, ethics, raw-data, controls, and
model constraints pass.

## Boundary

This finding does not provide diagnosis, treatment advice, dosing, trial
screening, travel, import, purchase guidance, procurement guidance, or lab
contact permission.
