## Current Findings

The May 19 source check did not change the evidence boundary. TIF 2025 remains
the clinical-context baseline, FDA AQVESME remains adult-label and safety
context, and pediatric mitapivat remains watchlist-only.

ClinicalTrials.gov still supports the same split: `NCT04770779` is adult TDT
benchmark evidence with posted results; `NCT07506863` pediatric TDT and
`NCT07517133` pediatric NTDT are not-yet-recruiting watchlist records with no
posted results in the current check.

The practical blocker is packet readiness. Seven domains must be label-ready
and release-checked before the mitapivat review-readiness question is useful:
diagnosis/genotype, age/weight, transfusion burden, iron/organ, liver safety,
medication interaction, and local access owner context.

The May 19 gate does not answer whether mitapivat fits case-001. It only
decides whether the packet is complete enough to ask a hematologist one narrow
review-readiness question.

## Current Blockers

- Any missing required domain keeps the lane at `packet_not_ready`.
- Any raw private material keeps the public record at
  `packet_private_release_blocked` or `release_blocked` only.
- Pediatric mitapivat remains blocked from efficacy claims because current
  pediatric records are not yet recruiting and have no posted results.
- Adult mitapivat evidence remains context only. It does not prove individual
  fit, pediatric fit, local access, affordability, liver safety, interaction
  safety, or trial eligibility.
- The clinician question should not be sent if it would invite dosing,
  treatment-change, access, import, or trial-screening advice.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Diagnosis and genotype context: subtype wording, hemoglobin fractions,
  `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH, delta-beta-thalassemia, `HBG`, and modifier
  context when available.
- Age and weight band, because adult-label, adult-trial, pediatric-trial, and
  patient-specific care contexts remain separate.
- Transfusion burden: interval pattern, units or volume, body weight, pre/post
  hemoglobin context, reaction labels, and annual `ml/kg/year` if the clinic can
  calculate it.
- Iron and organ context: ferritin trend, liver iron concentration, cardiac
  `T2*`, chelation identity, and organ-monitoring labels.
- Liver safety context: liver test trend and hepatobiliary labels for clinician
  review only.
- Medication interaction context: current medicine categories and the right
  review owner, not public medication instructions.
- Local access context: regulatory, pharmacy, trial, payer, and affordability
  owner categories, not access instructions.
