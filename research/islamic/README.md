# Islamic Research Lane

## Scope

This lane studies Quran, tafsir, sirah, hadith, and Islamic scholarly
interpretation as a serious research stream. It should support reflection,
ethics, and hypothesis generation, while keeping scientific validation separate.

## First Anchor

Quran 16:68-69 mentions bees and a drink in which there is healing for people.
This is not enough by itself to claim a thalassemia treatment. It is enough to
justify careful study of bee products, historical interpretation, and modern
biomedical evidence.

## Method

- Read Arabic text first.
- Compare reliable Indonesian and English translations.
- Record tafsir separately from translation.
- Record hadith separately from tafsir.
- Extract possible research questions.
- Test biological claims only through scientific evidence.

## Guardrails

- Do not force a verse to say more than it says.
- Do not present a natural product as a cure without clinical evidence.
- Do not use faith language to bypass safety, dosage, toxicity, or trial design.
- Invite scholars and clinicians to review sensitive interpretations.
