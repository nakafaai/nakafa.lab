## Current Blockers

- Diagnosis and clinical-dependence wording still need the right owner to
  confirm or correct the current public-safe labels.
- Transfusion burden still cannot be quantified without private log rows for
  date, body weight, transfused volume or approved unit conversion, red-cell
  fraction or unit hematocrit, pre-Hb, and enough interval coverage.
- Immune and blood-bank interpretation remains blocked until the blood-bank or
  transfusion-medicine owner reviews antibody, DAT/direct Coombs, matching,
  reaction, spleen, and hemolysis context.
- Iron, chelation, and organ-risk interpretation remains blocked until the
  hematology plus MRI/cardiology/endocrine owners review ferritin, LIC, cardiac
  `T2*`, chelator identity, toxicity monitoring, and organ-risk screening.
- Advanced therapy and trial relevance remains blocked until a specialist owner
  marks the packet ready, partial, data-missing, medically unsuitable,
  access-blocked, or under review.
- Public updates remain blocked unless they are de-identified labels,
  missing-record requests, or next-owner states.

## Strongest Research Lanes

1. **Specialist-owner routing.** Map each unresolved domain to the owner most
   likely to answer it or name the missing record.
2. **Doctor-response triage.** Convert clinician replies into confirmed,
   corrected, missing-record, not-applicable, or specialist-review labels.
3. **Private record indexing.** Keep raw records private while extracting only
   public-safe labels, packet states, and missing-record lists.
4. **Transfusion burden readiness.** Complete the private burden log before
   calculating annual `ml/kg/year`, pure red-cell volume, Hb response, or iron
   input.
5. **Separate specialist packets.** Keep immune/blood-bank, iron/organ-risk,
   genetics, and advanced-therapy screening as separate owner routes.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

Collect raw records only in ignored private storage. Public repo updates should
stay de-identified and label-level unless the release checklist passes.

- owner-routing metadata: domain, requested owner type, owner state, missing
  records requested, generalized response timing, and next public-safe label;
- diagnosis and molecular context: current clinician diagnosis label, HPLC or
  electrophoresis timing, `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH or delta-beta context, and
  HbF-modifier context if available;
- transfusion-burden minimums: date, body weight, transfused volume or approved
  unit conversion, red-cell fraction or unit hematocrit, pre-Hb, at least two
  rows, and at least a 28-day window;
- burden explanation fields: post-Hb, product type, interval, reaction history,
  matching context, delayed symptoms, and whether weekly visits reflect high
  total requirement or low per-visit volume;
- immune and blood-bank packet: antibody screen, named alloantibodies,
  DAT/direct Coombs specificity, extended matching policy, red-cell
  phenotype/genotype, crossmatch difficulty, spleen context, and hemolysis
  workup;
- iron, chelation, and organ-risk packet: ferritin trend, LIC MRI, cardiac
  `T2*`, chelator identity, monitoring, side effects, and organ-risk screening;
- advanced-therapy history: prior discussion, referral, rejection, access
  barrier, or specialist opinion for HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR therapy,
  luspatercept, mitapivat, hydroxyurea, or trial screening.
