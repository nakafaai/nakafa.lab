# Case-001 Affordable-Cure Candidate Promotion Gate

Date checked: 2026-05-30
Evidence label: literature, registry, pharmacoeconomic, prioritization, and
privacy synthesis, not medical advice

## Direct Answer

After the May 29 gap matrix, the next blocker is candidate promotion discipline.
The project should not add another "possible cure" lane unless the candidate
can name the benchmark gap it targets and how it can fail.

Current operational label:

`case001_affordable_cure_candidate_promotion_gate_ready`

## Four-Part Gate

Any promoted candidate must state:

1. the benchmark gap: cost, infrastructure, conditioning, monitoring, access,
   safety, fertility, or assay feasibility;
2. the closest curative and non-curative benchmarks;
3. the measurable endpoints;
4. the falsification test.

If one part is missing, the lane stays `hold`, `benchmark_only`, or `reject`.

## 2026 Source Refresh

The current source check does not create a new cure claim.

- A 2026 PubMed-indexed exa-cel cost-effectiveness model projects favorable US
  value versus standard care, but it is sponsor-affiliated and must be balanced
  against independent or public HTA reviews.
- CDA-AMC's TDT pharmacoeconomic review highlights comparative-evidence
  uncertainty, high acquisition-cost sensitivity, and price-reduction
  dependency.
- ClinicalTrials.gov still keeps close benchmarks active or recently updated:
  CS-101 phase 1 completed, CS-101 phase 2 not yet recruiting, VGB-Ex01
  recruiting, luspatercept plus thalidomide recruiting, and AND017 recruiting.

## Decision Rule

Do not promote by excitement. Promote only by gap, endpoint, safety stop, and
falsification.

Case-001 relevance remains blocked until qualified specialist review.

## Islamic Motivation Boundary

Quran 13:17 supports discarding weak foam-like signals. Quran 55:7-9 supports
measured claims. These are process anchors, not biomedical evidence for any
therapy.
