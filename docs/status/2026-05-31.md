recommendation, and no proof of Indonesia access to HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR

## Current Findings

- A candidate can pass the May 30 promotion gate and still fail assay
  readiness.
- HUDEP2 and reporter systems are screening contexts, not patient-specific
  treatment evidence.
- Hemolysis and viability readouts are safety filters, not proof of clinical
  benefit.
- 2026 model papers strengthen model-selection discipline, but do not establish
  case-001 relevance.
- Case-001 relevance remains blocked until qualified specialist review.

## Current Blockers

- No candidate can move to lab quote without exact identity or a stated
  identity gap.
- Missing model, control, endpoint, replicate, raw-data, or stop-rule details
  keep a candidate in `hold_for_model_or_endpoint_gap`.
- Approved or late gene-cell therapies remain benchmarks only; they do not
  prove Indonesia access, center availability, affordability, or case
  suitability.
- Patient samples, raw records, doctor messages, owner replies, exact private
  dates, trial-screening instructions, travel guidance, import guidance, and
  dosing questions remain blocked from public artifacts.

## Strongest Research Lanes

1. Minimum assay readiness: apply the May 31 gate before any lab quote or
   partner discussion.
2. Affordable-cure candidate promotion: require a named benchmark gap,
   endpoint set, safety stop, access boundary, and falsification test before
   promotion.
3. Curative affordability gaps: keep cost, infrastructure, conditioning,
   monitoring, access, safety, fertility, and assay feasibility separate.
4. Advanced-therapy route triage: keep HSCT, autologous gene-cell therapy,
   non-curative drugs, stabilization, and registry benchmarks in separate
   branches.
5. Clinical packet readiness: keep diagnosis, transfusion, immune, iron,
   organ, fertility, donor, access, and payer domains upstream of specialist
   review.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Diagnosis and genotype labels, including clinician-confirmed disease category
  and molecular-record status.
- Transfusion-burden labels: interval, pre-transfusion hemoglobin, units or
  volume, body weight, Hb response, and annualized burden.
- Immune and blood-bank labels: matching policy, antibody screen,
  alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs context, reaction history, phenotype or
  genotype context, and crossmatch issues.
- Iron, organ-risk, and chelation labels: ferritin trend, liver iron, cardiac
  `T2*`, chelator identity, toxicity monitoring, and organ-risk notes.
- Infection, fertility, transplant-readiness, HLA/donor, center, payer,
  geography, and affordability labels only as public-safe summaries after
  private owner review.
