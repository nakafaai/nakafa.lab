# Finding: Casgevy FDA Age-Expansion Benchmark

Date checked: 2026-07-07
Status: regulatory benchmark update, not treatment advice

## Direct Answer

The new current signal is regulatory, not patient-specific: the FDA product
page now lists Casgevy under `STN 125787` for patients aged 2 years and older
with transfusion-dependent beta-thalassemia. The prior 12 years and older
label remains visible under the earlier `STN 125785` entry.

For Nakafa Lab this changes the benchmark map, not the Case-001 branch state.
The public state remains:

`branch_review_handoff_packet_incomplete`

## Why This Matters

Casgevy is an autologous gene-editing benchmark. The FDA's July 2026 expansion
means the U.S. regulatory age boundary moved younger for TDT, making the
approved advanced-therapy benchmark broader than older repo notes stated.

That does not prove local access, affordability, center availability,
reimbursement, case suitability, or referral readiness in Indonesia. It also
does not remove the need for a hematologist or advanced-therapy owner to review
the private packet.

## What To Do With This Update

Use it for three safe tasks:

- update source maps that cite Casgevy as an approved benchmark;
- ask public helpers to cite the exact FDA product page or approval notice if
  they mention Casgevy age criteria;
- keep any private clinical question ordered through packet readiness first,
  then branch owner review.

Do not use it to ask for public eligibility, travel, import, purchase,
enrollment, treatment change, or lab contact.

## Doctor-Facing Interpretation

If a clinician is asked about advanced therapy, the first question is still:

> Is the private six-domain packet complete enough to decide whether an
> autologous gene-cell therapy owner should review the case?

The FDA update can be mentioned as a current benchmark source, but it should
not replace the six private packet domains: diagnosis/genotype/phenotype,
transfusion burden, blood-bank and immune history, iron and organ-risk state,
HCT/gene-cell access context, and consent or owner-review boundaries.
