# Daily Research Journal: 2026-05-02

Audience: founder / engineer
Status: active research summary, not a final conclusion and not medical advice

## TL;DR

Nakafa Lab has not found a new medicine.

Today the highest-value blocker is the clinician-response step. After the
doctor handoff is sent, any reply must be mapped into labels and missing
records before it changes the public repo or the case workflow.

Current operational label:

`doctor_response_triage_ready`

## What Changed

| Area | What changed | Founder takeaway |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Doctor response | Added a triage gate for clinician replies. | A reply is useful only if it tells us which labels are confirmed, corrected, missing-record blocked, not applicable, or under specialist review. |
| Privacy | Blocked raw chat screenshots and treatment instructions from public artifacts. | Only de-identified labels, missing records, and next clinical owner can move into public docs. |
| Workflow | Added a notebook that ranks response domains without private data. | Diagnosis, clinical-dependence, and transfusion-burden replies are highest priority. |

## Practical Rule

Do not treat one doctor message as a global answer. Route it by domain:

- diagnosis;
- clinical dependence;
- transfusion burden;
- immune and blood-bank;
- iron, chelation, and organ risk;
- referral readiness.

## Boundary

This journal does not provide diagnosis, dosing, transfusion changes,
chelation changes, immune-medicine changes, supplement changes, referral
decisions, or trial eligibility. It only records a safer way to process a
clinician response.
