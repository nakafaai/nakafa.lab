# Assay-Ready HbF Screen

Date checked: 2026-04-26
Status: experiment design note, not a wet-lab protocol for unsupervised use

## Working Conclusion

Nakafa Lab needs an assay-first discovery path. The strongest near-term screen
is not "try random natural products"; it is a staged HbF and red-cell safety
funnel that can reject weak or unsafe candidates early.

## Minimum Screen

| Gate | Purpose | Pass signal |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Identity | Know what is being tested | compound, extract, batch, supplier, storage |
| Reporter | Detect HbF induction cheaply | endogenous `HBG1/HBG2` or HbF reporter signal |
| Erythroid validation | Confirm signal in relevant cells | gamma-globin mRNA, HbF protein, F-cells |
| Thalassemia relevance | Test disease-like biology | globin-chain balance or patient-derived cells |
| Red-cell safety | Exclude membrane damage | low hemolysis versus negative control |
| General safety | Exclude broad toxicity | viability and stress markers in relevant cells |

## Practical Candidate Flow

1. Start with benchmarks: hydroxyurea, sirolimus, and thalidomide-class
   references as positive or cautionary comparators.
2. Test candidate materials only after identity and batch documentation are
   strong enough to repeat.
3. Run HbF reporter or gamma-globin mRNA screen before expensive disease models.
4. Confirm hits in erythroid differentiation or patient-derived systems through
   a qualified lab partner.
5. Run hemolysis and cytotoxicity gates before any animal or clinical thinking.
6. Promote only candidates with a clean source trail, repeated signal, and a
   clinician-readable safety summary.

## Bee-Derived Candidate Rule

Bee-derived materials can enter this screen only as defined materials. "Honey",
"propolis", "royal jelly", "venom", or "brood" are too broad unless the source,
processing, concentration, contaminants, and batch are documented.

Melittin or venom-derived fractions require hemolysis-first triage because red
cell membrane damage is an obvious hazard.

## What This Solves

This turns Quran-inspired and natural-product hypotheses into testable science.
It preserves the spiritual motivation while forcing every biological claim
through repeatable HbF, erythroid, and red-cell safety endpoints.
