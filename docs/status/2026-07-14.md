# Daily Research Journal: 2026-07-14

Audience: founder / engineer
Status: genome-editing affordability gate tightened, not medical advice

## Question And Decision

**Question:** Does current FDA genome-editing guidance make an ex vivo editing
route credible enough to promote as affordable?

**Decision:** `hold_for_regulatory_safety_and_cost_evidence`. Gene-editing
routes remain curative benchmarks, not affordable-route candidates.

## What Changed

| Evidence | Decision-relevant result |
| --- | --- |
| FDA April 2026 NGS safety draft | Adds product-representative on-target, off-target, genomic-integrity, human-variation, reproducibility, and reporting work to the credible-route test. |
| FDA June 2026 prior-knowledge draft | Allows some platform methods and pipelines to be reused, but generally not product-specific results across different guide RNAs or genomic targets. |
| ClinicalTrials.gov query | No beta-thalassemia results were first posted from July 1 through July 14 under the exact query; no new outcome justified a route-rank change. |

## Evidence Read

- **Fact:** Both genome-editing documents are nonbinding drafts. The April
  draft's public-comment deadline is 2026-07-14.
- **Fact:** The April draft covers ex vivo products and recommends representative
  human cells, biological replicates, sequencing depth and quality criteria,
  off-target confirmation, relevant human genetic variation, and genomic-
  integrity analysis where indicated.
- **Fact:** The June draft permits reuse of suitable platform knowledge, but
  says guide-RNA, locus, cell, editor, and manufacturing differences can keep
  safety evidence product-specific.
- **Interpretation:** An affordable editing claim needs a regulator-owned and
  manufacturing-owned safety scope plus total delivered cost. Product price or
  reagent price is not enough.
- **Contradiction:** Platform reuse may reduce duplicated work, so the evidence
  does not prove that every assay must be new or quantify an unavoidable cost.
- **Confidence:** High for the FDA draft content; low for Indonesia-specific
  cost and feasibility because no accepted plan or quote is public.
- **Open question:** Which safety elements can the closest Asia editing platform
  reuse, and what are the remaining product-specific cost and capacity needs?

## Falsification And Next Action

Replace the hold only when a qualified regulatory and manufacturing owner
documents the regulator-accepted safety plan, reusable versus product-specific
elements, raw-data and software traceability, and total delivered cost. A final
guidance that materially removes these elements would also trigger reassessment.

Next decisive action: obtain one public-safe, owner-validated safety-package
scope and cost breakdown for the closest Asia editing benchmark. Do not begin an
NGS analysis without real sequence inputs and essential metadata.
