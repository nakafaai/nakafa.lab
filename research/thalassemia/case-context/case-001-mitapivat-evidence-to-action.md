# Case-001 Mitapivat Evidence To Action

Date: 2026-05-16
Status: public-safe evidence-to-action aid, not treatment advice
Privacy: no private records, screenshots, identifiers, exact dates, hospital
names, clinician names, doctor messages, owner messages, or patient-specific
treatment instructions

## Purpose

Use this note when explaining the current mitapivat lane to a clinician or
research collaborator. The goal is to keep adult evidence, pediatric registry
watchlists, safety gates, access gates, and case relevance separate.

Current label: `mitapivat_evidence_to_action_ready`

## Safe Summary

Adult mitapivat evidence is now strong enough to use as a benchmark because the
adult TDT registry snapshot has posted results and the FDA label covers adults
with alpha- or beta-thalassemia anemia. Pediatric mitapivat records remain
not-yet-recruiting watchlist records with no posted results. Case relevance
still requires qualified clinician review.

## What To Ask A Clinician

Ask this narrow question:

> Given the diagnosis, age and weight band, transfusion burden, iron and organ
> status, liver tests, medication list, and local access constraints, is
> mitapivat clinically worth reviewing at all, or should this lane stay paused?

## What Not To Ask

- Do not ask for dosing or treatment changes.
- Do not ask whether adult approval automatically applies to the case.
- Do not ask a manufacturer, pharmacy, trial owner, or payer to replace the
  treating hematologist.
- Do not share raw records through public channels.
