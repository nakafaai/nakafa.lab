# Finding: HbF Reactivation Target Map

Date checked: 2026-04-26
Evidence label: target-prioritization synthesis

## Working Conclusion

The strongest cure-oriented low-cost research lane is not generic antioxidant
screening. It is fetal hemoglobin reactivation and globin-balance biology.

CRISPR therapies validate the endpoint: if enough fetal hemoglobin is produced
durably in red cells, many transfusion-dependent beta-thalassemia patients can
become transfusion independent. Nakafa Lab's question is whether a cheaper,
safer, scalable route can approximate that biology.

## Target Families

| Target family | Role | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `BCL11A` | Major HbF silencer | Clinically validated by CRISPR HbF-reactivation strategy |
| `HBG1/HBG2` promoters | Gamma-globin genes | Direct locus for fetal hemoglobin production |
| `HBS1L-MYB` | HbF modifier region | Human genetic modifier of HbF and disease severity |
| `KLF1` | Erythroid transcription factor | Can influence HbF through erythroid programs and `BCL11A` |
| Epigenetic regulators | DNA methylation, histone state | Drug-like route for HbF induction, but safety is limiting |
| Erythroid maturation pathways | Red-cell precursor survival/maturation | Relevant when HbF induction alone is insufficient |

## Candidate Drug Classes To Track

- Hydroxyurea: low-cost and clinically familiar, but response is variable and
  evidence is stronger in some NTDT/HbE-beta settings than as a universal TDT
  cure.
- DNMT inhibitors such as decitabine/azacitidine: strong HbF biology but
  cytotoxicity, dosing, and safety are major barriers.
- Immunomodulatory derivatives such as pomalidomide/lenalidomide: HbF signal
  exists, but immune, thrombotic, teratogenic, and cost issues make them
  high-caution candidates.
- Sirolimus: repurposing candidate with clinical-trial interest for HbF
  induction and quality-of-life endpoints.
- Phenotypic-screen hits: useful for discovery, but must pass human erythroid
  validation and safety before becoming hypotheses.

## Prioritization Rule

Rank candidates by:

1. HbF or transfusion-burden evidence in humans.
2. Safety in chronically anemic or transfused patients.
3. Mechanistic proximity to `BCL11A`, `HBG1/HBG2`, `HBS1L-MYB`, `KLF1`, or
   erythroid maturation.
4. Affordability and availability in Indonesia.
5. Compatibility with chelation, liver monitoring, and immune complications.
