# Quran Anchor: Ar-Ra'd 13:17

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran ethics and research method, not pharmacology proof

## Scope

This note documents Quran 13:17 as a research-method guardrail. It does not
identify a thalassemia molecule, herb, supplement, or treatment.

## Working Reading

The verse gives a parable of rainwater flowing through valleys according to
their capacity, foam carried by floodwater, and similar foam from heated ore
used for ornaments or tools. The foam passes away, while what benefits people
remains.

For Nakafa Lab, the research implication is disciplined attrition: many ideas
will look impressive at first, but weak, unsafe, unmeasured, or irreproducible
signals should be allowed to disappear.

## Fit With Thalassemia Research

This anchor strengthens four rules:

- do not preserve a candidate just because it is emotionally meaningful;
- require measurable benefit: HbF, hemoglobin, transfusion burden, red-cell
  survival, iron burden, hemolysis, viability, or patient-relevant safety;
- keep noisy or negative searches in the repo when they prevent repeated false
  starts;
- let failed candidates teach the pipeline instead of forcing them into cure
  claims.

## Practical Rule

Every candidate should end in one of three states:

- `promote`: measurable endpoint improves without safety failure;
- `hold`: signal is incomplete, noisy, or identity-limited;
- `reject`: safety, reproducibility, identity, or endpoint failure is clear.

This is already reflected in the first assay work order and candidate scoring
model. Quran 13:17 gives ethical language for the same research discipline:
benefit remains; foam is discarded.

## Guardrail

Do not use this verse to claim that any specific material cures thalassemia.
Use it to make the research pipeline more honest about evidence attrition.
