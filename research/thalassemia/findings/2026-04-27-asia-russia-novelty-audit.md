# Finding: Asia And Russia Novelty Audit

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: regional novelty audit, not treatment advice

## Direct Answer

Yes, Asia is highly relevant. The strongest regional signals found today are
from Thailand and China, with important supporting signals from India, Iran,
Turkey, Hong Kong, Japan, Korea, and Russia.

The audit does not support a claim that Nakafa Lab has found a cure. It also
does not support the opposite claim that "no one is working on this." Many
groups are working on thalassemia, HbF, gene therapy, gene editing, transplant,
luspatercept, mitapivat, hydroxyurea, thalidomide-class therapy, and iron-axis
interventions.

The stronger and more honest conclusion is:

> The component biology is not new. The still-underanswered opportunity is an
> integrated, affordable, beta-thalassemia-relevant validation package that
> requires HbF/F-cell rescue, alpha-globin burden or cleanup readouts, erythroid
> maturation, viability, mature red-cell hemolysis safety, and realistic access.

That is the question Nakafa Lab should pitch to a lab.

## Novelty Argument To Use With A Lab

Do not say:

> "Nobody has done thalassemia HbF research before."

That is false.

Say this:

> "HbF reactivation, HPFH biology, gene editing, hydroxyurea, thalidomide-class
> drugs, luspatercept, mitapivat, and alpha-globin/autophagy biology already
> exist. Nakafa Lab wants to test whether any affordable candidate can pass a
> stricter integrated gate: HbF/F-cell rescue plus alpha-globin burden or
> autophagy cleanup, preserved erythroid maturation, viability, and mature
> red-cell hemolysis safety."

This is a stronger pitch because it gives the lab a falsifiable work order. It
does not rely on hype.

## What To Ask A Doctor Or Research Lab

For a hematologist:

- Can the patient's exact diagnosis be pinned down as TDT, NTDT, HbE/beta, alpha
  thalassemia, or mixed disease?
- Are `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HbF, HbA2, HbE, XmnI-HBG2, BCL11A/HBS1L-MYB, and
  transfusion/immune data available?
- Is hydroxyurea, luspatercept, mitapivat, HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR therapy,
  or a trial/referral route clinically relevant for this exact case?

For a research lab:

- Can the lab use beta-thalassemia-relevant cells, ideally patient-derived or
  primary erythroid cells, not K562-only?
- Can it measure HbF protein, F-cells, HBG1/HBG2 expression, alpha/non-alpha
  globin balance, free or insoluble alpha-globin, `ULK1`, p62/SQSTM1, erythroid
  maturation, viability, apoptosis, and mature RBC hemolysis?
- Can it include positive and negative comparators such as hydroxyurea,
  sirolimus, and known cytotoxic or hemolytic controls?

## Limitations

This is not a formal systematic review. It used accessible indexed sources:
PubMed/NCBI snapshots, ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 snapshots, browser inspection
of a PubMed Thailand beta0-thalassemia/HbE source, and focused web checks around
J-STAGE, KoreaMed, ChiCTR, CRIS, jRCT, CTRI, CyberLeninka, and eLIBRARY. Some
regional databases, especially CNKI/Wanfang, Ichushi, KMbase/RISS, and
eLIBRARY, may require native-language queries or institutional access.

Therefore the conclusion is not "global novelty is proven." The conclusion is:

The accessible evidence strongly supports Asia as a critical research map, while
the exact Nakafa integrated affordable validation package still looks
underanswered and worth testing.
