# Finding: Indonesia Access Deep Dive

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Indonesia policy + registry + literature synthesis

## Working Conclusion

Indonesia access is a separate scientific problem. Global proof that HSCT,
lentiviral gene addition, or CRISPR-based HbF reactivation can work does not
mean those options are reachable for Indonesian patients. The current evidence
points to supportive care as the routine backbone, emerging national genomic
capacity, and gene-therapy coordination work, but not routine domestic gene
therapy access.

## What Looks Verified

- Indonesian guidance frames thalassemia care around diagnosis, lifelong
  transfusion, chelation, and complication monitoring.
- The Ministry of Health guideline notes large annual cost for one major
  thalassemia child and recognizes transfusion risks, iron monitoring needs,
  DNA testing, HPLC, and MRI T2*.
- RSAB Harapan Kita's 2025-2029 business plan lists a thalassemia strategic
  project that includes variant detection, a GenoArray thalassemia detection kit
  for the Indonesia population with NGI, and coordination with the Ministry of
  Health and P2PTM for gene therapy.
- A 2025 case report says thalassemia treatment in Indonesia remains largely
  supportive in many settings and states that gene therapy remained unavailable
  domestically at the time of writing, while reporting a first Indonesian
  pediatric patient treated through gene therapy with six-month
  transfusion-independence.
- ClinicalTrials.gov all-status search for thalassemia in Indonesia found six
  records; the snapshot did not show an active Indonesia gene-therapy trial.

## What Remains Unverified

- BPOM registration status for Casgevy, Zynteglo, Reblozyl, and Aqvesme.
- Whether any Indonesian hospital can provide thalassemia gene therapy
  domestically rather than referring abroad or coordinating future access.
- Whether BPJS has a pathway for disease-modifying thalassemia drugs or
  gene/cell therapy beyond baseline transfusion and chelation care.
- Which centers can evaluate an adult TDT patient for HSCT, gene therapy, or
  clinical-trial eligibility.

## Research Implication

Nakafa Lab needs two Indonesia tracks:

1. Access verification: BPOM, BPJS, RSAB Harapan Kita, transplant centers, and
   hematology referral pathways.
2. Affordable discovery: candidates that can be produced, standardized, tested,
   and eventually priced for Indonesia, not only for high-income systems.

For the patient's family context, this also means the immediate useful work is
not only "find a new molecule." It is to prepare a clinician-ready data package:
diagnosis, genotype, transfusion burden, chelation, iron MRI, antibody history,
immune status, and referral questions.
