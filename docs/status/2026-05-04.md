## Current Practical Goal

Contact one owner at a time with one narrow ask:

1. hematology or thalassemia clinic: confirm diagnosis wording, dependence
   category, and missing records;
2. transfusion service or blood bank: define burden and immune/blood-bank record
   needs;
3. molecular genetics or genetic counseling: define molecular confirmation and
   modifier-record needs;
4. iron, MRI, cardiology, endocrine, or organ-monitoring owner: define iron and
   organ-risk packet completeness;
5. transplant, gene-cell therapy, or trial team: define whether screening is
   ready, partial, blocked by missing data, access-blocked, medically unsuitable,
   or under review.

4. **Immune and blood-bank packet.** Separate transfusion service questions
   from broad hematology questions so antibody, DAT/direct Coombs, matching,
   reaction, spleen, and hemolysis records are reviewed by the right owner.
5. **Iron and organ-risk packet.** Keep ferritin, LIC, cardiac `T2*`,
   chelator monitoring, and organ screening together as a specialist packet
   before any advanced-therapy relevance claim.
6. **Specialist-screening readiness.** Treat HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR
   therapy, luspatercept, mitapivat, hydroxyurea, and trial questions as
   screening lanes, not repository recommendations.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

Collect raw records only in ignored private storage. Public updates should stay
de-identified and label-level unless the release checklist passes.

- owner outreach state: owner route, current public-safe state, missing-record
  request, redirect state, private-response state, and next public-safe label;
- diagnosis and molecular context: current clinician diagnosis wording,
  clinical-dependence category, HPLC or electrophoresis timing, `HBB`,
  `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH, delta-beta-thalassemia, HbF-modifier, and family-testing
  context if available;
- transfusion-burden minimums: date, body weight, transfused volume or
  approved unit conversion, red-cell fraction or unit hematocrit, pre-Hb,
  post-Hb if available, at least two rows, and at least a 28-day window;
- immune and blood-bank packet: antibody screen, named alloantibodies,
  DAT/direct Coombs specificity, extended matching policy, red-cell
  phenotype/genotype, crossmatch difficulty, reaction history, spleen context,
  and hemolysis workup;
- iron, chelation, and organ-risk packet: ferritin trend, LIC MRI, cardiac
  `T2*`, chelator identity, monitoring, side effects, and organ-risk screening;
- advanced-therapy history: prior discussion, referral, rejection,
  access barrier, or specialist opinion for HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR therapy,
  luspatercept, mitapivat, hydroxyurea, or trial screening.
