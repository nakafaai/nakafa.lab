Nakafa Lab is organizing source-backed thalassemia research. We have not found
a cure, and we are not asking the public for treatment advice.

The current blocker is routing: a transfusion-dependent thalassemia
branch-review packet needs qualified, role-bounded review. We need help from
thalassemia clinicians, transfusion medicine, molecular genetics, iron/organ
monitoring, HCT or gene-cell therapy pathway experts, wet-lab assay reviewers,
privacy-safe software builders, access mappers, funders, and Islamic ethics
reviewers.

Please do not post raw records, screenshots, doctor messages, names, hospitals,
exact dates, private contact details, or treatment suggestions. If you can help,
reply with your role, the narrow domain you can review, and any public source or
registry ID you can point to.

## Longer Public Post Draft

Nakafa Lab is an open, source-backed research effort focused on thalassemia. The
current blocker is coordination: a transfusion-dependent thalassemia
branch-review packet cannot be reviewed as one broad internet question.

We need role-bounded help across diagnosis/genetics, transfusion burden,
blood-bank and immune records, iron and organ monitoring, HCT and gene-cell
therapy pathway explanation, clinical-trial or registry disambiguation,
wet-lab assay feasibility, privacy-safe tooling, access mapping, funding, and
Islamic ethics boundaries.

This is not public treatment advice, and we have not found a cure. Please do
not post raw records, screenshots, doctor messages, names, hospitals, exact
dates, private contact details, eligibility claims, referral instructions, or
treatment suggestions. If you can help, reply with your role, narrow review
domain, and any public source or registry ID that supports the pathway you want
to discuss.

## Do Not Share

- Raw records, screenshots, doctor messages, or medical files.
- Names, exact dates, hospitals, clinicians, phone numbers, local paths,
  accession numbers, or private timeline details.
- Full case-specific lab values unless a release checklist approves them.
- Public treatment recommendations or cure claims.

## Inbound Routing

| Public reply type | Safe next step |
| --- | --- |
| Clinical, genetics, transfusion, iron, or organ role | Ask which packet domain they can review; route to public collaborator intake and private introduction only if the scope is bounded. |
| HCT, gene-cell therapy, or trial-access role | Ask for public pathway explanation, public registry ID, country, and constraints; do not ask for eligibility. |
| Wet-lab partner | Ask for assay type, readouts, sample requirement, biosafety boundary, and quote constraints; do not route patient samples. |
| Software, data, privacy, funding, access, or Islamic ethics role | Route to a scoped task that does not require private records. |
| General supporter | Ask them to share the public call with qualified roles or support source mapping, not medical interpretation. |
| Treatment advice in public | Mark `blocked_treatment_advice` and do not copy into public repo. |
| Request for raw records in public | Mark `blocked_raw_record_request` and move to privacy review. |
