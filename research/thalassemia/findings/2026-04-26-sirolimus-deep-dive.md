# Sirolimus Deep Dive

Date checked: 2026-04-26
Status: research evidence map, not treatment advice

## Working Conclusion

Sirolimus is a credible second-priority research lane after hydroxyurea because
it has direct human beta-thalassemia evidence for gamma-globin induction. It is
not a cure claim. The current evidence supports deeper study of who responds,
what endpoints change, and whether immune and monitoring risks make it practical
in Indonesia.

## Why This Matters

The core cure-oriented question is whether we can make erythroid cells produce
more usable hemoglobin without replacing the entire blood-forming system.
Sirolimus matters because the Sirthalaclin pilot trial tested a repurposed drug
against the same broad direction: increasing gamma-globin and fetal hemoglobin
biology in beta-thalassemia.

## Human Evidence

- The Sirthalaclin trial studied eight patients with beta-plus/beta-plus or
  beta-plus/beta-zero genotypes.
- The trial used low-dose oral sirolimus over 24 to 48 weeks.
- Reported signals included increased gamma-globin mRNA in blood and erythroid
  precursor cells.
- The same report described lower markers linked to ineffective erythropoiesis,
  including free alpha-globin chains, bilirubin, soluble transferrin receptor,
  and ferritin.
- Transfusion demand index decreased in seven of eight patients.
- The main reported side effect in the abstract was recurrent stomatitis.

## Mechanism Hypothesis

Sirolimus should be tracked as a multi-endpoint erythroid modifier, not only as
an HbF inducer.

- HbF lane: gamma-globin mRNA and HbF protein.
- Chain-balance lane: free alpha-globin reduction.
- Proteostasis lane: ULK1/autophagy activation.
- Stabilization lane: AHSP expression as a possible alpha-hemoglobin buffer.
- Clinical lane: transfusion burden, ferritin trend, and quality-of-life
  signals.

## Access And Safety Caveats

Sirolimus is repurposed, which may help affordability, but it is also an
immunomodulating drug. For Nakafa Lab this creates a hard rule: no patient-facing
use without hematologist review, drug-level monitoring, and review of infection,
autoimmune, kidney, liver, lipid, mouth-ulcer, wound-healing, and drug
interaction risks.

This caution is especially relevant to the de-identified family context because
an autoimmune issue was reported but not yet characterized.

## Research Gate

Keep sirolimus at priority rank 2 if future work confirms:

- reproducible gamma-globin or HbF increase;
- measurable red-cell or transfusion-burden improvement;
- a responder signature that can be tested before treatment;
- monitoring requirements that are realistic in Indonesia.

Lower its priority if the signal remains limited to small selected cohorts, if
immune risk is too high, or if no affordable monitoring path exists.
