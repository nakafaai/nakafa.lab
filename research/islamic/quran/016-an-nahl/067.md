# Quran Anchor: An-Nahl 16:67

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran anchor for provision and caution, not pharmacology proof

## Scope

This note documents the Quranic anchor involving fruits of date-palms and
grapevines. It does not claim that grape-derived materials cure thalassemia.

## Working Reading

The Quran.com translation separates intoxicants from wholesome provision and
frames the verse as a sign for people who understand.

For Nakafa Lab, this verse is important because it adds caution. A natural
material can be provision, but processing, dose, and use still matter.

## Research Implication

- Natural origin does not automatically mean safe.
- Fermentation, extraction, concentration, and formulation can change risk.
- Any grape-associated research candidate, including resveratrol, must be
  treated as a defined compound or standardized material, not as a generic food
  claim.
- Patient-facing use requires clinician review.

## Guardrail

Do not collapse "grapevines are mentioned" into "grape-derived compounds treat
thalassemia." The verse supports reflection and caution, not shortcut claims.
