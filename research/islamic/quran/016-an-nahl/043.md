# Quran Anchor: An-Nahl 16:43

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran research ethics, not pharmacology proof

## Scope

This note documents Quran 16:43 as an expert-consultation guardrail for Nakafa
Lab. It does not identify a thalassemia treatment, molecule, herb, supplement,
or clinical action.

## Working Reading

The verse instructs asking the people of knowledge when one does not know. In
its immediate Quranic context, the verse addresses knowledge about messengers
and revelation, not hematology. The research-method lesson is narrower and
safer: ignorance should not be hidden by confidence.

For Nakafa Lab, this supports a non-negotiable rule: AI-assisted research and
founder conviction must be checked by qualified domain experts.

## Fit With Thalassemia Research

This anchor strengthens four rules:

- ask hematologists and transfusion specialists before interpreting patient
  records;
- ask assay-capable scientists before treating a candidate signal as real;
- ask Islamic scholars for tafsir and legal/ethical framing when needed;
- document uncertainty instead of forcing a cure claim.

## Practical Rule

Every patient-facing or clinician-facing claim should state:

- what is known from source-backed evidence;
- what is unknown;
- which expert can review it;
- what data is needed before the claim becomes stronger.

For `case-001`, this means the research package should help a hematologist ask
better questions. It should not replace the hematologist.

## Guardrail

Do not use this verse to claim that an AI researcher can bypass medical
training. Use it to make the project more disciplined about expert review.
