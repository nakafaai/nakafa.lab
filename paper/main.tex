\begin{abstract}
This draft defines the first Nakafa Lab research program: a source-backed,
AI-assisted search for credible and affordable thalassemia treatments. The work
combines clinical literature review, mechanism mapping, computational
hypothesis generation, Indonesia access analysis, and a separate Islamic
research lane. This document is not medical advice.
\end{abstract}

\section{Problem}

Thalassemia remains a major inherited blood disorder. For severe
beta-thalassemia, standard management can include lifelong transfusion and iron
chelation. Curative or disease-modifying therapies exist, but cost,
infrastructure, and eligibility limit access \cite{farmakis2022tif}.

\section{Research Objective}

The objective is to identify credible treatment paths that can reduce disease
burden and become accessible to patients who cannot realistically access
high-cost curative therapies.

\section{Working Definition of Cure}

For severe transfusion-dependent thalassemia, the practical cure-oriented
endpoint is durable transfusion independence with safe hemoglobin, controlled
iron burden, and acceptable long-term toxicity. Existing curative or near-curative
strategies point toward hematopoietic stem cells: replace them through
allogeneic transplantation, modify them through lentiviral gene addition, or
edit them to reactivate fetal hemoglobin \cite{locatelli2024exaCel}.

\section{HbF Reactivation Strategy}

Fetal hemoglobin reactivation is central because it targets the biological
problem directly. In beta-thalassemia, defective beta-globin production can be
partly compensated by gamma-globin and fetal hemoglobin. Clinical gene-editing
evidence validates this principle by disrupting the erythroid enhancer of
\textit{BCL11A}, a fetal hemoglobin silencer, in autologous hematopoietic stem
cells \cite{locatelli2024exaCel,verma2023hbf}.

Nakafa Lab treats this as a proof-of-concept, not as the only acceptable route.
The affordable research question is whether safer and cheaper interventions can
approximate the same biology through fetal hemoglobin induction, globin-chain
balance, erythroid maturation, or improved red-cell survival.

This question is not new biology from zero. HbF induction, HPFH promoter
logic, gene and cell therapy, hydroxyurea-like pharmacology, and
sirolimus-linked autophagy are all active research areas. The unresolved
Nakafa Lab question is the integrated translational version: whether an
affordable candidate can approach an HPFH-like erythroid signature while also
preserving maturation, avoiding mature red-cell hemolysis, improving
globin-chain or alpha-globin-burden endpoints, and remaining feasible in the
Indonesian access context
\cite{locatelli2024exaCel,musallam2013hbfInduction,ravi2022hpfhBaseEditing,zuccato2022sirolimus,zurlo2023sirolimusUlk1}.

The first BCL11A small-molecule boundary is also clear. Erythroid enhancer
editing and patient-cell enhancer disruption support BCL11A repression as a
valid HbF switch, while recent protein-state and degrader work suggests
BCL11A can be attacked experimentally. That does not make blunt systemic
BCL11A inhibition a safe chronic strategy. For Nakafa Lab, BCL11A is currently
a signature axis: candidates should be evaluated for HBG1/HBG2, HbF, BCL11A,
erythroid maturation, viability, and red-cell hemolysis before any
therapeutic language is used
\cite{psatha2018bcl11aEnhancer,zheng2024bcl11aTetramer,shen2022bcl11aDegrader}.

HPFH sharpens the desired target phenotype. Clinical genetics sources describe
hereditary persistence of fetal hemoglobin as an ameliorating factor that can
sustain gamma-globin output in adult life and reduce beta-thalassemia severity.
Recent promoter studies show why: HPFH-like variants in the \textit{HBG1} and
\textit{HBG2} promoters can disrupt repressor binding or create activator
motifs involving KLF1, GATA1, and NF-Y. Genome-editing studies in CD34+ cells
and beta-thalassemia hematopoietic progenitors can reproduce this promoter
logic and raise HbF. For Nakafa Lab, HPFH is therefore a natural positive
control: affordable candidates should move toward an HPFH-like erythroid
signature without damaging erythroid maturation or mature red cells
\cite{ravi2022hpfhBaseEditing,doerfler2021hpfhGataNfy,mingoia2021hpfhBeta0,finotti2023combinedHbfEditing}.

\section{Initial Evidence Map}

The initial evidence map covers clinical guidelines, luspatercept, mitapivat,
gene addition therapy, CRISPR-edited autologous stem-cell therapy, fetal
hemoglobin induction, erythropoiesis, iron overload, and Indonesia-specific
access constraints \cite{cappellini2020luspatercept,taher2025energize,locatelli2024exaCel,fda2025aqvesme}.

Current gene therapy establishes the strongest proof of cure-oriented biology
but also defines the access problem. FDA-listed products include betibeglogene
autotemcel for beta-thalassemia patients requiring regular transfusions and
exagamglogene autotemcel for transfusion-dependent beta-thalassemia in patients
12 years and older \cite{fda2022zynteglo,fda2026casgevy}. NICE described
exagamglogene autotemcel as a possible cure in selected patients when HSCT is
suitable but no matched related donor is available, while noting short follow-up
and uncertain cost-effectiveness \cite{nice2024exacel}. This supports the
Nakafa Lab focus on lower-cost HbF, erythroid, and red-cell-survival
interventions rather than vague supportive claims.

A 2026-04-27 ClinicalTrials.gov curation reinforces this benchmark. Active
registry records include beta-globin addition or restoration products and
HbF-reactivation editing products, but keyword searches also retrieve unrelated
drug or observational records. Registry hits therefore require curation before
they are treated as mechanism evidence \cite{clinicaltrials2026thalassemia}.

A first numeric extraction sharpens the benchmark. Exagamglogene autotemcel
reported transfusion independence in 32 of 35 evaluable patients, while
betibeglogene autotemcel reported transfusion independence in 20 of 22
evaluable patients in a non-\(\beta^0/\beta^0\) genotype phase 3 study
\cite{locatelli2024exaCel,thompson2022beticel}. Luspatercept met its BELIEVE
primary endpoint in 21.4\% of treated patients versus 4.5\% with placebo, and
mitapivat's FDA summary reports transfusion reduction response in 30\% versus
13\% in transfusion-dependent thalassemia and hemoglobin response in 42\%
versus 2\% in non-transfusion-dependent thalassemia
\cite{cappellini2020luspatercept,fda2025aqvesme,taher2025energize}. By
comparison, the lower-cost hydroxyurea TDT meta-analysis reported a longer
transfusion interval and higher hemoglobin, while thalidomide randomized data
showed larger short-term hemoglobin and transfusion-volume effects but with a
much harder safety boundary \cite{tdtHydroxyurea2023,chen2021thalidomide}.
These numbers are benchmark data, not patient-level treatment instructions.

Mitapivat adds a distinct red-cell-metabolism benchmark. FDA approval and
phase 3 evidence support pyruvate kinase activation as an oral
disease-modifying strategy for anemia or transfusion reduction in alpha- or
beta-thalassemia, while mouse-model work links the mechanism to improved red
cell survival, lower oxidative stress, reduced alpha-globin membrane
precipitation, and better iron handling. Nakafa Lab therefore separates this
lane from HbF-reactivation claims: it is a benchmark for erythroid metabolism,
hemolysis, and iron-axis readouts, not proof that any lower-cost antioxidant or
natural product can reproduce the effect
\cite{fda2025aqvesme,taher2025energize,matte2021mitapivatMouse}.

For Indonesia, discovery must be genotype-first. Indonesian studies report
regional beta-globin mutation heterogeneity, including HbE-related genotypes,
and modifier evidence suggests that XmnI and BCL11A variants can affect HbF and
clinical score in HbE/beta-thalassemia patients
\cite{old1998indonesiaBetaThal,rujito2016modifiers}. Therefore, any affordable
HbF-inducing candidate should be evaluated with diagnosis, HBB/HBA genotype, Hb
electrophoresis or HPLC, and HbF modifier context where possible.

The patient-facing context also makes transfusion immunology a required safety
lane. TIF guidance treats alloimmunisation, delayed transfusion reactions, and
autoimmune haemolytic anaemia as serious transfusion complications, and
recommends extended red-cell antigen typing, Rh/Kell-compatible units,
antibody screening, IAT crossmatch, leukodepleted red cells, and careful
records of antibodies and reactions \cite{farmakis2022tif}. Cohort and review
data show wide but clinically important rates of alloantibodies and
autoantibodies in chronically transfused thalassemia, with Rh and Kell
antibodies recurring across studies; DAT-positive patients can remain silent or
progress to autoimmune haemolytic anaemia, especially in higher-risk contexts
\cite{kocyigit2014alloauto,dhawan2014alloauto,khaled2019aiha,alriyami2019emro}.
This means an affordable candidate must be evaluated not only for HbF, but
also for mature red-cell hemolysis, red-cell survival, and compatibility
context.

\section{Discovery Pipeline}

Candidate therapies should pass through a staged evidence funnel before any
patient-facing claim is made: source-backed hypothesis, target or endpoint map,
assay selection, hemolysis and toxicity screening, affordability review, and
licensed clinical review. For HbF-oriented discovery, the first useful endpoints
include gamma-globin messenger RNA, fetal hemoglobin protein, F-cell percentage,
erythroid maturation markers, and red-cell hemolysis safety.

The first assay-ready plan uses endogenous HbF reporter or gamma-globin assays
as the entry gate, followed by erythroid validation and mature red-cell
hemolysis exclusion. This is especially important for natural products and
bee-derived materials, where antioxidant or anti-inflammatory activity is not
enough to show thalassemia relevance \cite{hbfReporter2024,hemolysis2023}.

A later dual-readout prioritization step keeps the first qualified-lab quote
panel small: hydroxyurea as a positive HbF comparator, purified resveratrol as
an HbF seed, sirolimus as an autophagy and alpha-globin cleanup comparator,
standardized 6-shogaol-rich ginger extract as a red-cell support comparator,
and melittin only as a hemolysis hazard boundary. AMPKB1/NRF2 probes,
pyruvate-kinase activation, and T-BDMC-like curcuminoid chemistry remain
expansion lanes unless a lab can support identity, alpha-globin, autophagy,
maturation, viability, and hemolysis gates
\cite{fibach2012resveratrol,zurlo2023sirolimusUlk1,hara2025ampkb1}.

\section{First Candidate Prioritization}

The first affordable comparator is hydroxyurea, because it is a clinically
familiar fetal hemoglobin inducer with human thalassemia evidence and lower
expected access barriers than gene or cell therapy. Its role in this project is
not to serve as a presumed cure, but to define a practical benchmark for any
lower-cost candidate. Candidate bee-derived materials, repurposed drugs, or new
small molecules should not be prioritized above hydroxyurea unless they show a
clearer target link, better safety, stronger thalassemia endpoints, or a better
affordability path \cite{algiraigri2017hydroxyurea,tdtHydroxyurea2023}.

The hydroxyurea lane must also be responder-aware. A HbE/beta-thalassemia study
reported 30 responders among 49 transfusion-dependent patients and a significant
association between XmnI-HBG2 status and transfusion-free interval, while an
ex-vivo beta-thalassemia/HbE erythroid culture study showed variable HbF
induction after hydroxyurea exposure \cite{ghosh2021hbeHydroxyurea,watanapokasin2006hydroxyureaCulture}.
This supports a genotype-first and functional-response-first interpretation
rather than a single generic hydroxyurea score.

Butyrate and short-chain fatty acid derivatives are an older HbF
proof-of-biology lane. Sodium butyrate increased fetal globin expression in erythroid
progenitors from beta-thalassemia patients, and arginine butyrate reached early
human beta-globin disorder trials. The limitation is translational: gamma-globin
or F-reticulocyte increases did not reliably become a durable, practical cure,
so this lane is best used as a benchmark for newer HbF candidates
\cite{perrine1989butyrate,perrine1993arginineButyrate,perrine1995arginineButyrate,musallam2013hbfInduction}.

Sirolimus is the second active repurposing lane. A small Sirthalaclin pilot
trial reported increased gamma-globin messenger RNA in blood and erythroid
precursor cells from selected beta-thalassemia patients, along with signals in
ineffective erythropoiesis markers and transfusion demand. This is hypothesis
strengthening evidence, not patient-level treatment guidance, because the cohort
was small and sirolimus requires immune and drug-level monitoring
\cite{zuccato2022sirolimus}.

The sirolimus lane now has a broader mechanistic bridge. A beta-thalassemia
autophagy review frames excess free alpha-globin as a core toxic burden that
can be reduced through HbF induction and through ULK1-linked autophagy, while a
sirolimus follow-up reported increased ULK1, reduced p62, and lower
alpha-globin in beta-thalassemia erythroid precursors. Separately, a 2025
Science Advances study found that selective AMPK beta1 activation induced HbF
in human erythroid cells and sickle-cell mice through a noncanonical NRF2
pathway involving ULK1 and SQSTM1/p62. Nakafa Lab therefore treats
AMPKB1/ULK1-NRF2/autophagy as a target-discovery bridge for dual HbF and
alpha-globin-cleanup assays, not as a treatment claim
\cite{gambari2024autophagyThal,zurlo2023sirolimusUlk1,hara2025ampkb1}.

A 2026-04-27 gap-matrix pass strengthened this bridge. Broader PubMed searches
retrieved direct beta-thalassemia alpha-globin and autophagy sources. The
sources include AMBRA1 modifier work and a miR-144 and miR-451 study that both
connect autophagy regulators to free alpha-globin handling. This reinforces the
assay rule: measure HbF and F-cells, but also measure alpha-globin burden, ULK1
and autophagy state, maturation, and hemolysis before promoting any candidate
\cite{long2025ambra1Autophagy,keith2023mir144451Ulk1}.

The AMPK/NRF2 expansion lane also has a negative guardrail. Metformin has
HbF-related literature, including a K562 fetal-globin signal, but a retrieved
NTDT study reported no extra benefit when metformin was added to stable
hydroxyurea. This keeps metformin as a low-priority translational warning
comparator and keeps selective AMPKB1 probes as assay-only, not patient
candidates \cite{eltagui2024metformin,eini2025metforminK562}.

AHSP adds a buffer-readout gate to the same alpha-globin cleanup question.
AHSP is an erythroid chaperone for free alpha-globin, and PubMed retrieval
links it to beta-thalassemia modulation, NRF2-mediated response to excess
alpha-globin, and sirolimus-treated beta-thalassemia erythroid precursor cells.
The boundary is equally important: supraphysiologic AHSP overexpression did not
produce major hematologic rescue in a murine beta-thalassemia model. Nakafa Lab
therefore treats AHSP as an optional alpha-globin-buffer readout beside HbF,
alpha-globin burden, ULK1, p62/SQSTM1, maturation, viability, and hemolysis,
not as a standalone cure candidate
\cite{favero2011ahspChaperone,cheYaacob2020ahspModulator,han2022nrf2Ahsp,zurlo2024sirolimusAhsp,nasimuzzaman2010ahspOverexpression}.

Thalidomide class therapy is a high-caution comparator.

Randomized evidence reports hemoglobin and transfusion volume improvements in
TDT, while a smaller adult trial did not find single-agent
hydroxyurea or low-dose thalidomide sufficient to reduce transfusion burden.
Because thalidomide carries severe teratogenic, thrombotic, and neurologic
risks, Nakafa Lab treats this lane as mechanism and responder-signature evidence
rather than an access shortcut
\cite{chen2021thalidomide,bhattacharjee2023thalidomide}.

The next drug-discovery layer is epigenetic HbF reactivation. Human erythroid
work identifies MBD2-NuRD as a major fetal hemoglobin silencing complex, while
LSD1/KDM1A and DNMT1 inhibition provide druggable but safety-constrained paths
to HbF induction. These lanes are not patient recommendations; they are target
definitions for safer screening, where any candidate must raise HbF while
preserving erythroid maturation and avoiding hemolysis
\cite{yu2019mbd2,le2021lsd1Hbe,saunthararajah2011decitabine,wang2025lsd1}.

This target layer also creates a disciplined bridge to natural-product
discovery. NRF2/BACH1 biology and curcuminoid-trienone analog studies suggest
that stress-response and natural-product-adjacent molecules can be tested
against HbF endpoints, but only as assay candidates until identity, potency,
bioavailability, red-cell safety, and clinician review are established
\cite{zhu2017nrf2,chaneiam2013curcuminoid,nuamsee2021curcuminoids}.

This distinction matters. Ordinary curcumin and curcuminoid mixtures belong
mainly in a supportive biology lane for oxidative stress, iron burden, and
liver-related markers. Defined reduced or trienone curcuminoid analogs are the
more cure-relevant hypothesis because they connect directly to fetal hemoglobin
induction. The reduced analog HHBDMC has human primary erythroid precursor HbF
signal, but its chemistry identity remains unresolved. The highest-priority
trienone seed, T-BDMC, now resolves to PubChem CID 10447050 and ChEMBL469419,
but supplier, synthesis, hemolysis, and translation gates remain open. Nakafa
Lab also treats ChEMBL KB/Vero cytotoxicity records for T-BDMC as a dose-window
warning. Nakafa Lab therefore separates curcumin supplementation claims from
curcuminoid analog HbF discovery.

The natural-product HbF lane also includes lower-priority seeds. Resveratrol
has abstract-level evidence for gamma-globin and HbF induction in erythroid
precursors from beta-thalassemia patients \cite{fibach2012resveratrol}.
\textit{Curcuma comosa} labdane diterpenes have K562 gamma-globin reporter
evidence, with isocoronarin D reported as the strongest abstract-level signal.
Quercetin analogs also have abstract-level K562 reporter evidence:
the strongest extracted analog was reported at 2.6-fold gamma-globin reporter
induction at 8 micromolar. These leads remain assay candidates, not treatment
claims
\cite{chokchaisiri2010labdane,pabuprapap2019quercetinAnalogs}.

Furanocoumarins such as angelicin and bergapten add a useful hazard boundary.
They have gamma-globin or HbF-adjacent evidence in K562 and human erythroid
precursor systems, including beta-thalassemia patient-cell context for
angelicin analogs. Follow-on identity work resolves exact
4,6,4'-trimethylangelicin and citropten as sub-lane comparators, but this
chemical neighborhood is tied to psoralen-like
photosensitization, DNA interaction, and UVA-linked cytotoxicity. Nakafa Lab
therefore tracks them as mechanism probes, not therapy leads
\cite{lampronti2003angelicin,lampronti2009angelicinAnalogs,lampronti2009bergamot}.

Quran 6:99 also motivated a disciplined olive and pomegranate check. The
pomegranate lane remains an evidence-gap example. Olive polyphenols are more
biologically interesting for mature red-cell support: hydroxytyrosol and
olive-oil polyphenols have human erythrocyte oxidative-stress, eryptosis, and
hemolysis-protection literature, but current first-pass searches did not find
direct thalassemia, HbF, or gamma-globin evidence. Nakafa Lab therefore tracks
olive polyphenols as red-cell-support comparators, not cure-oriented HbF leads
\cite{quran699,notariale2025hydroxytyrosol,paivaMartins2009olivePolyphenols,fernandes2020olivePolyphenolConjugates}.

Date-palm is a nutrition guardrail rather than an HbF lead. Date-fruit studies
can appear in iron-deficiency anemia or pregnancy hemoglobin contexts, but this
must not be generalized to transfusion-dependent thalassemia, where transfusion
burden and iron overload require monitoring and chelation
\cite{irandegani2019dateIronDeficiency,farmakis2022tif}.

Black seed is a separate prophetic-medicine lane. Sahih al-Bukhari 5688
motivates investigation, but it does not replace disease-specific evidence. A
2024 pediatric beta-thalassemia major trial reported an adjunct iron-overload
signal for `Nigella sativa` plus manuka honey with conventional therapy, while
first-pass searches did not establish a clean black-seed HbF cure mechanism.
Full-text extraction confirms that the main signal is ferritin, serum iron,
transferrin saturation, TIBC, ALT, and AST over ten months, not durable
transfusion independence or correction of beta-globin production. Nakafa Lab
therefore tracks this as an adjunct iron-overload boundary, not a
patient-facing treatment claim
\cite{sunnahBukhari5688,mogharbel2024nigellaThalassemia}. The next gate is
material quality: commercial black-seed oils and capsules can vary widely in
thymoquinone content, so unmeasured retail products cannot be treated as
equivalent to a standardized research material
\cite{khaikin2022nigellaStandardization,alkhatib2020blackSeedTq}.

Quran 76:17 adds ginger to the natural-material map. The strongest current
ginger signal is not HbF induction, but red-cell support: a 2025 PLOS One study
reported that PEF-processed, 6-shogaol-rich ginger extract reduced oxidative
hemolysis, lipid peroxidation, and membrane non-heme iron in red blood cells
from transfusion-dependent beta-thalassemia patients. Focused searches did not
retrieve ginger HbF or gamma-globin evidence, so Nakafa Lab tracks this as a
red-cell-support assay candidate, not a cure-oriented lead
\cite{quran7617,chuljerm2025gingerThalassemia}.

Green-tea extract and EGCG form a stronger clinical adjunct comparator than
most generic antioxidant botanicals because two randomized placebo-controlled
TDT papers have been retrieved. The 2024 erythropoiesis paper reported
supportive signals around hemoglobin, ERFE, hepcidin, hemolysis, and RBC
membrane non-heme iron, while the 2024 platelet paper reported reduced platelet
aggregation and higher protein C/S activity. These signals may help define
red-cell-survival and hypercoagulability assays, but they do not establish HbF
reactivation, transfusion independence, or a cure
\cite{settakorn2024egcgTdt,petiwathayakorn2024egcgPlatelet}.

This lane now has a hard safety gate. LiverTox treats green-tea extract as a
well-established cause of clinically apparent liver injury, while EFSA and USP
reviews distinguish traditional green-tea infusions from concentrated extracts
and emphasize EGCG dose, fasting bolus exposure, product variability, and
idiosyncratic susceptibility. Therefore, EGCG remains an adjunct comparator
only when the material is standardized and a clinician-reviewed liver and
interaction monitoring plan exists
\cite{livertox2020greenTea,efsa2018greenTeaCatechins,oketchRabah2020uspGreenTea,isomura2016greenTeaLiverSafety}.

A separate disease-modifying lane now focuses on iron restriction rather than
HbF induction. In beta-thalassemia, ineffective erythropoiesis can suppress
hepcidin through erythroid signals such as erythroferrone, allowing excessive
ferroportin-mediated iron absorption and recycling. Clinical-stage approaches
therefore include ferroportin inhibition with vamifeport, `TMPRSS6` inhibition
with siRNA or antibody strategies, and hepcidin agonism or minihepcidin
probes. This does not correct `HBB` or prove transfusion independence, but it
is a more direct disease-biology comparator than generic antioxidant claims,
especially for iron-overload and ineffective-erythropoiesis endpoints
\cite{camaschella2020hepcidinEra,kautz2015erythroferrone,manolova2019ferroportinInhibitor,taher2025vamifeportPhase2a,vadolas2021sln124,porter2023sln124,anderson2025regn7999,motta2020minihepcidins}.

K562-only findings must now be held against a stronger endogenous-reporter
benchmark. A 2024 HUDEP2 \textit{HBG1} HiBiT screen of about 5000 annotated
clinical-stage or FDA-approved compounds confirmed 10 HUDEP2 hits, including
avadomide, autophinib, triciribine, and `R574'. The `R574' label is kept as
written in the source, but its cited CDK1/2/4 inhibitor reference resolves to
R547. Because the source study is a sickle-cell-disease HbF screen, Nakafa Lab
uses these compounds as assay and mechanism comparators, not as thalassemia
treatment leads. The published primary CD34+ validation lane selected
pomalidomide, avadomide, and idoxuridine, so avadomide is the strongest novel
benchmark from this screen. Any affordable candidate must survive the same
ladder: endogenous HbF signal, erythroid maturation, disease-cell relevance,
and hemolysis exclusion \cite{yang2024smallMoleculeHbF,chu2006r547}.

\section{Islamic Research Lane}

The Islamic research lane begins with Quran 16:68-69 and expands through
translation, tafsir, sirah, hadith, and scholarly review. This lane can generate
ethical framing and hypotheses, but biological claims must pass scientific
validation \cite{quran1668,quran2680}.

Quran 26:80 is the theology-of-cure anchor: healing is attributed to Allah when
illness occurs. For this research program, that strengthens disciplined search
for means; it does not license untested treatment claims.

Quran 10:57 and 17:82 add a separate guardrail: the Quran is healing and mercy
for hearts and believers. In this project, that means disciplined intention,
truthfulness, and resistance to false certainty; it is not a shortcut around
clinical evidence \cite{quran1057,quran1782}.

Quran 9:14 and 41:44 strengthen the same guardrail. The `shifa` root includes
healing of hearts and the Quran as guidance and healing, so the Islamic lane
must distinguish spiritual healing from biomedical hypothesis generation
\cite{quran914,quran4144}.

Quran 2:155 and the story of Ayyub in 21:83-84 add an illness-and-hardship
guardrail. Severe disease should not be described as proof of divine punishment
against a specific patient or family. The research posture is active patience:
dua, mercy, evidence collection, clinician review, and continued search for the
means of cure \cite{quran2155,quran2183}.

Quran 57:25 adds the Al-Hadid iron guardrail. The verse motivates reflection on
iron's strength and benefits, but Nakafa Lab must not convert that reflection
into an iron-supplement claim. In thalassemia, iron is both necessary and
dangerous when overloaded, so the Quranic lane supports careful measurement,
balance, and evidence-led study of hepcidin, ferroportin, `TMPRSS6`, and
chelation biology \cite{quran5725}.

The bee-derived research lane is therefore broad but not careless. Honey,
propolis, royal jelly, pollen, wax, venom-derived molecules, brood, and
exoskeleton-derived materials can be studied as candidate biological materials,
but none should be named as a thalassemia treatment without mechanism, dose,
safety, reproducibility, and clinician review.

Propolis is the first bee-derived material kept in the active map, but only as a
red-cell oxidative-stress lane. Existing studies support erythrocyte membrane
and cytoprotection questions outside beta-thalassemia; they do not establish
HbF induction or transfusion-burden benefit \cite{wozniak2020propolis,moreira2011propolis}.

Royal jelly is weaker as a thalassemia lead. Human randomized-trial reviews
support general oxidative-stress marker effects, but current searches did not
find fetal hemoglobin or thalassemia-specific evidence. It remains an assay
candidate only if material identity, allergy risk, hemolysis, and erythroid
endpoints are handled first \cite{taheri2025royalJelly}.

Melittin and whole bee venom are hazard-first materials. Melittin has direct
red-cell lysis evidence, and bee venom therapy carries adverse-event and
allergy concerns. For a severe anemia program, venom-derived components should
not advance unless hemolysis, allergy, composition, and delivery risks are
controlled before any efficacy claim \cite{tosteson1985melittin,park2015beeVenom}.

No output from this project should be used for diagnosis, dosing, or treatment
