# Finding: Luspatercept Transfusion-Burden Benchmark

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Scope: clinical benchmark extraction, not treatment advice

## Working Conclusion

Luspatercept is a strong disease-modifying benchmark for adult
transfusion-dependent beta-thalassemia because it has randomized clinical
evidence for reducing red-cell transfusion burden. It is not a cure benchmark:
it does not replace the defective `HBB` gene, does not remove conditioning risk
like stem-cell therapy, and should not be framed as a self-directed option.

For Nakafa Lab, this lane answers a practical question:

> If a future affordable candidate cannot cure thalassemia, can it at least
> approach a real transfusion-burden reduction benchmark?

## Evidence Extract

| Evidence anchor | Extraction | Research meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| FDA 2019 approval | Approved for anemia in adult beta-thalassemia patients requiring regular red-cell transfusions. BELIEVE enrolled 336 adults and showed the primary endpoint in 21.4% on luspatercept vs 4.5% on placebo. | Confirms the indication and the minimum benchmark endpoint. |
| 2025 TDT guideline chapter | BELIEVE used adults with TDT who had received 6-20 packed red-cell units in the prior 24 weeks and no transfusion-free period of 35 days or more. | 2025 BELIEVE final-results PubMed snapshot | The updated PubMed snapshot captures the final BELIEVE long-term efficacy and safety paper, PMID `39947215`. | Marks this as an active long-term evidence lane, not only the original 2020 trial. |
| Active ClinicalTrials.gov snapshot | Current active/recruiting registry snapshot returns 9 luspatercept beta-thalassemia records, including real-world, pediatric safety/PK, and long-term safety studies. | Shows the field is still collecting safety, access, pediatric, and real-world response data. |

## Case-001 Doctor Questions

These are questions for a hematologist, not requests to start treatment:

- Is the patient's subtype confirmed as beta-thalassemia or HbE/beta-thalassemia
  with a transfusion-dependent phenotype?
- What is the patient's baseline transfusion burden in units per 12 weeks,
  units per 24 weeks, and `ml/kg/year`?
- Does weekly transfusion reflect very high disease burden, low pre-transfusion
  hemoglobin target, poor red-cell survival, alloimmune/autoimmune hemolysis,
  hypersplenism, or another cause?
- Would the doctor measure response as at least 33% transfusion-burden
  reduction, at least 50% reduction, improved pre-transfusion hemoglobin,
  reduced iron loading, or another endpoint?
- Do autoimmune disease, antibody status, spleen status, thrombotic risk,
  liver/cardiac iron, infection history, or current medicines create safety or
  eligibility concerns?
- Is luspatercept approved, available, importable, reimbursed, or trial-accessible
  in Indonesia for this patient's exact subtype?

## Research Boundary

Promote luspatercept as:

- a clinical benchmark for transfusion-burden reduction;
- a late-stage erythroid maturation mechanism comparator;
- a doctor-discussion question when genotype, phenotype, transfusion burden,
  immune context, and iron burden are documented.

Do not promote it as:

- a cure;
- a substitute for transfusion when immediate anemia correction is needed;
- a reason to change transfusion, chelation, immune medicine, or supplements
  without clinician direction;
- an affordability answer before Indonesia access and cost are verified.
