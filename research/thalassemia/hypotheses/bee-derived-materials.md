# Hypothesis Group: Bee-Derived Materials

Status: exploratory
Evidence label: hypothesis group, not treatment claim

## Claim Boundary

Bee-derived materials may contain bioactive molecules worth studying. No
bee-derived material is currently a Nakafa Lab treatment recommendation for
thalassemia.

## Candidate Tracks

| Track | Candidate | Initial status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Non-venom extract | propolis | red-cell oxidative-stress lane, no HbF evidence yet |
| Non-venom extract | royal jelly | systemic oxidative-stress lane, no HbF evidence yet |
| Non-venom extract | pollen, bee bread | safety and standardization first |
| Venom molecule | melittin, apamin, PLA2 | hazard-first; whole venom not prioritized |
| Biomaterial | chitin/chitosan from exoskeleton or wings | delivery/materials lane |
| Nutrition | larvae, pupae, drone brood | food composition lane |
| Quran anchor | 16:68-69 | tafsir and hypothesis generation |

## First Tests Before Any Promotion

- Product identity and batch standardization.
- Allergy and anaphylaxis risk review.
- Hemolysis screen using human red-cell model.
- Liver safety and chelation-interaction review.
- In vitro erythroid/red-cell endpoint tied to thalassemia biology.
- Clinician and toxicologist review.
