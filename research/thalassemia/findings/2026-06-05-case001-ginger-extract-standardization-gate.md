# Finding: Case-001 Ginger Extract Standardization Gate

Date checked: 2026-06-05
Evidence label: held red-cell support comparator, not cure and not treatment
advice

## Working Conclusion

Standardized 6-shogaol-rich ginger extract remains held out of the first lab
quote. The strongest current evidence is a 2025 PLOS One ex vivo
beta-thalassemia red-cell study, not an HbF, transfusion-independence, or cure
signal.

The useful action is to tighten the material and endpoint gate. Ginger can be
discussed with a lab only as a standardized red-cell support comparator after
the extract process, marker compounds, batch controls, vehicle/matrix controls,
and support-only endpoint wording are explicit.

## Evidence Snapshot

| Question | Current answer | Research meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Which ginger material? | 6-shogaol-rich extract, not ordinary dietary ginger. | Material preparation is part of the hypothesis. |
| Which markers? | 6-shogaol `CID 5281794`, 6-gingerol `CID 442793`. | Marker identity is stable enough to require quantification. |
| Which thalassemia evidence? | PMID `40938906`, PLOS One 2025 ex vivo beta-thalassemia red-cell support. | Useful for mature red-cell oxidative stress endpoints only. |
| Any direct HbF evidence? | June 5 focused PubMed ginger/HbF query returned 0 records. | Do not include HbF or `HBG` claims. |
| Any trial evidence? | June 5 ClinicalTrials.gov ginger/Zingiber thalassemia checks returned no studies. | No clinical or access claim. |
| Quran role? | Quran 76:17 motivates curiosity about ginger. | Not biomedical evidence. |

## Pass/Hold/Reject Rule

| State | Rule |
| --- | --- |
| Pass to quote-candidate | Standardized extract, quantified 6-shogaol and 6-gingerol, batch/lot, vehicle, matrix control, and support-only readouts are all named. |
| Hold | Any material identity, marker quantification, batch, vehicle, matrix, or endpoint wording field is missing. |
| Reject | The proposal uses ordinary ginger, supplement dosing, HbF/cure language, patient-action framing, or skips hemolysis/membrane-damage safety context. |

## Decision

Keep the quote state as:

`exclude_from_first_quote_until_standardization_and_support_endpoint_requirements_pass`

## What This Lets Us Ask A Lab

The lab question should be narrow:

Can the lab quote a standardized 6-shogaol-rich ginger extract only as a mature
red-cell support comparator with hemolysis, oxidative hemolysis, `TBARS`,
red-cell membrane non-heme iron, osmotic fragility/deformability, and
methemoglobin or membrane-damage context if available?

Do not ask whether ginger can raise HbF, reduce transfusions, replace
chelation, or treat the patient.

## Boundary

This finding does not recommend ginger, ginger extract, supplements, dosing,
transfusion changes, chelation changes, trial screening, travel, import, or
treatment selection.
