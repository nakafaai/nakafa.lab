# Hypothesis Card: Sirolimus Evidence Map

Status: active evidence map
Evidence label: not a treatment recommendation

## Claim

Low-dose sirolimus may induce gamma-globin expression in selected
beta-thalassemia patients. The most important question is whether that molecular
signal can become a practical, safe, affordable reduction in transfusion burden.

## Mechanism

- mTOR pathway modulation.
- Reported gamma-globin mRNA increase in the Sirthalaclin pilot trial.
- Possible reduction of free alpha-globin burden.
- Possible ULK1/autophagy and AHSP-linked erythroid effects.
- Broader `AMPKB1` / `ULK1` / `NRF2` bridge now makes dual HbF plus
  alpha-globin-cleanup assays more important than HbF-only screening.

## Extracted Evidence

- Sirthalaclin reported eight treated patients with beta-plus/beta-plus or
  beta-plus/beta-zero genotypes.
- Gamma-globin mRNA increased in blood and erythroid precursor cells.
- Transfusion demand index decreased in seven of eight patients.
- Reported biochemical changes included lower free alpha-globin chains,
  bilirubin, soluble transferrin receptor, and ferritin.
- The registry snapshot identifies NCT03877809 as a completed phase 2 Italy
  study.

## Safety Questions

- Immunosuppression and infection risk.
- Drug-level monitoring.
- Proteinuria and liver/kidney constraints.
- Suitability in patients with autoimmune disease.
- Interaction with chelation and other daily medicines.
- Mouth ulcers, lipid changes, wound-healing concerns, and infection risk.

Reject as a priority if HbF signal does not translate into meaningful clinical
benefit or if immune/infection risk makes it unsuitable for the target patient
population.
