# Case-001 Mitapivat Clinician Review Readiness

Date: 2026-05-17
Status: public-safe clinician-review aid, not treatment advice
Privacy: no private records, screenshots, identifiers, exact dates, hospital
names, clinician names, doctor messages, owner messages, or patient-specific
treatment instructions

## Purpose

Use this note when a clinician asks, "What do you want confirmed?" The goal is
to ask one clear review-readiness question before any mitapivat-specific access,
safety, or trial discussion.

Current label: `mitapivat_clinician_review_readiness_ready`

## Safe Summary

Mitapivat is a serious adult benchmark, not a case recommendation. Adult label
and adult TDT evidence can be discussed as context. Pediatric TDT and NTDT
records remain no-result watchlist evidence. The case lane must stay paused
unless a hematologist says the current packet is complete enough to review.

## What To Ask

Ask this narrow question:

> Given the diagnosis, age and weight band, transfusion burden, iron and organ
> status, liver tests, medication list, and local access constraints, is
> mitapivat clinically worth reviewing at all, or should this lane stay paused?

## What The Doctor Can Answer

Use one label: `review_not_ready_missing_records`,
`not_clinically_relevant_now`, `review_with_hematologist_only`,
`refer_to_trial_or_access_owner_after_clinician_review`,
`safety_review_required_before_any_discussion`, or
`lane_paused_until_pediatric_results_or_recruitment`.

## What To Prepare Before The Visit

- diagnosis and genotype context;
- age and weight band;
- transfusion burden and hemoglobin response context;
- ferritin trend, LIC, cardiac `T2*`, chelation, and organ monitoring;
- liver tests and hepatobiliary context;
- current medication list;
- local access, pharmacy, trial, payer, and affordability constraints as
  owner-category labels only.

## What Not To Ask

- Do not ask for a dose or treatment change.
- Do not ask whether adult approval automatically applies to this case.
- Do not ask a public responder to interpret raw records.
- Do not ask trial, pharmacy, payer, or manufacturer owners to replace the
  treating hematologist.
- Do not publish raw records, doctor messages, screenshots, or private owner
  replies.
