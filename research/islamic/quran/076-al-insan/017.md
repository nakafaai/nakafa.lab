# Quran Anchor: Al-Insan 76:17

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran anchor for ginger hypothesis generation, not pharmacology proof

## Scope

This note documents the Quranic anchor for ginger. It does not claim ginger,
ginger extract, 6-gingerol, or 6-shogaol cures thalassemia.

## Working Reading

Quran.com renders the verse as a drink flavored with ginger. The verse is a
description of reward and provision, not a biomedical instruction.

For Nakafa Lab, the verse justifies disciplined curiosity about ginger as a
natural material. The scientific ranking must still come from source-backed
thalassemia endpoints.

## Research Implication

- Ginger belongs in the Quranic natural-material candidate map.
- The relevant scientific lane is not ordinary dietary ginger; it is defined
  ginger material with measured 6-gingerol and 6-shogaol content.
- A 2025 PLOS One study used 6-shogaol-rich ginger extract on red blood cells
  from transfusion-dependent beta-thalassemia patients and reported
  anti-hemolysis, lower membrane non-heme iron, and lower lipid peroxidation.
- First-pass focused searches did not retrieve ginger fetal-hemoglobin or
  gamma-globin evidence.

## Guardrail

Do not use Quran 76:17 as a treatment claim. Use it as a reason to investigate
ginger carefully, with compound identity, red-cell safety, clinical endpoints,
and physician review.
