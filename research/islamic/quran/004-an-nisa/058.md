# Quran Anchor: An-Nisa 4:58

Date checked: 2026-05-13
Evidence label: Quran research ethics, not biomedical evidence

## Scope

This note documents Quran 4:58 as an owner-responsibility and trust-routing
guardrail for Nakafa Lab. It does not identify a thalassemia treatment,
molecule, trial, supplement, or clinical action.

## Working Reading

The verse commands that trusts be rendered to those to whom they are due, and
that judgment be made with justice. For Nakafa Lab, the safe research-method
lesson is narrow: consequential questions should be routed to the owner with
the right responsibility and competence.

This does not mean every answer from an authority is automatically correct. It
means the project should not bypass the owner who is responsible for a clinical,
pharmacy, regulatory, manufacturer, trial, or financial-access question.

## Fit With Thalassemia Research

This anchor strengthens four rules:

- route clinical relevance questions to qualified clinicians;
- route dispensing and access operations to pharmacy or regulatory owners;
- route public registry questions to official study contacts;
- keep manufacturer information separate from medical care.

## Guardrail

Do not use this verse as biomedical evidence. Use it to prevent unsafe owner
confusion and to keep claims accountable.
