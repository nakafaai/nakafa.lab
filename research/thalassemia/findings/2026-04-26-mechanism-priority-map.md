# Finding: Mechanism Priority Map

Date checked: 2026-04-26
Evidence label: Mechanism synthesis

## Working Conclusion

The first Nakafa Lab hypotheses should focus on mechanisms already connected to
human thalassemia biology. The goal is not novelty first; it is credible impact.

## Priority Mechanisms

1. Globin-chain balance and fetal hemoglobin
   - Rationale: disease severity is shaped by alpha/non-alpha globin imbalance.
   - Evidence anchor: TIF notes HbF-modifying loci such as `BCL11A` and
     `HBS1L-MYB`, and gene-editing therapies validate HbF reactivation as a
     clinical path.
   - Research question: can any affordable intervention safely increase useful
     HbF or reduce globin imbalance?

2. Ineffective erythropoiesis
   - Rationale: beta-thalassemia marrow produces many erythroid precursors that
     fail to mature into durable red cells.
   - Evidence anchor: luspatercept targets late-stage erythroid maturation and
     has regulator approval for transfusion-dependent adult beta-thalassemia.
   - Research question: are there lower-cost ways to improve erythroid
     maturation without unsafe immune or thrombotic effects?

3. Red-cell metabolism and survival
   - Rationale: thalassemic red cells face oxidative stress, membrane damage,
     hemolysis, and reduced survival.
   - Evidence anchor: mitapivat validates pyruvate kinase activation as an oral
     disease-modifying approach, but liver monitoring is a major constraint.
   - Research question: can red-cell energy, oxidative resilience, or membrane
     stability be improved safely and affordably?

4. Iron toxicity and organ protection
   - Rationale: transfusion saves life but adds iron; poor chelation or poor
     monitoring causes irreversible organ damage.
   - Evidence anchor: TIF places ferritin, liver iron, cardiac T2*, chelation,
     and adherence at the center of TDT survival.
   - Research question: can monitoring and adherence be improved before trying
     speculative adjuncts?

## Lower Priority For Now

Natural products, including bee-derived products, stay exploratory until there is
a clear mechanism, dose, toxicity review, and interaction review with
transfusion, chelation, liver disease, and autoimmune disease.
