## Current Findings

FDA approved Aqvesme (mitapivat) for anemia in adults with alpha- or
beta-thalassemia in the United States. The FDA page also summarizes adult
ENERGIZE-T transfusion-reduction response and ENERGIZE hemoglobin-response
results, while the label includes liver-risk, REMS, and drug-interaction
constraints.

This is not a case-specific recommendation. The pediatric registry records
remain separate access-mapping contexts:

- `NCT07506863`: pediatric TDT mitapivat phase 3, not yet recruiting, primary
  endpoint is transfusion reduction response through week 48.
- `NCT07517133`: pediatric NTDT mitapivat phase 3, not yet recruiting, primary
  endpoint is hemoglobin response.
- `NCT04770779`: adult TDT mitapivat phase 3, active not recruiting, primary
  endpoint is transfusion reduction response.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Molecular diagnosis: `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH or delta-beta context, and
  hemoglobin-analysis timing relative to transfusion.
- Age and weight band, because the current mitapivat public contexts separate
  adult and pediatric review lanes.
- Transfusion burden: dates, pre/post hemoglobin, interval pattern, unit volume,
  red-cell fraction when available, body weight, and reaction history.
- Immune and blood-bank records: ABO/Rh, extended phenotype or genotype,
  Rh/Kell matching, antibody screen, alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs, and
  prior reaction context.
- Iron, organ, and safety context: ferritin trend, liver iron concentration,
  cardiac `T2*`, endocrine monitoring, renal/liver labs, chelator identity,
  liver tests, medication list, and local access or referral constraints.
