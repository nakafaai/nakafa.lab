# Finding: Hydroxyurea Local-Access Comparator

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: access comparator plus responder-biology guardrail

## Working Conclusion

Hydroxyurea is now the clearest local-access comparator in the repo: it has
human thalassemia evidence, known HbF-induction biology, and a BPOM public
product-record signal for `HYDROXYUREA MEDAC`.

This does not make hydroxyurea a cure, a universal thalassemia therapy, or a
self-directed option. It means every affordable candidate should be judged
against a real low-cost comparator instead of vague claims about being
"natural," "supportive," or "antioxidant."

## Why This Matters For Indonesia

| Evidence | Interpretation |
| --- | --- |
| BPOM product search found `HYDROXYUREA MEDAC`, registration `DKI1030600201A1`, status `Berlaku`. | A public Indonesian product record exists for a hydroxyurea product. |
| BPOM search did not find Reblozyl/luspatercept, Aqvesme/mitapivat, Zynteglo/betibeglogene, or Casgevy/exagamglogene. | Advanced therapies remain global benchmarks and Indonesia access questions, not assumed local options. |
| Hydroxyurea response predictor map shows heterogeneous response by `TDT`/`NTDT`, HbE/beta-thalassemia, XmnI-HBG2, and functional erythroid response. | Patient-fit claims require genotype and phenotype context. |

## Case-001 Doctor Questions

Ask these only as clinician-review questions:

- Has hydroxyurea ever been considered, tried, rejected, stopped, or continued
  for this exact subtype?
- If it was rejected or stopped, was the reason non-response, cytopenia,
  infection risk, liver/kidney concerns, fertility/pregnancy counseling,
  autoimmune disease, interaction with other medicines, or another reason?
- If the doctor considers it medically relevant, which responder predictors
  would matter: `TDT`/`NTDT`, HbE/beta status, baseline HbF, XmnI-HBG2,
  `BCL11A`, `HBS1L-MYB`, spleen status, or transfusion-burden pattern?
- Does the BPOM product record translate into real hospital availability,
  affordability, and reimbursement for this patient's care setting?

## Boundary

Do not infer that BPOM product presence means thalassemia indication, patient
eligibility, reimbursement, safe use, or expected response. Those require a
hematologist and hospital pharmacy.
