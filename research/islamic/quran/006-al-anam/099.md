# Quran Anchor: Al-Anam 6:99

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran anchor for botanical diversity, not pharmacology proof

## Scope

This note documents the Quranic anchor for rain, plant growth, grain, dates,
grapevines, olives, and pomegranates. It does not identify a thalassemia drug.

## Working Reading

The Quran.com translation points to vegetation, grain, date clusters,
grapevines, olives, and pomegranates, and calls attention to fruit as it yields
and ripens.

For Nakafa Lab, this widens the natural-material search space while keeping the
scientific filter strict.

## Research Implication

- Botanical diversity can inspire candidate discovery.
- Candidate ranking must come from evidence, not from verse-counting.
- A Quranic natural-world anchor can justify looking, but only reproducible
  biomedical evidence can justify promoting.
- Resveratrol is currently tracked because of PubMed HbF evidence and clean
  chemistry identity, not because grapes are mentioned in this verse.

## Guardrail

Do not rank a plant material higher because it appears in a verse. Rank it
higher only when it has better thalassemia-relevant evidence and plausible
safety.
