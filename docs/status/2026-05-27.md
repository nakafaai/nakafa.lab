## Current Findings

- The May 27 finding is a response-capture gate, not a biomedical upgrade.
  The repo can now classify clinician answers before changing the public case
  state.
- TIF 2025 keeps hematopoietic cell transplantation in the curative category
  for selected transfusion-dependent thalassemia patients, but specialist
  review still gates donor route, iron and organ status, pre-transplant workup,
  fertility, conditioning, expert-center context, and follow-up.
- TIF 2025 gene-manipulation guidance and FDA Casgevy/Zynteglo pages remain
  benchmark anchors for gene addition and gene editing. They do not establish
  Indonesia access, affordability, individual eligibility, or medical
  suitability.
- A clinician answer can advance the public state only through allowed labels.
  It cannot become HSCT advice, gene therapy advice, CRISPR advice, trial
  screening, travel guidance, import guidance, dosing, or a cure claim.
- Access blockers remain separate from medical unsuitability. Medical
  unsuitability remains separate from product failure or research failure.

## Current Blockers

Case-001 still remains `advanced_therapy_referral_packet_missing` unless the
seven owner domains are label-ready and a qualified specialist says screening
is reasonable.

Other blockers:

- Raw records, owner replies, doctor messages, identifiers, HLA details, donor
  details, exact private dates, and patient-specific instructions cannot enter
  the public repo.
- The May 27 answer ladder can classify a reply, but it does not prove
  eligibility, access, affordability, center availability, or payer feasibility.
- Registry geography and regulator product pages cannot be converted into
  case-specific therapy choice.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Diagnosis and genotype labels: clinician-confirmed disease category,
  genotype context, hemoglobin fraction context, and molecular-record status.
- Transfusion burden labels: interval, dates, pre-transfusion hemoglobin,
  product type, volume or units, body weight, Hb increment, annual burden, and
  iron input if available.
- Immune and blood-bank labels: matching policy, antibody screen,
  alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs context, reaction history, phenotype or
  genotype context, and crossmatch issues if present.
- Iron, organ-risk, and chelation labels: ferritin trend, liver iron, cardiac
  `T2*`, chelator identity, toxicity monitoring, and organ-risk owner notes.
- Infection, fertility, and transplant-readiness labels: infection and
  vaccination context, fertility-preservation context, conditioning risk,
  follow-up capacity, and specialist review status.
- Donor or HLA labels: private-only donor/HLA status reduced to public-safe
  labels.
- Access, center, and payer labels: center availability, payer constraints,
  affordability blockers, prior referral status, and whether access is blocked
  separately from biology.
