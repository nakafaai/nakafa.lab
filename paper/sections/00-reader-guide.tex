\section*{Reader Guide}

This manuscript is a research evidence map, not the first document to send for
case-specific clinical confirmation. A hematologist who is asked to review
case-001 should start with the concise doctor handoff brief and the private
medical records. This manuscript is only an appendix for clinicians,
collaborators, reviewers, donors, or lab partners who want to inspect the
broader Nakafa Lab research logic.

For a clinician conversation, the confirmation request is narrow:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item confirm the current diagnosis category and molecular-data gap;
  \item confirm whether the case should be described as TDT, NTDT with regular
  transfusion, HbE/beta-thalassemia, or another category;
  \item explain why transfusion is frequent using actual transfusion, blood-bank,
  hemoglobin-response, spleen, immune, and iron records;
  \item identify which missing records should be requested first;
  \item state whether specialist or advanced-therapy screening is ready,
  data-missing, medically unsuitable, access-blocked, or already under review.
\end{enumerate}

Nothing in this manuscript changes transfusion, chelation, immune medicine,
supplements, referral decisions, or trial eligibility.
