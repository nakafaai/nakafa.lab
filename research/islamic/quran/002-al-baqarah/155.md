# Quran Anchor: Al-Baqarah 2:155

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran anchor + tafsir intake

## Scope

This note documents hardship as a Quranic category of test and patience. It is
not a biomedical source and it does not diagnose why a specific patient became
ill.

## Working Reading

The verse mentions that believers may be tested through fear, hunger, loss of
wealth, loss of lives, and loss of fruits, then gives good news to the patient.
For Nakafa Lab, this supports a guardrail: severe illness and family hardship
can be held as a test requiring patience and effort, without declaring that a
specific patient is being punished.

## Tafsir Intake

QuranX and Ibn Kathir connect this verse group to affliction, patience, and
reward. The tafsir material frames trials as part of the believer's life, not as
a license for outsiders to blame a sick person.

## Research Implication

- The lab should not call thalassemia an azab against a child, sibling, or
  family.
- The right posture is patience plus work: collect evidence, seek treatment,
  ask clinicians, and avoid false certainty.
- Emotional suffering in a family is real research context because it shapes
  urgency, ethics, and communication.

## Guardrail

If a Quranic verse is about tests, patience, and hope, do not turn it into
patient blame. The project may study causes scientifically, but it should not
assign hidden divine blame to a specific person.
