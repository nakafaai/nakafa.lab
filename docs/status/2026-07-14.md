# Daily Research Journal: 2026-07-14

Audience: founder / engineer
Status: genome-editing affordability gate tightened, not medical advice

## Question And Decision

**Question:** Does current FDA genome-editing guidance make an ex vivo editing
route credible enough to promote as affordable?

**Decision:** `hold_for_regulatory_safety_and_cost_evidence`. Gene-editing
routes remain curative benchmarks, not affordable-route candidates.

## What Changed

| Evidence | Decision-relevant result |
| --- | --- |
| FDA April 2026 NGS safety draft | Adds product-representative on-target, off-target, genomic-integrity, human-variation, reproducibility, and reporting work to the credible-route test. |
| FDA June 2026 prior-knowledge draft | Allows some platform methods and pipelines to be reused, but generally not product-specific results across different guide RNAs or genomic targets. |
| ClinicalTrials.gov query | No beta-thalassemia results were first posted from July 1 through July 14 under the exact query; no new outcome justified a route-rank change. |
| CS-101 registry records | `NCT06024876` is completed early phase 1 and `NCT07489196` is not-yet-recruiting phase 2; neither has posted results in the 2026-07-14 registry version. |

## Evidence Read

- **Fact:** Both genome-editing documents are nonbinding drafts. The April
  draft's public-comment deadline is 2026-07-14.
- **Fact:** The April draft covers ex vivo products and recommends representative
  human cells, biological replicates, sequencing depth and quality criteria,
  off-target confirmation, relevant human genetic variation, and genomic-
  integrity analysis where indicated.
- **Fact:** The June draft permits reuse of suitable platform knowledge, but
  says guide-RNA, locus, cell, editor, and manufacturing differences can keep
  safety evidence product-specific.
- **Fact:** CS-101 is the named Asia reference for this decision. It uses ex
  vivo base editing of autologous `CD34+` cells at the `HBG` promoter BCL11A-
  binding site to reactivate gamma-globin. Its registry scope is beta-
  thalassemia, including beta-thalassemia major; this is not genotype-specific
  eligibility or Case-001 evidence.
- **Evidence tier:** Official draft regulatory guidance and official registry
  records, with no newly posted clinical outcome.
- **Registry design and population:** `NCT06024876` is a completed, open-label,
  single-arm early phase 1 study with five participants aged 6-35 and beta-
  thalassemia genotype examples including beta+/beta0, betaE/beta0, and
  beta0/beta0. `NCT07489196` is a not-yet-recruiting, open-label, single-arm
  phase 2 study planning 20 participants aged 12-35 with beta-thalassemia major.
  Registered endpoints include adverse events, engraftment, transplant-related
  mortality, transfusion independence, hemoglobin, and HbF.
- **Registry limitation and conflict:** Neither record has posted results. A
  completed phase 1 status and a planned phase 2 design do not establish safety,
  durability, access, or cost, and do not conflict with the zero-results query.
- **Resolved contradiction:** FDA allows reuse at the method and platform layer,
  including suitable analytical procedures, NGS strategies, and bioinformatics
  pipelines. It generally keeps off-target, on-target-outcome, and genomic-
  integrity results product-specific when guide RNAs or edited loci differ.
  The evidence therefore supports neither "every assay is new" nor "platform
  reuse removes the safety burden."
- **Interpretation:** An affordable editing claim needs a regulator-owned and
  manufacturing-owned reuse-versus-product-specific scope plus total delivered
  cost. Product price or reagent price is not enough.
- **Affordability and access impact:** No public source establishes regulator-
  accepted reuse, manufacturing capacity, total delivered cost, or a payer path
  for Indonesia. The existing CS-101 access score remains `0`; the route stays
  a benchmark.
- **Safety risk:** Off-target editing, unintended on-target outcomes, genomic-
  integrity loss, conditioning, collection, manufacturing, and long-term
  follow-up remain material route risks.
- **Confidence:** High for the FDA draft content; low for Indonesia-specific
  cost and feasibility because no accepted plan or quote is public.
- **Open question:** Which safety elements can the closest Asia editing platform
  reuse, and what are the remaining product-specific cost and capacity needs?

## Falsification And Next Action

Replace the hold only when a qualified regulatory and manufacturing owner
documents the regulator-accepted safety plan, reusable versus product-specific
elements, raw-data and software traceability, and total delivered cost, and that
cost meets a recorded program affordability threshold. A final guidance that
materially removes these elements would also trigger reassessment.

Next decisive action: the cure-program owner should use CS-101 as the named
reference and obtain one public-safe reuse-and-cost matrix, jointly validated by
a qualified regulatory owner and manufacturing/analytics owner. It must
separate reusable methods from product-specific results, name required bridging
data, price total delivered capacity, and state the program's affordability
threshold. Do not begin an NGS analysis without real sequence inputs and
essential metadata.
