# Finding: Pomegranate and Ellagic-Acid Evidence Gap

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran 6:99 botanical anchor, not treatment advice

## Working Conclusion

Pomegranate is a valid Quran-inspired botanical search lane because Quran 6:99
mentions pomegranates among natural-world signs. The first-pass biomedical
search did not find a thalassemia or HbF signal strong enough to make
pomegranate, ellagic acid, or punicalagin an active cure-oriented lead.

This is useful negative evidence. It keeps the Islamic lane honest: a Quranic
botanical anchor can motivate search, but it does not promote a compound above
source-backed HbF leads.

## Research Decision

Do not add pomegranate, ellagic acid, or punicalagin to the active HbF seed
queue yet.

Keep the lane as:

- a Quran 6:99 botanical evidence-gap note;
- a future oxidative-stress or nutrition-adjacent review candidate;
- a reminder that Quranic mention and biomedical ranking are separate.

Promotion would require at least one of:

- thalassemia patient-cell data;
- `HBG1/HBG2`, HbF protein, or F-cell endpoint data;
- red-cell hemolysis and erythroid maturation safety data;
- a clinical or translational source specific to thalassemia.
