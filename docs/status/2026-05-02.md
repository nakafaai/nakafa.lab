## Current Blockers

- The doctor handoff brief or compiled doctor handoff PDF still needs to be the
  first clinical-facing artifact; the main paper remains an appendix.
- Any clinician reply must be triaged by domain before it becomes public repo
  content.
- Diagnosis remains blocked until current clinical category and molecular
  context are confirmed, corrected, or marked not confirmable from missing
  records.
- Transfusion burden remains blocked until the private log has date, body
  weight, transfused volume or approved unit conversion, red-cell fraction,
  pre-Hb, and enough rows/window for interpretation.
- Immune and blood-bank interpretation remains blocked until antibody, DAT,
  matching, reaction, spleen, and hemolysis context are packeted.
- Iron, chelation, and organ-risk interpretation remains blocked until ferritin
  trend, LIC MRI, cardiac `T2*`, chelator identity, monitoring, and organ-risk
  data are indexed for clinician review.
- Referral readiness remains blocked until a qualified specialist can screen a
  complete enough packet.

## Strongest Research Lanes

1. **Doctor response triage.** Convert any clinician reply into confirmed,
   corrected, missing-record, not-applicable, or specialist-review labels.
2. **Private record indexing.** Keep raw records and raw messages in ignored
   private storage while extracting only public-safe labels.
3. **Transfusion burden readiness.** Complete the private burden log before
   calculating annual `ml/kg/year`, pure red-cell volume, Hb response, or iron
   input.
4. **Immune plus iron packets.** Build separate clinician-review packets for
   blood-bank/immune context and iron/organ-risk context.
5. **Research-only benchmark gates.** Keep HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR therapy,
   luspatercept, mitapivat, hydroxyurea, sirolimus, hepcidin-axis work, and
   assay candidates as comparator lanes until clinician packet gates close.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

Collect raw records only in ignored private storage. Public repo updates should
stay de-identified and label-level unless the release checklist passes.

- clinician response metadata: role, generalized receipt date, answered domain,
  corrected label or missing-record request, confidence level, and next clinical
  owner;
- current diagnosis label and molecular context: `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH or
  delta-beta context, HBG modifiers, and HPLC/electrophoresis timing;
- transfusion-burden minimums: date, body weight, transfused volume or approved
  unit conversion, red-cell fraction or unit hematocrit, pre-Hb, at least two
  rows, and at least a 28-day window;
- burden explanation fields: post-Hb, product type, interval, reactions,
  matching context, and delayed symptoms;
- antibody screen, named alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs specificity,
  extended matching policy, red-cell phenotype/genotype, crossmatch difficulty,
  spleen context, and hemolysis workup;
- ferritin trend, LIC MRI, cardiac `T2*`, chelator identity, adherence notes,
  side effects, toxicity monitoring, and organ-risk screening;
- prior discussion, referral, rejection, access barrier, or specialist opinion
  for HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR therapy, luspatercept, mitapivat,
  hydroxyurea, or trial screening.

## Practical Rule

Do not treat one doctor message as a global answer. Route it by domain:

- diagnosis;
- clinical dependence;
- transfusion burden;
- immune and blood-bank;
- iron, chelation, and organ risk;
- referral readiness.
