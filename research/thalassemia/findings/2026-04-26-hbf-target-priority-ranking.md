# Finding: HbF Target Priority Ranking

Date checked: 2026-04-26
Evidence label: target prioritization

## Working Conclusion

For cure-oriented low-cost discovery, `BCL11A` remains the top clinically
validated HbF reactivation target, but it is not the most directly druggable
small-molecule target. `ZBTB7A/LRF`, `HBG1/HBG2`, `KLF1`, and `HBS1L-MYB`
define the inherited regulatory layer; `MBD2-NuRD`, `LSD1/KDM1A`, `DNMT1`,
and `NRF2/BACH1` define the first drug-screening layer.

## Priority Ranking

| Rank | Target | Why it matters | Current risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | `BCL11A` | Clinically validated by HbF-reactivating gene editing | hard to drug safely and specifically |
| 2 | `HBG1/HBG2` promoter elements | direct fetal globin expression control | gene-editing proof stronger than drug proof |
| 3 | `ZBTB7A/LRF` | independent fetal globin repressor at promoter elements | broad biology and cancer relevance |
| 4 | `HBS1L-MYB` | human genetic modifier of HbF and severity | complex regulatory region |
| 5 | `KLF1` | erythroid regulator affecting HbF network | broad erythroid effects |
| 6 | `MBD2-NuRD` | strong human erythroid HbF derepression signal | no simple clinical drug path yet |
| 7 | `LSD1/KDM1A` | druggable epigenetic HbF target | hematopoietic toxicity and lineage effects |
| 8 | `DNMT1` / decitabine | human HbF induction proof-of-biology | cytotoxicity, dosing, and long-term safety |
| 9 | `NRF2/BACH1` | stress-response bridge for natural-product screens | broad pathway and viability concerns |
| 10 | HDAC-associated regulation | druggable chromatin pathway | non-specific toxicity |

## Interpretation

The safest discovery strategy is not to completely disable broad transcription
factors. It is to identify:

- specific erythroid regulatory nodes;
- dosing windows that increase HbF without marrow toxicity;
- compounds that induce HbF but preserve erythroid maturation;
- candidates that avoid hemolysis and immune toxicity.

## Research Implication

Bee-derived molecules or natural products should be screened against endpoints,
not beliefs:

- `HBG1/HBG2` reporter increase;
- gamma-globin mRNA;
- HbF protein;
- F-cell percentage;
- erythroid maturation markers;
- red-cell hemolysis safety.
