# Assay Plan: Indonesia Lab Partner Candidate Map

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Status: outreach planning map, not endorsement and not contact permission

## Purpose

This map identifies Indonesian institutions or research settings that already
appear in public thalassemia literature. It does not mean they have agreed to
collaborate, and it does not mean they can run every assay Nakafa Lab needs.

The goal is to separate three partner types:

- clinical thalassemia cohort or registry access;
- molecular/genotyping capability;
- erythroid, HbF, or red-cell functional assay capability.

The ideal partner may require more than one institution.

## Outreach Logic

Start with a short non-patient-specific research ask:

1. We are building an open, source-backed thalassemia research map.
2. We need expert review of assay feasibility, not treatment advice.
3. The first target is an HbF and hemolysis assay ladder.
4. We can share de-identified research context only.
5. We will not ask the lab to approve supplements or unsupervised therapy.

Do not send this outreach without explicit user confirmation.

## Minimum Screening Questions

Before ranking a partner highly, ask:

- Do they work with thalassemia patient cohorts, carrier screening, or molecular
  diagnostics?
- Can they measure Hb fractions or HbF by HPLC, flow cytometry, qPCR, or an
  equivalent assay?
- Can they run mature red-cell hemolysis or oxidative-stress assays?
- Can they work with de-identified samples under ethics approval?
- Can they collaborate with a hematologist and transfusion medicine specialist?
- Can they export raw data and method metadata for open documentation?

## Current Interpretation

The strongest first outreach targets are likely clinical-academic centers, not
generic commercial labs. Nakafa Lab needs clinician trust, patient-context
guardrails, ethics review, and assay capability. A purely chemistry-focused lab
is not enough unless it partners with hematology and red-cell biology expertise.
