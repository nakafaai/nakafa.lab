## Current Findings

The May 20 source check did not loosen the evidence boundary. TIF 2025 remains
the clinical-context baseline; FDA AQVESME remains adult-label and safety
context; pediatric mitapivat records remain watchlist-only.

ClinicalTrials.gov API checks showed `NCT07506863` pediatric TDT and
`NCT07517133` pediatric NTDT are not-yet-recruiting records with no posted
results, last updated 2026-04-08, under version 2026-05-19.

The practical blocker is owner routing. Each of the seven packet domains needs
a primary owner before the May 17 mitapivat clinician-review question is useful:
hematology/genetics, treating hematology, transfusion medicine, iron/organ
monitoring, hepatology, clinical pharmacy, or access-owner routing after
clinician review.

The May 20 map does not answer whether mitapivat fits case-001. It only decides
who should own each missing or label-ready private record question before any
hematologist review-readiness question is asked.

## Current Blockers

- Any missing domain remains `domain_missing_private_record_request`.
- Any raw private material remains
  `domain_release_blocked_public_label_only` in the public repo.
- Pediatric mitapivat remains blocked from efficacy claims because the current
  pediatric records are not yet recruiting and have no posted results.
- Adult-label and safety evidence remains context only. It does not establish
  individual fit, pediatric fit, affordability, local access, interaction
  safety, liver safety, or trial eligibility.
- Access-owner routing should wait until a hematologist says the mitapivat lane
  is worth continuing.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Diagnosis and genotype owner context: subtype wording, hemoglobin fraction
  context, `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH, delta-beta-thalassemia, `HBG`, and
  modifier records when available.
- Age and weight band, because adult-label, adult-trial, pediatric-trial, and
  patient-specific care contexts remain separate.
- Transfusion burden owner context: interval pattern, units or volume, body
  weight, pre/post hemoglobin context, reaction labels, and annual
  `ml/kg/year` if the clinic can calculate it.
- Iron and organ owner context: ferritin trend, liver iron concentration,
  cardiac `T2*`, chelation identity, and organ-monitoring labels.
- Liver safety owner context: liver test trend and hepatobiliary labels for
  clinician review only.
- Medication interaction owner context: current medicine categories and the
  right review owner, not public medication instructions.
- Local access owner context: regulatory, pharmacy, trial, payer, and
  affordability owner categories after hematologist review, not access
  instructions.
