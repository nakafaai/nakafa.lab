# Asia Registry Source Radar

Date checked: 2026-07-06
Status: benchmark-only source radar, not treatment advice

## Direct Answer

The Asia-facing source lane should focus on public registry and literature
disambiguation, not patient access. The current strongest public-source signals
are China CS-101/base-editing records, China gene-modified autologous cell
records, Pakistan thalidomide/hydroxyurea records, and one regional CTX001
record listing Saudi Arabia with Germany, Italy, and the United States.

Current label:

`asia_registry_source_radar_ready_benchmark_only`

## Why It Matters

Public outreach can ask helpers to explain what a registry ID means, what
country/status it has, and which public paper or guideline supports the pathway.
That is useful because the current landscape is moving quickly and includes
Asia records that a Western-only search would miss.

This radar does not imply Case-001 eligibility, access, referral, travel,
import, purchase, dosing, transfusion change, chelation change, lab contact,
private-message routing, or sample routing.

## Follow-Up Questions For Public Helpers

- What public source, PMID, registry ID, or guideline explains this pathway?
- Which country and public status does the registry record list?
- Is the record active, invitation-only, completed, suspended, or not yet
  recruiting?
- What qualified clinical owner would need to interpret the pathway privately?

## Boundary

Quran 49:6, 4:58, and 16:43 remain process anchors for verification,
owner-routing, and expert consultation only. They are not biomedical evidence.
