# Daily Research Journal: 2026-07-24

Audience: founder / researcher
Status: HGI-001 remains an Asian phase 2 registry benchmark only

## Question And Decision

**Question:** Does the newly posted HGI-001 phase 2 record justify promoting
lentiviral gene addition as an affordable Asian cure route?

**Decision:**
`hold_hgi001_at_asia_phase2_registry_benchmark_only_pending_source_linked_outcomes_and_cost`.

The record adds a cure-oriented institutional development but no posted outcome,
complete safety denominator, manufacturing model, or delivered cost. The
gene-cell route ranking does not change.

## Direct Evidence

- **Fact:** `NCT07721480` was first posted on July 23, 2026. It is a
  not-yet-recruiting, single-arm phase 2 study with six estimated participants
  aged 12-45 and a Prince of Wales Hospital, Hong Kong lead sponsor.
- **Fact:** The intervention is autologous `CD34+` cells transduced with the
  lentiviral `LentiHBBT87Q` vector after busulfan myeloablation. The primary
  endpoint requires at least 12 months without red-cell transfusion while
  maintaining weighted average hemoglobin of at least 9 g/dL.
- **Fact:** The record has no posted results and currently lists no study
  location.
- **Evidence tier:** Official prospective registry design and status, not an
  efficacy, safety, durability, affordability, or access result.

## Provenance Gap And Confidence

- **Registry statement:** The new summary says five people in an earlier China
  study achieved transfusion independence, four for more than two years, with
  no severe adverse events. It does not link a complete report for those five
  people.
- **Primary report:** The closest public primary evidence is a
  non-peer-reviewed preprint linked to `NCT05745532`. It reports only two
  non-beta-zero/beta-zero participants, with 24 and 21 months of transfusion
  independence at its cutoff. It reports no serious product-related adverse
  event, clonal dominance, or replication-competent lentivirus during that
  limited follow-up and says most adverse events were related to myeloablative
  conditioning.
- **Resolved boundary:** The registry's five-person statement may include later
  participants, but it cannot be merged with the two-person preprint into a
  verified complete denominator. None of the four reviewed HGI-001
  ClinicalTrials.gov records has posted results. An exact PubMed query for the
  product and four trial IDs returned zero records on July 24.
- **Confidence:** High that this is a new phase 2 institutional development;
  low that it changes efficacy, durability, safety, access, or affordability.

## Mechanism, Safety, Affordability, And Access

- **Mechanism:** HGI-001 adds an erythroid-expressed beta-globin transgene to
  autologous hematopoietic stem and progenitor cells ex vivo. This can restore
  beta-like globin production without donor GVHD.
- **Safety:** Mobilization, apheresis, busulfan myeloablation, cytopenias,
  infection, organ toxicity, manufacturing failure, insertional risk, clonal
  dominance, and long-term surveillance remain material.
- **Affordability:** No reviewed source itemizes product, manufacturing,
  conditioning, inpatient, or surveillance cost. The route cannot displace the
  matched-sibling HSCT benchmark on current affordability evidence.
- **Access:** Hong Kong sponsor affiliation does not prove an operational study
  site, recruitment, Indonesia availability, authorization, payer coverage, or
  manufacturing transfer.

## Hypothesis, Falsification, And Next Action

- **Hypothesis:** A complete six-person phase 2 cohort could strengthen the
  Asian lentiviral gene-addition benchmark if it reproduces durable
  transfusion independence with complete conditioning, adverse-event,
  integration-site, and manufacturing data.
- **Open question:** What are the source-linked outcomes for all five earlier
  participants, and what complete delivered-cost and manufacturing model will
  be used for the phase 2 cohort?
- **Falsification criterion:** Deprioritize HGI-001 if a complete treated
  denominator shows non-durable transfusion independence, unacceptable
  conditioning or product toxicity, clonal dominance, replication-competent
  lentivirus, manufacturing failure, or delivered cost outside the program
  threshold.
- **Next decisive action:** Capture the first actual enrollment, posted result,
  or peer-reviewed full-cohort report. Reconcile treated, evaluable, and
  transfusion-independent denominators before any health-economic comparison.
