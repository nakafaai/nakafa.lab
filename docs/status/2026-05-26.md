## Current Findings

- TIF 2025 keeps hematopoietic cell transplantation in the curative option
  category for selected transfusion-dependent thalassemia patients, but the
  decision depends on specialist review, donor route, iron and organ workup,
  conditioning, fertility, follow-up, and expert-center context.
- TIF 2025 gene-manipulation guidance and FDA Casgevy/Zynteglo pages confirm
  that gene addition and gene editing are real curative benchmark categories.
  They do not establish Indonesia access, affordability, individual
  eligibility, or medical suitability.
- The May 25 ClinicalTrials.gov refresh remains benchmark context only: China
  had the strongest public curative gene-cell registry signal in that pass, and
  Indonesia had zero active thalassemia records in the country-specific pass.
- For case-001, the strongest current conclusion is procedural:
  `advanced_therapy_referral_packet_missing` remains the blocker until the
  seven owner domains are label-ready.

## Current Blockers

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Diagnosis and genotype labels: clinician-confirmed disease category,
  genotype context, hemoglobin fraction context, and whether molecular records
  are present or missing.
- Transfusion burden labels: interval, dates, pre-transfusion hemoglobin,
  product type, volume or units, body weight, Hb increment, annual burden, and
  iron input if available.
- Immune and blood-bank labels: matching policy, antibody screen,
  alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs context, reaction history, phenotype or
  genotype context, and crossmatch issues if present.
- Iron, organ-risk, and chelation labels: ferritin trend, liver iron, cardiac
  `T2*`, chelator identity, toxicity monitoring, and organ-risk owner notes.
- Infection, fertility, and transplant-readiness labels: infection and
  vaccination context, fertility-preservation context, conditioning risk,
  follow-up capacity, and specialist review status.
- Donor or HLA labels: private-only donor/HLA status reduced to public-safe
  labels.
- Access, center, and payer labels: center availability, payer constraints,
  affordability blockers, prior referral status, and whether access is blocked
  separately from biology.
