## Current Findings

The May 17 refresh supports a narrow conclusion: adult mitapivat evidence can
remain a benchmark, but case-001 relevance still depends on clinician review.
The pediatric TDT and NTDT mitapivat records remain not yet recruiting with no
posted results, so pediatric evidence stays watchlist-only.

The May 17 readiness map does not add a patient-specific therapy claim. It
turns the mitapivat lane into one review-readiness question:

> Given the diagnosis, age and weight band, transfusion burden, iron and organ
> status, liver tests, medication list, and local access constraints, is
> mitapivat clinically worth reviewing at all, or should this lane stay paused?

This is a routing question for a qualified hematologist. It is not a request to
start, stop, dose, import, or seek trial eligibility for mitapivat.

## Current Blockers

- The minimum hematologist packet is still the operational blocker: diagnosis
  and genotype context, age and weight band, transfusion burden, iron and organ
  context, liver tests, medication list, and local access constraints must be
  available before the mitapivat question is useful.
- Pediatric mitapivat remains blocked from efficacy claims because the current
  pediatric TDT and NTDT records are not yet recruiting and have no posted
  results.
- Adult AQVESME label evidence and adult TDT results remain benchmark evidence
  only. They do not establish individual response, pediatric relevance, local
  access, or affordability.
- Public notes must not include raw records, screenshots, identifiers, exact
  private dates, hospital names, clinician names, doctor messages, owner
  replies, adverse-event narratives, or patient-specific instructions.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Diagnosis and genotype: current subtype wording, `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH,
  delta-beta-thalassemia, `HBG`, and modifier context when available.
- Age and weight band, because adult-label, adult-trial, pediatric-trial, and
  patient-specific care contexts remain separate.
- Transfusion burden: dates, pre/post hemoglobin, interval pattern, unit
  volume, red-cell fraction when available, body weight, reaction history, and
  annual `ml/kg/year` if the clinic can calculate it.
- Immune and blood-bank context: ABO/Rh/Kell, extended phenotype or genotype,
  antibody screen, alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs, crossmatch difficulty,
  matching policy, and prior reaction context.
- Iron, organ, and safety context: ferritin trend, liver iron concentration,
  cardiac `T2*`, chelation identity and monitoring, endocrine and organ
  screening, liver tests, medication list, and local access constraints.
