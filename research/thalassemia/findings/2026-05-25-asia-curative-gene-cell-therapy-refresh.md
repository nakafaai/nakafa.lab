## What Changed In The Current Pass

The May 25 ClinicalTrials.gov refresh keeps China as the densest public
registry geography for beta-thalassemia gene-cell and editing benchmarks. The
current pass found active or not-yet-recruiting China signals for CS-101,
YOLT-204, LentiRed, KL003, VGB-Ex01, vebeglogene autotemcel, and other
autologous HSC-style programs.

The same country pass found zero active, not-yet-recruiting, or
active-not-recruiting thalassemia records for Indonesia.

That means the public repo can say:

- China is a high-yield benchmark geography for curative registry monitoring.
- Indonesia trial-geo evidence is absent in this pass.
- Case-001 remains `advanced_therapy_referral_packet_missing`.

It cannot say:

- a person is eligible for any trial;
- travel, import, referral, or access should happen;
- China records imply Indonesia access.

## Case-001 Consequence

The next manual owner question is not "which trial should we join?" It is:

> Which records must be complete before a hematologist or referral center can
> say whether advanced-therapy screening is reasonable?

The required packet remains diagnosis/genotype, transfusion burden, immune and
blood-bank context, iron and organ-risk context, infection/fertility/donor
context, prior referral/access history, and local center or payer constraints.
