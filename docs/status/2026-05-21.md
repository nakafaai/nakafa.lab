## Current Findings

The May 21 source check did not loosen the evidence boundary. TIF 2025 remains
the clinical-context baseline for transfusion, iron, organ, and chelation
questions before disease-modifying drug review. FDA AQVESME remains adult-label,
hepatocellular-injury, REMS, hepatic-impairment, and interaction context.
Pediatric mitapivat remains watchlist-only.

ClinicalTrials.gov API checks showed `NCT07506863` pediatric TDT and
`NCT07517133` pediatric NTDT are not-yet-recruiting records with no posted
results, last updated 2026-04-08, under version 2026-05-20. `NCT04770779`
adult TDT remains active-not-recruiting with posted results, last updated
2026-04-24, under version 2026-05-20.

The practical blocker is conversation order. The first question to a doctor is
not "can we use mitapivat?" The ordered question is: are the core labels
correct, which records are missing, are the seven domains ready, and only then
is the mitapivat lane worth reviewing at all?

## Current Blockers

- Any unclear core diagnosis, transfusion, immune/transfusion, iron/organ, or
  referral-readiness label stops the mitapivat question.
- Any missing seven-domain item remains
  `domain_missing_private_record_request`.
- Any raw private material remains
  `domain_release_blocked_public_label_only` in the public repo.
- Pediatric mitapivat remains blocked from efficacy claims because the current
  pediatric records are not yet recruiting and have no posted results.
- Adult-label, adult safety, and adult TDT evidence remain context only. They do
  not establish individual fit, pediatric fit, affordability, local access,
  interaction safety, liver safety, or trial eligibility.
- Access, import, travel, payer, manufacturer, and trial-owner questions remain
  downstream of hematologist review.

## Strongest Research Lanes

1. General doctor handoff: use the minimum hematologist packet before any
   disease-specific treatment-lane question.
2. Mitapivat conversation sequence: keep scope, label validation,
   missing-record routing, and seven-domain readiness before the mitapivat ask.
3. Mitapivat seven-domain owner routing: assign missing records to the right
   owner category without publishing raw records.
4. Mitapivat clinician-review question: ask only one review-readiness question
   after prerequisites pass.
5. Mitapivat answer ladder: reduce any clinician answer to an allowed public
   label and next research state.
6. Pediatric watchlist: monitor `NCT07506863` and `NCT07517133` for status or
   results without mixing TDT and NTDT.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Diagnosis and genotype owner context: subtype wording, hemoglobin fraction
  context, `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH, delta-beta-thalassemia, `HBG`, and
  modifier records when available.
- Age and weight band, because adult-label, adult-trial, pediatric-trial, and
  patient-specific care contexts remain separate.
- Transfusion burden owner context: interval pattern, units or volume, body
  weight, pre/post hemoglobin context, reaction labels, and annual
  `ml/kg/year` if the clinic can calculate it.
- Iron and organ owner context: ferritin trend, liver iron concentration,
  cardiac `T2*`, chelation identity, and organ-monitoring labels.
- Liver safety owner context: liver test trend and hepatobiliary labels for
  clinician review only.
- Medication interaction owner context: current medicine categories and the
  right review owner, not public medication instructions.
- Local access owner context: regulatory, pharmacy, trial, payer, and
  affordability owner categories after hematologist review, not access
  instructions.
