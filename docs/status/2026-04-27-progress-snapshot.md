# Progress Snapshot: 2026-04-27

Audience: founder / engineer
Status: current working summary, not final paper and not medical advice

## Direct Answer

We have not found a new proven cure or patient-ready medicine.

The current strongest Nakafa Lab research direction is:

`HPFH-like HbF rescue + alpha-globin/autophagy cleanup + preserved erythroid maturation + no mature red-cell hemolysis`.

In plain language, we are trying to make the blood-making system produce
usable red cells again, or compensate for the broken beta-globin pathway well
enough that transfusion dependence can be reduced or removed. A true cure-like
result would require durable transfusion independence or a major transfusion
burden reduction with acceptable safety.

## What Is The Disease Problem?

For beta-thalassemia, the root cause is defective or insufficient beta-globin
production, usually from `HBB` variants. The downstream damage is not only "low
blood." The project tracks five linked failure modes:

- too little functional adult hemoglobin;
- excess unpaired alpha-globin and globin-chain imbalance;
- ineffective erythropoiesis, where red-cell precursors fail before becoming
  useful mature red cells;
- chronic anemia and transfusion dependence;
- transfusion and iron-overload complications, plus immune/transfusion risks.

## What Counts As A Cure Target?

The repo treats a cure-like signal as a measurable phenotype, not a slogan:

- higher endogenous `HBG1` / `HBG2`, HbF protein, and F-cell percentage;
- better alpha/non-alpha globin balance;
- lower free or insoluble alpha-globin burden;
- preserved erythroid maturation;
- preserved viability and acceptable apoptosis;
- no increase in mature red-cell hemolysis;
- reduced transfusion burden or transfusion independence in a clinical setting.

## What Is The "Medicine" Right Now?

There is no Nakafa Lab medicine yet.

Clinically, existing options that a hematologist may evaluate include:

- transfusion and iron chelation as supportive standards;
- hematopoietic stem-cell transplant in selected patients;
- gene/cell therapies such as betibeglogene autotemcel and exagamglogene
  autotemcel in eligible settings;
- luspatercept for adult beta-thalassemia patients requiring regular
  transfusions in approved settings;
- mitapivat for anemia in adults with alpha- or beta-thalassemia in approved
  settings;
- hydroxyurea or other HbF-modifying approaches only where clinically
  appropriate.

None of those should be started, stopped, or changed from this repo. They are
doctor-facing discussion points.

## What Is Our Best Research Candidate Direction?

The leading discovery direction is not a patient drug. It is an assay lane:

`PF-06409577 / PRKAB1 / AMPK beta1 / NRF2 / ULK1`.

Why it matters:

- PF-06409577 is now pinned to exact PubChem and ChEMBL identity snapshots;
- `PRKAB1` is the correct lab-facing human gene symbol for AMPK beta1;
- the retrieved literature bridge suggests selective AMPK beta1 biology can
  connect HbF induction with `NRF2`, `ULK1`, and p62 / `SQSTM1` context;
- beta-thalassemia cure work still needs direct testing for alpha-globin burden,
  erythroid maturation, viability, apoptosis, and mature red-cell hemolysis.

This lane is a qualified-lab quote addendum only. It is not a treatment
recommendation, supplement idea, or self-use candidate.

## What We Are Trying To Prove Next

The next proof is not "does PF-06409577 cure thalassemia?" That would be too
large and unsafe.

The next proof is smaller:

Can a qualified lab test whether a defined `PRKAB1` / AMPK beta1 probe produces
a dual useful signal: increased HbF/F-cells and improved alpha-globin/autophagy
biology without damaging red-cell maturation, viability, or mature red cells?

If no, reject or hold the lane.

If yes, it becomes a deeper preclinical research candidate, still not a patient
medicine.

## Current Blockers

- We do not yet have a wet-lab result from Nakafa Lab.
- We do not yet have enough de-identified case detail to map the patient's
  subtype, genotype, immune/transfusion risk, and existing-therapy eligibility.
- Indonesia access, cost, referral path, and lab capability remain unresolved.
- Natural products, Quranic natural-material hypotheses, and bee-derived
  materials remain motivation or hypothesis sources only unless they pass
  identity, assay, and safety gates.

## Bottom Line

The working "obat" is not a bottle yet.

The working cure hypothesis is: restore useful hemoglobin output by mimicking an
`HPFH-like` fetal-hemoglobin rescue state while also reducing beta-thalassemia
alpha-globin toxicity and preserving red-cell safety.

The most actionable research object today is the lab assay plan, especially the
PF-06409577 / `PRKAB1` quote addendum and the dual HbF plus alpha-globin
autophagy readout gate.
