# Daily Research Journal: 2026-05-05

Audience: founder / engineer
Status: active research summary, not a final conclusion and not medical advice

## TL;DR

Nakafa Lab has not found a new medicine.

Today the highest-value blocker is response capture. Once a doctor or clinical
owner replies, the repo needs a safe way to record the answer as labels without
publishing raw messages or turning clinical instructions into research claims.

Current operational label:

`owner_response_capture_gate_ready`

| Area | What changed | Founder takeaway |
| --- | --- | --- |

## Current Practical Goal

When an owner replies, reduce the response to one of these public-safe outputs:

- `capture_pending`;
- `captured_label_only`;
- `records_requested`;
- `owner_redirected`;
- `owner_under_review`;
- `owner_declined`;
- `follow_up_needed`;
- `private_treatment_instruction_omitted`;
- `release_blocked`.

## Current Blockers

- Raw replies cannot enter the public repo.
- Patient-specific treatment instructions must remain private and
  clinician-owned.
- Diagnosis, transfusion burden, immune/blood-bank risk, iron/organ risk, and
  advanced-therapy relevance remain separate domains.
- A single owner response can close only the domain it actually answers.

## Practical Rule

Do not paste doctor or owner messages into public files. Capture the private
reply in ignored storage, classify it, and publish only a label after release
checks pass.

## Islamic Motivation Boundary

Islamic motivation remains separate from biomedical evidence.

- Quran 16:43 motivates asking qualified experts when knowledge is missing.
- Quran 55:7-9 motivates careful measurement and avoiding distorted claims.

These anchors support disciplined research conduct. They do not prove diagnosis,
drug efficacy, transfusion decisions, chelation decisions, referral decisions,
or trial eligibility.
