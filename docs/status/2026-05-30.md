## TL;DR

Nakafa Lab still has no new cure claim, no patient-specific treatment
recommendation, and no proof of Indonesia access to HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR
therapy, mitapivat, luspatercept, or any trial.

The useful May 30 change is an affordable-cure candidate promotion gate. It
turns the May 29 gap matrix into a hard rule: a candidate needs a named gap,
benchmark, endpoint, safety stop, and falsification test before promotion.

## Current Finding

The source refresh sharpens the discipline:

- exa-cel has favorable sponsor-affiliated cost-effectiveness modeling in the
  US literature;
- public HTA review still highlights uncertainty, cost sensitivity, and
  price-reduction dependency;
- China editing and non-editing registry benchmarks remain close enough that
  weak novelty claims should be blocked.

## Next Evidence Tasks

1. Apply the May 30 gate before promoting any new small-molecule,
   natural-product, gene-cell, or assay lane.
2. Keep case-specific candidate relevance blocked until specialist review.
3. Use failure tests aggressively: identity failure, no endpoint movement,
   hemolysis, toxicity, access failure, or benchmark-only status.
