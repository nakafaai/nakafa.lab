# Finding: Nigella Manuka Trial Full-Text Extraction

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: clinical adjunct signal, not treatment advice

## Working Conclusion

The full text of the 2024 Heliyon trial strengthens the existing boundary for
`Nigella sativa`: this is an adjunct iron-overload lane, not a cure lane.

The study tested black-seed material with manuka honey on top of conventional
care in pediatric beta-thalassemia major. It reported improvements in ferritin,
serum iron, transferrin saturation, total iron-binding capacity, ALT, and AST
after ten months. The paper does not show correction of the beta-globin defect,
durable transfusion independence, or a clean fetal-hemoglobin reactivation
mechanism.

## Trial Extraction

| Item | Extracted detail | Research interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Article | Gamaleldin et al., Heliyon 2024; PMCID `PMC11263651`; PMID `39044986`; DOI `10.1016/j.heliyon.2024.e33707` | open-access full text available |
| Trial design | randomized, double-blind, standard-therapy-controlled, parallel, multisite trial | stronger than anecdote; still needs independent replication |
| Population | pediatric beta-thalassemia major with iron overload, altered liver enzymes, ineffective erythropoiesis, and severe anemia | relevant to the iron-overload problem in severe disease |
| Allocation | 165 allocated; 152 included in final analysis after 13 exclusions | dropout and complete-case analysis need caution |
| Arms | conventional therapy alone; hydroxyurea plus manuka honey; `Nigella sativa` plus manuka honey | separates supportive add-ons from standard care only partially |
| Conventional care | all groups received chelation and transfusion support as needed | add-on evidence, not replacement evidence |
| Primary endpoint | ferritin, measured across the trial | clinically relevant but imperfect; MRI liver iron would be stronger |
| Secondary endpoints | serum iron, transferrin saturation, TIBC, ALT, AST, and thymoquinone exposure | iron-overload and liver-marker lane |

## Outcome Snapshot

The full text reports the following baseline-to-month-10 patterns:

| Endpoint | Control | Hydroxyurea plus manuka | `Nigella sativa` plus manuka |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ferritin | `1690 -> 1195 ng/mL` | `1623 -> 950 ng/mL` | `1700 -> 605 ng/mL` |
| Serum iron | `136 -> 125 ug/dL` | `132 -> 93 ug/dL` | `156 -> 82 ug/dL` |
| Transferrin saturation | `68% -> 56%` | `72% -> 51%` | `64% -> 48%` |
| TIBC | `182 -> 243 ug/dL` | `195 -> 267 ug/dL` | `205 -> 402 ug/dL` |
| ALT | `72 -> 51 U/L` | `64 -> 38 U/L` | `59 -> 32 U/L` |
| AST | `56 -> 48 U/L` | `52 -> 45 U/L` | `53 -> 41 U/L` |

This pattern is worth tracking because the `Nigella sativa` plus manuka arm was
directionally strongest across the iron and liver-marker endpoints. It still
does not establish disease modification at the globin-production level.

## Critical Guardrails

- Do not copy the study protocol into patient use.
- Do not describe black seed, thymoquinone, or manuka honey as a thalassemia
  cure.
- Do not treat this as a substitute for transfusion, chelation, hematology
  monitoring, or physician-directed care.
- The registry snapshot for `NCT04292314` has no posted results in
  ClinicalTrials.gov, so the publication remains the main public results
  source.
- The trial used ferritin and serum iron markers; liver iron MRI, cardiac T2*,
  transfusion burden, HbF response, hemolysis, and adverse events still need
  deeper review.

## Research Decision

Promote this lane from a general prophetic-medicine idea to a real clinical
adjunct hypothesis:

- `Nigella sativa` / thymoquinone: iron chelation and oxidative-stress support;
- manuka honey: potential iron-binding and antioxidant support;
- combination: worth replication and mechanistic separation;
- cure relevance: low until HbF, erythropoiesis, or transfusion-burden effects
  are shown reproducibly.

For Nakafa Lab, this is useful because it gives doctors and researchers a
specific, citable trial to evaluate. It is not enough to recommend any change in
a patient's care.
