# Next Steps

Date updated: 2026-07-13

## Program Outcome

Build a scientifically credible and realistically affordable path to durable
transfusion independence for thalassemia. Research can inform clinician,
laboratory, regulatory, manufacturing, and funding decisions. It cannot select
or change treatment for a patient.

## Current Truth

- The repository has a broad evidence map, candidate hypotheses, assay plans,
  and a de-identified Case-001 workflow.
- [Case-001 remains phenotype-only](status/2026-07-13.md) in the public research
  state. A molecular diagnosis, quantified transfusion burden, complete
  iron/organ-risk record, and qualified clinician review are still missing.
- Nakafa Lab has not produced wet-lab, patient-sample, animal, or clinical-trial
  results.
- No sequencing input or required NGS metadata exists in the repository.
- The [current curative evidence radar](../research/thalassemia/findings/2026-06-22-current-curative-evidence-radar.md)
  supports durable transfusion independence as a biologically reachable
  benchmark. It does not establish Case-001 eligibility, Indonesian access,
  affordability, or long-term outcome.
- Recent work has improved source and owner routing, but another gate, tracker,
  or synthetic notebook is not scientific progress unless it changes a decision.

## Outcome Ladder

Work the highest unblocked outcome below. Each research run should end with a
decision of `promote`, `hold`, `deprioritize`, `falsify`, or `unresolved`.

### 1. Make The Clinical Branch Reviewable

Goal: give a qualified hematologist enough de-identified evidence to determine
which branches merit specialist screening.

Required evidence:

- exact thalassemia diagnosis and genotype, including relevant alpha-globin and
  fetal-hemoglobin modifiers when clinically ordered;
- source-linked transfusion history and annualized burden;
- chelation history, ferritin trend, and liver/cardiac iron assessment;
- antibody, crossmatch, immune, and transfusion-reaction history;
- organ function, infection status, fertility considerations, and prior
  transplant or advanced-therapy review where applicable.

Completion signal: a qualified clinician records which branch is in scope for
further evaluation: allogeneic HSCT, autologous gene-cell therapy,
disease-modifying therapy, standard-care stabilization, or benchmark-only
tracking. This is screening, not a treatment recommendation.

### 2. Verify A Real Curative Access Route

Goal: convert public evidence of a curative route into verified infrastructure,
governance, and total-cost facts.

Priorities:

- verify the route behind the
  [reported Indonesian pediatric gene-therapy case](../research/thalassemia/findings/2026-07-13-indonesia-gene-therapy-owner-verification-gate.md)
  with qualified clinical, regulatory, manufacturing, payer, and follow-up
  owners;
- keep ClinicalTrials.gov and relevant Asian national registries current;
- distinguish local, cross-border, research-only, commercial, and exceptional
  access;
- capture conditioning, collection, manufacturing, release testing, chain of
  identity, follow-up, fertility, travel, and payment requirements;
- model total delivered cost and capacity, not headline product price alone.

Completion signal: a sourced route map states what is available, to whom, under
which authority, at what documented cost range, and which facts remain unknown.
It must not infer Case-001 eligibility.

### 3. Run One Affordable-Cure Experiment

Goal: move one candidate from literature plausibility into reproducible
multi-endpoint in-vitro evidence.

The [first quotation panel](../research/thalassemia/assays/2026-06-02-first-quote-request-table.md)
should remain small. It uses hydroxyurea as a clinical HbF comparator and keeps
the current assay-ready candidates behind material-identity, concentration,
safety, and laboratory-ownership checks. Planned candidates include
resveratrol, sirolimus, and the AMPK probe PF-06409577; none is a treatment
recommendation.

Minimum experiment contract:

- relevant erythroid model plus a primary-cell confirmation plan;
- gamma-globin/HbF and total hemoglobin readouts;
- alpha/non-alpha globin balance or insoluble alpha-globin burden;
- erythroid maturation, viability, hemolysis, and stress/autophagy readouts;
- positive, vehicle, and toxicity controls;
- predefined replicate, dose-response, effect-size, and reproducibility rules;
- explicit kill criteria and a cost-per-test estimate.

Completion signal: real raw data and provenance support `promote`, `hold`,
`deprioritize`, or `falsify`. A planned assay or generated notebook does not.

## Evidence Work While External Inputs Are Pending

- Re-run time-sensitive literature, regulatory, and registry searches with
  reproducible queries, checked dates, inclusion/exclusion notes, and negative
  results.
- Prefer primary literature, registry records, regulator documents, guidelines,
  and official databases.
- Update an existing canonical finding, hypothesis, prioritization table, or
  paper section when evidence changes. Do not create a parallel artifact.
- For every changed claim, record its evidence tier, population or model,
  endpoint, limitations, contradiction status, affordability implication, and
  falsification criterion.
- Use public omics data only when it answers a named candidate or mechanism
  question. Record accession, checksum, reference build, pipeline version, QC,
  and provenance. Human private data require explicit consent and a separate
  privacy-approved workflow.

## Artifact Budget

One research question should normally produce one canonical evidence change.
Do not add a gate, tracker, packet, JSON mirror, and notebook for the same idea.
Status journals summarize material changes; they are not the deliverable.

## Safety Boundary

Nothing in this roadmap recommends diagnosis, dosing, supplements, transfusion
changes, chelation changes, referral, travel, import, purchase, or sample
routing. Patient decisions require licensed clinicians. Islamic sources may
guide ethics and motivation but are not biomedical evidence.
