# Quranic Natural Material Candidate Map

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran hypothesis map, not pharmacology proof

## Working Conclusion

The Quranic research lane should not stop at bees. The Quran repeatedly points
to creation, water, plants, fruits, provision, and reflection. For Nakafa Lab,
these verses can inspire hypothesis generation, but they do not by themselves
prove a thalassemia treatment.

The rule is simple: Quranic mention can motivate investigation; biomedical
claims still need identity, mechanism, dose, safety, reproducibility, and
clinician review.

## Implication For Thalassemia Research

This map creates three lanes:

- bee-derived constituents, already started with melittin, apamin, 10-HDA,
  CAPE, chrysin, pinocembrin, and quercetin;
- plant polyphenol and nutrition-adjacent candidates, especially those with
  thalassemia or red-cell evidence;
- ethical guardrails around provision, moderation, and avoiding false claims.

The next scientific filter is not whether a material is mentioned in the Quran.
The filter is whether it can be identified and tested against thalassemia
endpoints: `HBG1/HBG2`, HbF protein, globin balance, red-cell hemolysis,
erythroid maturation, iron burden, inflammation, and liver/cardiac safety.

## Fit With Current Plant-Derived HbF Leads

The current plant-derived HbF seeds, `T-BDMC`, `resveratrol`, `isocoronarin D`,
`3,4'-Di-O-methylquercetin`, and hazard-first furanocoumarins such as
angelicin and bergapten, did not enter the project because their exact plants
are named in the Quran. They entered because scientific sources connect defined
molecules to gamma-globin or HbF-adjacent endpoints.

Resveratrol is useful as a guardrail example. Grapes are named in Quranic
natural-world verses, and resveratrol is a grape-associated polyphenol, but the
scientific reason for tracking it is PubMed evidence in beta-thalassemia
erythroid precursors plus clean PubChem/ChEMBL identity.

This distinction protects the Islamic lane:

- Quranic natural-world verses can widen curiosity beyond honey and bees;
- specific compound ranking must come from source-backed biomedical evidence;
- a plant-derived molecule is not more truthful because it feels "natural";
- a candidate becomes stronger only when identity, safety, mechanism, and
  reproducibility improve.

## Guardrail

Do not rank a material higher because it appears in a verse. Rank it higher only
when source-backed evidence connects it to a thalassemia-relevant endpoint and
when safety is plausible.
