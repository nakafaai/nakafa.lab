# Finding: Avadomide CRBN-HbF Evidence Boundary

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: mechanism comparator, not treatment advice

## Working Conclusion

`Avadomide` is useful for Nakafa Lab as a mechanistic comparator, not as a
thalassemia treatment lead.

The positive case is narrow but real: Yang 2024 found avadomide in an endogenous
HUDEP2 `HBG1` HbF screen, selected it for primary `CD34+` validation, and linked
it to downregulation of fetal-globin repressors including `BCL11A` and `IKZF1`.

The access and disease-specific case is weak: first-pass PubMed and
ClinicalTrials.gov snapshots did not find avadomide thalassemia evidence or
thalassemia trials.

## Evidence Split

| Question | Current answer |
| --- | --- |
| Does avadomide have HbF screen evidence? | Yes, from Yang 2024 in HUDEP2 and selected primary `CD34+` validation. |
| Is there direct beta-thalassemia evidence? | Not in the first-pass PubMed query for `avadomide thalassemia fetal hemoglobin`. |
| Are there avadomide thalassemia trials? | No studies found in the ClinicalTrials.gov intervention snapshot. |
| Is `CC-122` indexed as fetal-hemoglobin evidence? | No first-pass PubMed result for `CC-122 fetal hemoglobin`. |
| Is the broader class informative? | Yes. Pomalidomide and lenalidomide have human `CD34+` HbF evidence, and pomalidomide has SCD-oriented preclinical/clinical context. |
| Is there a clean pomalidomide thalassemia trial signal? | Not in the current snapshots; the returned pomalidomide registry record is an SCD study. |

## Research Decision

Use avadomide for three things:

1. a `CRBN`/`IKZF`/`BCL11A`-adjacent benchmark in HbF assays;
2. a positive comparator for target-signature discovery;
3. a caution example showing why primary-cell validation does not equal
   thalassemia treatment readiness.

Do not use avadomide as an affordability lead until there is independent
beta-thalassemia or HbE/beta-thalassemia cell evidence, erythroid safety data,
and a realistic access path.
