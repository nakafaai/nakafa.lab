# Research Protocol

This repo should make research auditable. Every claim needs a source, every
hypothesis needs a test, and every medical implication needs clinical review.

## Evidence Labels

- Fact: supported directly by a cited source.
- Guideline: from a recognized clinical guideline or regulator.
- Hypothesis: plausible but not proven for this project.
- Interpretation: religious, ethical, or conceptual reading.
- Open question: important but unresolved.

## Source Intake

For each source, record:

- URL or stable identifier.
- Date checked.
- Research lane.
- Main claim.
- Why it matters.
- Caveats.

Primary sources are preferred: PubMed papers, guidelines, trial registries,
regulatory labels, official databases, and classical Islamic sources.

When a conversation produces a reusable finding, write it down as a dated note
under the relevant `research/**/findings/` folder. Keep each note small enough
that a clinician, scientist, or Islamic scholar can audit it quickly.

## Patient-Specific Notes

Patient context may be recorded only when it is de-identified and useful for
research framing. Do not include names, exact addresses, hospital record
numbers, phone numbers, ID numbers, face photos, or exact birth dates.

Patient-specific notes should distinguish:

- family-reported information;
- medical-record information;
- lab values;
- interpretation by this repo;
- questions for licensed clinicians.

The repo must not recommend changing transfusion, chelation, immune treatment,
or any medicine. It can prepare evidence maps and questions for clinicians.

## Hypothesis Gate

A hypothesis should not be promoted unless it has:

- Mechanism.
- Evidence summary.
- Known risks.
- Validation path.
- Affordability path.
- Falsification criteria.
- Clinician review status.

## Initial Scientific Lanes

- Fetal hemoglobin induction and globin switching.
- Ineffective erythropoiesis and activin/TGF-beta signaling.
- Pyruvate kinase activation and red-cell metabolism.
- Iron overload, chelation, and organ protection.
- Curative options: HSCT, lentiviral gene addition, CRISPR-edited autologous
  stem cells.
- Adjunctive natural-product hypotheses only after safety and mechanism review.

## Islamic Research Lane

Islamic sources can guide reflection, ethics, and hypothesis generation. They do
not replace biological validation. Quranic verses, tafsir, and hadith must be
kept separate from laboratory claims.

The first anchor is Quran 16:68-69 about bees and a drink with healing. This
should be studied carefully through Arabic text, translations, tafsir, and
modern bee-product biology before any claim is made.
