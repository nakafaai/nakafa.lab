# Quran Anchor: Al-Ma'idah 5:2

Date checked: 2026-05-07
Evidence label: Quran research ethics, not biomedical proof

## Scope

This note documents Quran 5:2 as a cooperation guardrail for Nakafa Lab. It does
not identify a thalassemia treatment, molecule, supplement, referral, trial, or
clinical action.

## Working Reading

The verse includes a command to cooperate in righteousness and piety, and not to
cooperate in sin and aggression. For Nakafa Lab, the research-method lesson is
narrow: public collaboration should be directed toward truthful, lawful,
qualified, and privacy-preserving help.

This anchor does not mean every public offer of help is safe or qualified.

## Fit With Thalassemia Research

This anchor strengthens four rules:

- ask qualified people to help only within their expertise;
- reject offers that request raw private records in public channels;
- separate financial, technical, clinical, assay, and Islamic-ethics roles;
- do not let urgency justify false cure claims or unsafe medical advice.

## Practical Rule

Public collaboration requests should ask for bounded help:

- clinical owners can confirm labels or missing records;
- trial or access owners can explain screening pathways;
- assay owners can review feasibility;
- software and privacy contributors can improve tooling and safeguards;
- Islamic scholars can review motivation and ethics, not biomedical efficacy.

## Guardrail

Do not use this verse to pressure people, bypass clinicians, publish private
records, or turn public sympathy into unsourced treatment claims.
