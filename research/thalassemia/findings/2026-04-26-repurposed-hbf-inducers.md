# Finding: Repurposed HbF Inducers

Date checked: 2026-04-26
Evidence label: repurposing evidence map

## Working Conclusion

Repurposed HbF-inducing drugs are the most realistic near-term low-cost research
lane, but none should be treated as a universal cure. The current candidates
split into three different risk profiles: hydroxyurea, thalidomide-class drugs,
and sirolimus.

## Candidate Comparison

| Candidate | Evidence signal | Main risk | Research priority |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hydroxyurea | Meta-analyses in NTDT and TDT; familiar low-cost drug | variable response, myelosuppression, monitoring | high |
| Thalidomide | Randomized and non-randomized TDT studies; active trials | teratogenicity, neuropathy, thrombosis, sedation | high-caution |
| Sirolimus | Pilot trial shows gamma-globin mRNA induction signal | immunosuppression, drug-level monitoring, infection risk | medium-high |
| Pomalidomide/lenalidomide | HbF signal in preclinical/erythroid systems | high-cost, teratogenic/thrombotic/immune risks | medium-low |
| Decitabine/azacitidine | strong epigenetic HbF biology | cytotoxicity and dosing complexity | medium, high-caution |

## Research Interpretation

- Hydroxyurea deserves a genotype- and phenotype-specific evidence map because
  it is comparatively accessible and familiar to hematologists.
- Thalidomide deserves careful review because the signal in TDT appears real,
  but its safety profile is severe enough that it should never be framed as a
  casual low-cost answer.
- Sirolimus is attractive as a repurposing question because low-dose studies
  have explored HbF-related endpoints, but immune risk matters especially for a
  case with a reported autoimmune issue.
- Pomalidomide, lenalidomide, decitabine, and azacitidine are better treated as
  mechanism references unless safety, dose, and access become more favorable.

## What Would Count As Progress

- Identify which genotypes respond best.
- Separate TDT from NTDT evidence.
- Compare endpoints: HbF, total hemoglobin, transfusion interval, transfusion
  volume, ferritin trend, and quality of life.
- Track adverse events and exclusion criteria.
- Determine Indonesia availability and whether clinicians already use any of
  these off-label.
