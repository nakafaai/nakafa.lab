# Case-001 Curative Affordability Gap Matrix

Date checked: 2026-05-29
Status: public-safe research gap map, not treatment advice

## Purpose

Use this after the May 28 advanced-therapy route triage. It explains why
Nakafa Lab still needs an affordable-cure research lane even though curative
benchmarks already exist.

The question is not, "Which therapy should case-001 receive?"

The question is, "Which benchmark gap is blocking realistic cure access, and
which qualified owner can confirm it?"

## Owner Questions

| Gap | Ask this owner question |
| --- | --- |
| Cost | Is the route blocked by direct cost, payer coverage, managed access, or non-medical logistics? |
| Center infrastructure | Is there a qualified thalassemia transplant or gene-cell therapy center in scope? |
| Conditioning burden | Is myeloablation or transplant conditioning a major risk or feasibility blocker? |
| Monitoring | Is long-term follow-up capacity enough for transplant or gene-cell therapy review? |
| Safety and fertility | Are immune, infection, organ, fertility, or endocrine risks blocking specialist review? |
| Local access | Is biology plausible but jurisdiction, product, referral, or payer access still unverified? |
| Assay feasibility | Can a research candidate be tested against HbF, chain balance, hemolysis, viability, and access endpoints before any patient claim? |
