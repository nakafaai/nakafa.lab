# Finding: Casgevy Source Consistency Gate

Date checked: 2026-07-08
Status: source-consistency update, not treatment advice

## Direct Answer

The July 2026 FDA Casgevy age expansion created a source-consistency problem:
some older Nakafa Lab notes still said the FDA TDT benchmark was 12 years and
older. The current U.S. FDA benchmark is now 2 years and older under
`STN 125787`, while NICE's managed-access appraisal remains a 12 years and over
UK source.

This is a source-map correction only. It does not change the public case state:

`branch_review_handoff_packet_incomplete`

## What Was Updated

Current explanatory notes were patched so they do not read like active 12+
claims:

- therapy decision matrix;
- gene-therapy access frontier;
- curative-options eligibility gate.
- advanced-therapy referral readiness gate.

Dated JSON source snapshots and daily journals were not rewritten. They remain
historical records of what the repo captured before the FDA July 2026
expansion. Current interpretation should use the July 7 benchmark note and this
July 8 consistency gate.

## How To Talk About It

Safe public wording:

> Current FDA Casgevy sources list TDT under a 2 years and older benchmark, but
> this is still only a regulatory benchmark. It does not establish Indonesia
> access, affordability, referral readiness, or patient-specific eligibility.

Unsafe wording:

> Case-001 should get Casgevy, travel for Casgevy, enroll somewhere, change
> treatment, or skip the private six-domain packet because the age criterion
> changed.

## Doctor-Facing Implication

If a doctor asks why Casgevy appears in the packet, the answer is:

- it is a current autologous gene-cell benchmark;
- it helps define the branch vocabulary;
- it does not answer fit, timing, access, or referral;
- the first clinical question remains whether the private packet is complete
  enough for an advanced-therapy owner to review.
