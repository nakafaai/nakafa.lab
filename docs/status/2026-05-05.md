## Current Practical Goal

When an owner replies, reduce the response to one of these public-safe outputs:

- `capture_pending`;
- `captured_label_only`;
- `records_requested`;
- `owner_redirected`;
- `owner_under_review`;
- `owner_declined`;
- `follow_up_needed`;
- `private_treatment_instruction_omitted`;
- `release_blocked`.

## Current Blockers

- Raw replies cannot enter the public repo.
- Patient-specific treatment instructions must remain private and
  clinician-owned.
- Diagnosis, transfusion burden, immune/blood-bank risk, iron/organ risk, and
  advanced-therapy relevance remain separate domains.
- A single owner response can close only the domain it actually answers.
- A record request sends the workflow back to the private source index and
  record request matrix before any public label changes.

   one answer type, one public-safe label, and one next action.
2. **Owner-specific outreach.** Continue sending only the relevant owner row,
   not the full paper or a broad treatment question.
3. **Private record indexing.** Keep raw records and raw replies outside Git;
   publish only de-identified labels after release checks pass.
4. **Transfusion-burden readiness.** Complete the minimum private fields before
   calculating annual `ml/kg/year`, red-cell volume, hemoglobin response, or
   iron input.
5. **Immune and blood-bank packet.** Keep antibody, DAT/direct Coombs, matching,
   reaction, spleen, and hemolysis questions routed to the right owner.
6. **Iron and organ-risk packet.** Keep ferritin, LIC, cardiac `T2*`,
   chelator monitoring, and organ screening together before any advanced-therapy
   relevance claim.
7. **Specialist-screening readiness.** Treat HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR therapy,
   luspatercept, mitapivat, hydroxyurea, and trial questions as screening lanes,
   not repository recommendations.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

Collect raw records only in ignored private storage. Public updates should stay
de-identified and label-level unless the release checklist passes.

- owner response capture: source alias, owner route, owner role, generalized
  response timing, domain answered, answer type, missing-record category, next
  owner role, public output label, and release decision;
- diagnosis and molecular context: current clinician diagnosis wording,
  clinical-dependence category, HPLC or electrophoresis timing, `HBB`,
  `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH, delta-beta-thalassemia, HbF-modifier, and family-testing
  context if available;
- transfusion-burden minimums: date, body weight, transfused volume or approved
  unit conversion, red-cell fraction or unit hematocrit, pre-Hb, post-Hb if
  available, at least two rows, and at least a 28-day window;
- immune and blood-bank packet: antibody screen, named alloantibodies,
  DAT/direct Coombs specificity, extended matching policy, red-cell
  phenotype/genotype, crossmatch difficulty, reaction history, spleen context,
  and hemolysis workup;
- iron, chelation, and organ-risk packet: ferritin trend, LIC MRI, cardiac
  `T2*`, chelator identity, monitoring, side effects, and organ-risk screening;
- advanced-therapy history: prior discussion, referral, rejection, access
  barrier, or specialist opinion for HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR therapy,
  luspatercept, mitapivat, hydroxyurea, or trial screening.
