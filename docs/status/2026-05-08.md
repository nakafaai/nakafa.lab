Nakafa Lab has not found a new medicine.

The useful May 8 change is a public share kit. It turns the May 7 collaborator
intake gate into public-safe text for asking help without exposing private
records, requesting public treatment advice, or implying a cure.

Current operational label: `public_share_kit_ready`

| Area | What changed | Founder takeaway |

## Current Practical Goal

Use the public share kit to request bounded help from clinicians, transfusion
medicine, molecular genetics, iron/organ monitoring, advanced-therapy access,
wet-lab assay partners, software/privacy contributors, access mappers, and
Islamic ethics reviewers.

## Current Blockers

- Public posts must not contain raw medical records or identifiers.
- Public replies must not become treatment decisions.
- Role claims still need intake and scope matching.
- Clinical claims still route to private qualified review.
- Trial or advanced-therapy records are screening context only, not eligibility.

## Shareable Finding

The safe public finding is a workflow finding:

> A transfusion-dependent thalassemia case cannot be reviewed as one broad
> question. It has to be split into diagnosis and genetics, transfusion burden,
> immune and blood-bank records, iron and organ monitoring, advanced-therapy
> screening, assay feasibility, privacy, and access. Each lane needs the right
> kind of expert.

## Next Evidence Tasks

1. Share only the approved public text.
2. Classify each public reply by role and scope.
3. Block public raw-record requests and public treatment advice.
4. Move qualified, bounded clinical review into private owner routing.
5. Move non-clinical help into scoped repo, assay, privacy, or access tasks.
