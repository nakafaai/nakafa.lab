## Current Findings

- No treatment claim changed.
- TIF 2025 still keeps thalassemia care split across specialist domains.
- GeneReviews still separates phenotype labels from molecular confirmation and
  genetic counseling.
- ClinicalTrials.gov records remain screening context only, not Case-001
  eligibility or a repo recommendation.
- HHS de-identification guidance and the release checklist still block raw
  records, screenshots, identifiers, and clinician messages from public files.

Operationally, public collaboration is useful only when it reduces to role,
scope, private-introduction decision, and a safe intake label such as
`scope_matched`, `do_not_share_private_records`, or `blocked_medical_advice`.

## Current Blockers

- Public helpers must be routed by role and scope before private contact.
- No raw record, screenshot, exact date, facility name, clinician message, or
  patient-specific instruction can be shared publicly.
- Public role claims are not qualification proof, and public treatment advice
  must be blocked or routed to qualified private review.
- Release-approved Case-001 data is still missing across diagnosis, molecular,
  transfusion, immune/blood-bank, iron/organ, and advanced-therapy domains.

## Strongest Research Lanes

1. **Public collaborator intake.** Convert each public offer into role, scope,
   safe state, and next gate.
2. **Private record indexing.** Keep raw records outside Git and publish only
   release-approved labels.
3. **Owner follow-up discipline.** Route requests for clinical context back to
   the owner ladder.
4. **Case packet readiness.** Keep transfusion burden, immune/blood-bank, iron
   and organ-risk, and advanced-therapy fields grouped by owner.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

Collect raw records only in ignored private storage. Public updates should stay
role-level, state-level, or label-level unless the release checklist passes.

- collaborator intake: alias, channel, timing, role, scope, safe state,
  private-introduction decision, and release decision;
- diagnosis and transfusion: clinician wording, dependence category, hemoglobin
  testing, genotype context, date, weight, volume, red-cell fraction, pre-Hb,
  post-Hb if available, and at least a 28-day window;
- immune, blood-bank, iron, and organ context: antibody, DAT/direct Coombs,
  matching, reactions, spleen, hemolysis, ferritin, LIC MRI, cardiac `T2*`,
  chelation, monitoring, side effects, and organ screening;
- advanced-therapy context: prior discussion, referral, rejection, access
  barrier, or specialist opinion for HSCT, gene therapy, CRISPR therapy,
  luspatercept, mitapivat, hydroxyurea, or trial screening.

## Islamic Motivation Boundary

Islamic motivation remains separate from biomedical evidence.

Quran 5:2 motivates cooperation in good and ethical boundaries, Quran 16:43
motivates asking qualified experts when knowledge is missing, and Quran 55:7-9
motivates careful measurement and avoiding distorted claims. These anchors do
not prove diagnosis, drug efficacy, transfusion decisions, chelation decisions,
referral decisions, or trial eligibility.

## Boundary

This journal does not provide diagnosis, dosing, transfusion changes, chelation
changes, immune-medicine changes, supplement changes, referral decisions, or
trial eligibility. It records a safer public-collaboration workflow.
