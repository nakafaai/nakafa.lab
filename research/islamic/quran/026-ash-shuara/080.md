# Quran Anchor: Ash-Shuara 26:80

Date checked: 2026-04-26
Evidence label: Quran anchor + tafsir intake

## Scope

This note documents the Islamic foundation for seeking cure without making an
unsupported biomedical claim. It does not identify a thalassemia drug.

## Working Reading

Ibrahim attributes healing to Allah when he is ill. For Nakafa Lab, this is a
theology-of-cure anchor: hope is rational inside Islamic belief, but human work
still has to search, test, and verify the means.

## Tafsir Intake

The QuranX tafsir collection records classical readings that connect the verse
to Allah's exclusive power to heal. The Ibn Kathir passage also frames healing
through created means, not through abandoning means.

## Research Implication

- We can search boldly because cure belongs to Allah.
- We cannot claim a cure just because the intention is sincere.
- A proposed remedy must still pass mechanism, safety, dose, reproducibility,
  and clinician review.
- Biomedical uncertainty is not spiritual failure; it is part of discovering
  the means Allah has placed in creation.

## Guardrail

Do not call thalassemia an azab against a patient or family. The Quranic lane can
teach humility, repentance, patience, and effort, but it should not assign blame
to a sick person.
