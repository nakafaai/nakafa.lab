# Finding: Indonesia Access Context

Date checked: 2026-04-26
Evidence label: Policy/access synthesis

## Working Conclusion

In Indonesia, thalassemia is not only a biomedical problem. It is a lifelong
access problem: blood supply, chelation continuity, referral workflow, screening,
and affordability.

## Evidence Notes

- BPJS Kesehatan states that hemophilia and thalassemia are covered under JKN
  when patients follow applicable service procedures.
- BPJS reported 982.17 thousand covered thalassemia visits from 2023 through
  September 2025, with total financing of Rp2.17 trillion.
- Public reporting from a 2025 BPJS/Kemenkes webinar noted that thalassemia
  screening and referral activity is increasing, and that thalassemia remains a
  priority screening condition.
- Indonesian molecular studies show heterogeneity, including HbE/beta-
  thalassemia in East Java samples. This matters because Indonesia-specific
  genotype patterns can change diagnosis, prognosis, and research priority.

## Research Implication

The first affordable-impact path may be:

- better screening and carrier detection;
- fewer missed monitoring steps;
- safer transfusion compatibility;
- more consistent chelation;
- finding which approved therapies can realistically be accessed through
  Indonesia pathways;
- identifying low-cost adjunct hypotheses only after safety review.
