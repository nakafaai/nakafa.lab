# Case-001 Curative Affordability Gap Matrix

Date checked: 2026-05-29
Evidence label: guideline, regulator, health-technology appraisal, workflow, and
privacy synthesis, not medical advice

## Direct Answer

The next blocker is not another therapy claim. The next blocker is defining
which gap an affordable-cure research candidate must beat.

Current operational label:

`case001_curative_affordability_gap_matrix_ready`

Existing curative routes set the benchmark. Allogeneic HSCT is a curative
transplant route. Autologous gene-cell therapy is a curative gene-addition or
gene-editing route. Neither source category proves local access, affordability,
center availability, or case-001 suitability.

## Gap Matrix

| Route | Benchmark role | Gaps that matter for Nakafa Lab |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Allogeneic HSCT | Curative transplant benchmark | HLA or donor route, expert transplant center, conditioning toxicity, GVHD, infection, fertility, organ risk, follow-up, access, payer. |
| Autologous gene-cell therapy | Curative autologous gene-addition or gene-editing benchmark | CD34 collection, manufacturing or editing, myeloablation, product access, expert center, long-term follow-up, high cost, durability uncertainty. |
| Disease-modifying non-curative drugs | Transfusion-reduction benchmark | Subtype and age scope, safety monitoring, durability, local access, payer, and the fact that reduced transfusion is not cure. |
| Standard-care stabilization | Required safety baseline | Iron and organ records, immune and blood-bank records, infection context, chelation monitoring, and quantified transfusion burden. |
| Registry or trial benchmark | Public benchmark context | Registry disambiguation, country and center availability, no inferred screening, no travel or import instruction, and specialist question required. |

## Research Rule

An affordable-cure candidate must say which benchmark gap it targets:

- lower infrastructure burden;
- lower conditioning or myeloablation burden;
- lower monitoring burden;
- safer fertility, immune, infection, or organ-risk profile;
- better local access or payer feasibility;
- simpler assay feasibility before human claims.

If a candidate cannot name the gap and the falsification test, it should stay
below the case-relevance line.

## Evidence Boundary

TIF 2025 HSCT guidance keeps allogeneic HCT as the transplant cure benchmark
and also highlights donor route, pre-transplant workup, myeloablative
conditioning, expert-center practice, follow-up, and cost-effectiveness
context.

TIF 2025 gene-manipulation guidance and FDA Casgevy/Zynteglo pages keep
autologous gene-cell therapy as the gene-addition and gene-editing benchmark.
NICE's exa-cel appraisal keeps durability, complications, pre-infusion
withdrawal, standard-care modeling, and cost-effectiveness uncertainty visible
through managed access.

## Islamic Motivation Boundary

Quran 16:43 supports expert consultation. Quran 49:6 supports verification
before acting. Quran 55:7-9 supports measured claims. These are process
anchors, not biomedical evidence for any therapy.
