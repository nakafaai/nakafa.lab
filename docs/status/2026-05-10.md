## Current Findings

The May 9 ClinicalTrials.gov query returned `total_count=37`, but the first page
mixed direct TDT records with query-adjacent records. A May 10 lookup found
`NCT07506863`, a not-yet-recruiting pediatric mitapivat TDT record, while
`NCT07517133` is pediatric mitapivat NTDT.

This is access-mapping context only, not case eligibility and not a
recommendation.

- No current repo evidence supports a new medicine, cure claim, dosing change,
  transfusion change, chelation change, supplement change, referral decision, or
  trial eligibility decision.
- The strongest May 10 finding is operational: registry records need domain
  classification before owner routing.
- Registry presence is not enough. Each link needs an official ID, study
  domain, status, geography, intervention class, endpoint summary, and safe
  state.
- The saved first-page refresh is useful for access mapping, but it is noisy by
  design because query-adjacent NTDT, iron-axis, transplant, gene-cell therapy,
  and follow-up records can appear beside direct TDT records.

## Current Blockers

- Public links, comments, and follow-ups must not include raw records,
  screenshots, identifiers, exact dates, hospital names, clinician names,
  doctor messages, or patient-specific treatment instructions.
- Trial and center records remain screening context only until qualified owner
  review.
- Mitapivat, gene-cell therapy, transplant, iron-axis, and follow-up records
  must stay in separate domains before interpretation.
- Case-001 still lacks the minimum patient-context data needed for
  patient-specific relevance assessment.

## Strongest Research Lanes

1. Registry link disambiguation: convert each public link into an official ID,
   domain, status, geography, intervention, endpoint, and safe state.
2. Public responder qualification: keep role, scope, evidence cue, and blocked
   states explicit before private follow-up.
3. Minimum hematologist packet: keep diagnosis, genetics, transfusion burden,
   immune/blood-bank, iron, organ, and referral questions in one reviewable
   checklist.
4. Advanced-therapy access mapping: use public registry records to understand
   screening pathways and centers without implying eligibility.
5. Transfusion, immune, iron, and organ record completion: quantify burden and
   separate ferritin trend, MRI iron measures, chelation safety, and organ
   monitoring.

## Patient-Context Data Still Needed

- Molecular diagnosis records: `HBB`, `HBA1/HBA2`, HPFH or delta-beta context,
  and hemoglobin-analysis timing relative to transfusion.
- Transfusion burden records: dates, pre/post hemoglobin, unit volume, red-cell
  fraction when available, body weight, reactions, and interval pattern.
- Immune and blood-bank records: ABO/Rh, extended phenotype or genotype,
  Rh/Kell matching, antibody screen, alloantibodies, DAT/direct Coombs, and
  reaction history.
- Iron and organ records: ferritin trend, liver iron concentration, cardiac
  `T2*`, endocrine monitoring, renal/liver safety labs, and chelator identity
  with monitoring context.
- Advanced-therapy screening context: prior transplant or gene-therapy
  discussions, donor/HLA status if available, organ-function constraints,
  center location constraints, and exact public registry IDs mentioned by
  qualified contacts.

## Next Evidence Tasks

When someone sends a registry or center link:

1. extract the official ID or URL;
2. classify the study domain;
3. record status, country, intervention, and endpoint as access context;
4. route to the right owner only after classification;
5. keep `not_case_eligibility` until qualified review.

Then:

1. fill the registry link disambiguation template for every public trial or
   center link;
2. label each record as `direct_tdt_context`, `query_adjacent_context`,
   `access_mapping_only`, `owner_review_needed`, `not_case_eligibility`, or
   `release_blocked`;
3. request exact registry IDs from anyone claiming trial or center knowledge;
4. keep candidate relevance notes blocked until missing case-context records are
   available and reviewed by qualified clinicians.
