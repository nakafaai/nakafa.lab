## Current Findings

- Public outreach can help find qualified reviewers, public registry IDs,
  source links, assay feasibility input, software help, access mapping, funding,
  and Islamic ethics review.
- Public outreach cannot decide diagnosis, prognosis, eligibility, therapy
  branch, referral, trial screening, travel, import, purchase, dosing,
  transfusion, chelation, lab contact, private-message routing, or sample
  routing.
- Current guideline, PubMed, and registry sources support a routing split:
  HCT, autologous gene-cell therapy, trial pathways, luspatercept, mitapivat,
  and standard-care questions stay clinician-owned or specialist-owned.
- The public case state remains `branch_review_handoff_packet_incomplete`.
  Clinician review of the private six-domain packet is still required before
  any case branch interpretation.

## Islamic Motivation Boundary

Quran 49:6 is a verification ethic. Quran 4:58 is an owner-responsibility
ethic. Quran 16:43 is an expert-consultation ethic. They are not biomedical
evidence and do not authorize diagnosis, treatment selection, eligibility
claims, trial screening, referral, lab contact, sample routing, or private
message routing.
