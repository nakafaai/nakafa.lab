# Finding: HbF Alpha-Globin Autophagy Integration Boundary

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: research-boundary clarification, not treatment advice

## Direct Answer

The phrase:

```text
make red cells sufficiently functional through HbF / HPFH-like rescue plus
reduced alpha-globin burden, then prove it is safe in a lab
```

is not a totally new biological discovery from Nakafa Lab.

The component ideas already exist in the literature:

- HPFH and fetal-hemoglobin persistence can soften beta-hemoglobinopathy
  severity;
- pharmacologic and gene-editing strategies can increase HbF;
- beta-thalassemia damage includes excess unpaired alpha-globin and
  globin-chain imbalance;
- `ULK1`-linked autophagy can clear free alpha-globin in beta-thalassemia
  models;
- sirolimus-treated beta-thalassemia erythroid precursors can show increased
  `ULK1`, reduced p62, and lower alpha-globin;
- selective AMPK beta1 biology has a fetal-hemoglobin / `NRF2` / `ULK1` /
  p62 bridge, but it still needs direct beta-thalassemia validation.

## What Is New For Nakafa Lab

Nakafa Lab's novel work is not claiming to invent HbF, HPFH, or autophagy.

The novel work is the integrated translational question:

Can an affordable, scalable, and monitorable candidate create an `HPFH-like`
HbF/F-cell rescue signal while also reducing alpha-globin toxicity, preserving
erythroid maturation, avoiding mature red-cell hemolysis, and remaining
practical for Indonesia?

That exact package is not solved by the sources retrieved so far.

## Evidence Map

| Component | Existing evidence | Nakafa Lab gap |
| --- | --- | --- |
| HPFH-like HbF rescue | HPFH and HBG promoter biology support fetal hemoglobin as a protective beta-hemoglobinopathy phenotype. | Need a practical, non-editing or accessible route that can safely mimic enough of this phenotype. |
| HbF induction | Reviews and screens show many ways to influence HbF, including drugs, epigenetic targets, and gene editing. | HbF alone is not enough; we need F-cell distribution, maturation, viability, and hemolysis gates. |
| Alpha-globin burden | Beta-thalassemia pathophysiology includes toxic free alpha-globin and globin-chain imbalance. | Candidate promotion must include alpha/non-alpha balance or free/insoluble alpha-globin readouts. |
| Autophagy cleanup | `ULK1` can mediate free alpha-globin clearance in beta-thalassemia models. | Need to test whether a candidate improves this without stress toxicity or maturation arrest. |
| Sirolimus bridge | Sirolimus-linked beta-thalassemia studies connect `ULK1`, p62, and alpha-globin reduction. | Sirolimus remains a monitored comparator, not a patient-facing shortcut. |
| AMPK beta1 / PF-06409577 bridge | Selective AMPK beta1 activation has HbF, `NRF2`, `ULK1`, and p62 context in retrieved literature. | PF-06409577 is assay-probe material only until beta-thalassemia dual-readout safety data exists. |

## Research Decision

Keep the working cure hypothesis:

`HPFH-like HbF rescue + alpha-globin/autophagy cleanup + preserved maturation + no mature red-cell hemolysis`.

Label it as:

`integrated_translation_hypothesis_not_new_component_biology`.

This keeps the project honest: the biology is source-backed, while the
affordable and complete cure-oriented package remains the discovery task.

## What Would Count As A Nakafa Discovery?

A credible Nakafa discovery would need at least:

- defined material identity or intervention identity;
- beta-thalassemia-relevant model, not K562-only;
- increased `HBG1` / `HBG2`, HbF protein, and F-cell percentage;
- improved or non-worsened alpha/non-alpha globin balance;
- reduced free or insoluble alpha-globin, or validated equivalent;
- supportive `ULK1`, p62 / `SQSTM1`, or flux-aware autophagy context;
- preserved erythroid maturation;
- acceptable viability and apoptosis;
- no increase in mature red-cell hemolysis;
- independent replication before any patient-facing claim;
- clinician and ethics review before any clinical interpretation.

## Boundary

Do not tell doctors or the public that Nakafa Lab has found the cure.

The honest statement is:

Nakafa Lab has identified a source-backed, cure-oriented assay hypothesis. The
next work is to reject or validate it through qualified-lab readouts.
