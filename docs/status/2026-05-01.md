# Daily Research Journal: 2026-05-01

Audience: founder / engineer
Status: active research summary, not a final conclusion and not medical advice

## TL;DR

Nakafa Lab has not found a new medicine.

The highest-value blocker today is data readiness for the private transfusion
burden calculation. Regular transfusion dependence is acknowledged, but the
burden remains unquantified until the core fields are available without
guessing.

Current label:

`transfusion_dependent_burden_unquantified`

## What Changed

| Area | What changed | Founder takeaway |

| Label consistency | Updated the legacy burden label to `transfusion_dependent_burden_unquantified`. | The repo now distinguishes reported dependence from unquantified burden. |
| Readiness gate | Added a minimum-field gate before running burden calculations. | Do not interpret weekly transfusion until date, weight, volume, red-cell fraction, pre-Hb, row count, and observation window are sufficient. |

## Minimum Private Log Fields

- `date`
- `body_weight_kg`
- `transfused_volume_ml`
- `red_cell_fraction`
- `pre_hb_g_dl`
- at least two rows
- at least a 28-day observation window

Useful explanation fields:

- `post_hb_g_dl`
- product type
- reactions or delayed symptoms
- matching, antibody, or DAT context

## Boundary

This journal does not provide diagnosis, dosing, transfusion changes,
chelation changes, immune-medicine changes, supplement changes, referral
decisions, or trial eligibility. It only records the next safest workflow.
