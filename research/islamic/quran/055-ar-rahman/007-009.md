# Quran Anchor: Ar-Rahman 55:7-9

Date checked: 2026-04-27
Evidence label: Quran ethics and research method, not pharmacology proof

## Scope

This note documents the `mizan` / balance anchor for Nakafa Lab's research
method. It does not identify a molecule or treatment for thalassemia.

## Working Reading

Quran 55:7-9 connects the raised sky with `mizan`, warns against transgressing
the balance, and commands upright measure.

For Nakafa Lab, the research implication is methodological: measure carefully,
avoid exaggeration, and keep justice in claims. In biomedical work, this means
not promoting a candidate beyond its evidence, not hiding safety risks, and not
turning hope into false certainty.

## Fit With Thalassemia Research

This anchor strengthens three existing rules:

- use quantitative endpoints such as HbF, hemoglobin, transfusion burden,
  ferritin, MRI iron, hemolysis, and viability;
- keep Quranic motivation separate from biomedical proof;
- reject candidates when the measured safety or endpoint balance is poor.

It also supports the scoring model: a candidate with strong mechanism but poor
safety, identity, or access should not be promoted.

## Guardrail

Do not use `mizan` language to make vague claims such as "this balances the
body" unless a measurable biological endpoint is named and tested.
